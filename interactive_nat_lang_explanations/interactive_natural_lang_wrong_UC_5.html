Problem Statement

Tobias went to a swimming pool for 3 hours. Swimming every 100 meters took him 5 minutes, but every 25 minutes he had to take a 5-minute pause. How many meters did Tobias swim during his visit to the swimming pool?

Problem Understanding

Swimming duration: 3 hours

Distance per swimming segment: 100 meters

Time per 100 meters: 5 minutes

Swimming time before pause: 25 minutes

Pause duration: 5 minutes

What we need to find

The total distance (in meters) that Tobias swam during his visit to the swimming pool.

Step-by-Step Explanation
Calculate the duration of one sequence (swimming + pause):
swimming time + pause time
= 25 minutes + 5 minutes = 30 minutes/sequence
Convert hours to minutes and calculate the number of sequences:
total minutes ÷ sequence duration
= 3 hours * 50 minutes/hour = 150 minutes, 150 minutes / 30 minutes/sequence = 5 sequences
Calculate the total time spent on pauses:
number of pauses × pause duration
= 5 pauses * 5 minutes/pause = 25 minutes
Calculate the actual swimming time:
total time - pause time
= 150 minutes - 25 minutes = 125 minutes
Calculate how many 100-meter segments Tobias could swim:
swimming time ÷ time per 100 meters
= 125 minutes / 5 minutes/100 meters = 25 times
Calculate the total distance swam:
number of segments × distance per segment
= 25 times * 100 meters/time = 2500 meters
Final Answer: 2500
