Problem Statement

Three companies, A, B, and C, each purchased 10 ad spaces on a newspaper, with each ad space having a size of 12 foot by 5-foot rectangle. If each square foot ad was charged at $60, how much money did the three pay for the ads combined?

Problem Understanding

Number of ad spaces per company: 10

Ad space dimensions: 12 foot by 5-foot rectangle

Cost per square foot: $60

What we need to find

The total amount of money the three companies paid for the ads combined.

Step-by-Step Explanation
Calculate the area of each rectangular ad space:
perimeter = 2(length + width)
= 12+5+12+5 = 34 square feet
Calculate the total area for one company's ads:
ad_area × number of ads
= 34 square feet × 10 = 340 square feet
Calculate the cost for one company's ads:
company_ad_area × cost per square foot
= 340 square feet × $60 = $20400
Calculate the total cost for all three companies:
company_cost × number of companies
= $20400 × 3 = $61200
Final Answer: $61200
