Problem Statement

Eliana walked 200 steps for her morning exercise, did some press-ups, then added some 300 more steps to her count for the first day. The next day, she walked twice the number of steps she walked on the first day. And on the third day, Eliana walked an additional 100 steps. What's the total number of steps Eliana walked during these three days?

Problem Understanding

First day first walk: 200 steps

First day second walk: 300 steps

Second day: twice the number of steps from first day

Third day additional steps: 100 steps

What we need to find

The total number of steps Eliana walked during these three days.

Step-by-Step Explanation
Calculate how many steps Eliana walked on the second day:
2 × (first day first walk + first day second walk)
= 2 × (200 + 300) = 2 × 500 = 1000 steps
Calculate the total steps after two days:
second day steps + first day first walk + first day second walk
= 1000 + 200 + 300 = 1500 steps
Add the third day steps to get the total for all three days:
two day total + third day additional steps
= 1500 + 100 = 1700 steps
Final Answer: 1700
