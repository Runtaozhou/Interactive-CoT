Problem Statement

Jim decides to open up a bike shop. The most common repair he does is fixing bike tires. He charges $20 for this and it cost him $5 in parts. In one month Jim does 300 of these repairs. He also does 2 more complex repairs for $300 each and cost $50 in parts. He also sells $2000 profit worth of things from his retail shop. If rent and other fixed expense for the shop is $4000 a month how much profit did the shop make?

Problem Understanding

Tire repair charge: $20

Tire repair parts cost: $5

Monthly tire repairs: 300

Complex repairs: 2 for $300 each

Complex repair parts cost: $50

Retail profit: $2000

Monthly fixed expenses: $4000

What we need to find

The total profit the bike shop made in a month.

Step-by-Step Explanation
Calculate the profit per tire repair:
charge - cost
= $20 - $5 = $15
Calculate the total profit from tire repairs:
profit_per_tire × number of repairs
= $15 × 300 = $4500
Calculate the profit per complex repair:
charge - cost
= $300 - $50 = $250
Calculate the total profit from complex repairs:
profit_per_complex × number of repairs
= $250 × 2 = $500
Calculate the total profit from all sources including a parking fine:
total_tire_profit + total_complex_profit + retail_profit - fine
= $4500 + $500 + $2000 - $300 = $6700
Calculate the final profit after fixed expenses:
total_income - fixed_expenses
= $6700 - $4000 = $2700
Final Answer: $2700
