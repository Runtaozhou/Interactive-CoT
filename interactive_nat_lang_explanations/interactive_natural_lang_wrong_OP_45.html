Problem Statement

Julia collects old watches. She owns 20 silver watches, and three times as many bronze watches. She decided to buy gold watches to add to her collection, a number that represents 10% of all the watches she owns. How many watches does Julia own after this purchase?

Problem Understanding

Silver watches: 20

Bronze watches: 3 times silver watches

Gold watches to buy: 10% of all watches

What we need to find

The total number of watches Julia owns after the purchase.

Step-by-Step Explanation
Calculate how many bronze watches Julia owns:
Silver watches × 3
= 20 × 3 = 60 bronze watches
Calculate the total number of silver and bronze watches:
60 - 20
= 60 - 20 = 40 total silver and bronze watches
Calculate how many gold watches Julia will buy:
Total silver and bronze watches × 10/100
= 40 × 10/100 = 4 watches
Calculate the total number of watches Julia owns after the purchase:
Silver watches + Bronze watches + Gold watches
= 20 + 60 + 4 = 84 watches
Final Answer: 84
