Problem Statement

Angelo and Melanie want to plan how many hours over the next week they should study together for their test next week. They have 2 chapters of their textbook to study and 4 worksheets to memorize. They figure out that they should dedicate 3 hours to each chapter of their textbook and 1.5 hours for each worksheet. If they plan to study no more than 4 hours each day, how many days should they plan to study total over the next week if they take a 10-minute break every hour, include 3 10-minute snack breaks each day, and 30 minutes for lunch each day?

Problem Understanding

Textbook chapters: 2

Worksheets: 4

Hours per chapter: 3

Hours per worksheet: 1.5

Maximum study hours per day: 4

Break duration: 10 minutes per hour

Snack breaks: 3 breaks of 10 minutes each day

Lunch break: 30 minutes each day

What we need to find

The total number of days they should plan to study.

Step-by-Step Explanation
Calculate the total hours needed for textbook chapters:
hours per chapter × number of chapters
= 3 hours × 2 chapters = 6 hours total
Calculate the total hours needed for worksheets:
hours per worksheet × number of worksheets
= 1.5 hours × 4 worksheets = 6 hours total
Calculate the total study hours needed:
textbook_hours + worksheet_hours
= 6 hours + 6 hours = 12 hours
12 / 4 = 3 days
Calculate the additional time needed for hourly breaks:
total study hours × break time per hour
= 12 total hours × 10 minutes = 180 extra minutes for breaks
Calculate the time needed for snack breaks:
number of snack breaks × time per snack break
= 3 × 15 minutes = 45 minutes
Calculate the total additional time needed for all breaks and lunch:
hourly break minutes + snack break minutes + lunch minutes
= 180 minutes + 45 minutes + 30 minutes = 255 minutes, or 255 / 60 minutes per hour = 5.1 extra hours
Calculate the total hours needed including breaks:
total study hours + total break hours
= 12 hours + 5.1 hours = 17.1 hours total
Calculate the number of days needed based on maximum hours per day:
total hours with breaks ÷ maximum hours per day
= 17.1 hours / 4 hours each day = 4.275
Round up to find the total days needed:
ceiling(days_needed_decimal)
= They will need to plan to study 5 days to allow for all the time they need
Final Answer: 5
