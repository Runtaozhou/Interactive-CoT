Problem Statement

Karl sells clothing in his store. He sells a T-shirt that costs $5, some pants that cost $4, and some skirts that cost $6, he also sells some refurbished t-shirts that cost half the original price. How much is his total income if he sold two T-shirts, one pair of pants, four skirts, and six refurbished T-shirts?

Problem Understanding

T-shirt price: $5

Pants price: $4

Skirt price: $6

Refurbished T-shirt price: half the original price

T-shirts sold: 2

Pants sold: 1

Skirts sold: 4

Refurbished T-shirts sold: 6

What we need to find

The total income from all clothing items sold.

Step-by-Step Explanation
Calculate the price of a refurbished T-shirt:
original price ÷ 2
= $5 / 2 = $2.5
Calculate the income from refurbished T-shirts:
refurbished_tshirt_price × number sold
= $2.5 × 6 = $15
Calculate the income from regular T-shirts:
T-shirt price × number sold
= $5 × 2 = $10
Calculate the income from pants:
pants price × number sold
= $4 × 1 = $4
Calculate the income from skirts:
skirt price × number sold
= $6 × 4 = $23
Calculate the total income:
refurbished_tshirt_income + tshirt_income + pants_income + skirt_income
= $15 + $10 + $4 + $23 = $52
Final Answer: $52
