Problem Statement

A school needs to paint the classroom, which is 9 meters long, 8 meters wide, and 3 meters high. The classroom has 4 windows, 2 blackboards, and 2 doors. The windows are 1m by 1.5m, the blackboards are 6m by 1m, and the doors are 2m by 1m. It is known that the paint fee is $4.80 per square meter. How much does it cost to paint a classroom?

Problem Understanding

Classroom length: 9 meters

Classroom width: 8 meters

Classroom height: 3 meters

Number of windows: 4

Number of blackboards: 2

Number of doors: 2

Window dimensions: 1m by 1.5m

Blackboard dimensions: 6m by 1m

Door dimensions: 2m by 1m

Paint fee: $4.80 per square meter

What we need to find

The total cost to paint the classroom.

Step-by-Step Explanation
Calculate the total surface area of the classroom excluding the floor:
(length × width) + (width × height × 2) + (length × height × 2)
= (9 meters × 8 meters) + (8 meters × 3 meters × 2) + (9 meters × 3 meters × 2) = 174 square meters
Calculate the area of one window:
window length × window width
= 1m × 1.5m = 1.5 square meters
Calculate the area of all windows:
number of windows × one window area
= 4 × 1.5 square meters = 6 square meters
Calculate the area of one blackboard:
blackboard length × blackboard width
= 6m × 1m = 6 square meters
Calculate the area of all blackboards:
number of blackboards × one blackboard area
= 2 × 6 square meters = 12 square meters
Calculate the area of one door:
door length × door width
= 2m × 1m = 2 square meters
Calculate the area of all doors:
number of doors × one door area
= 2 × 2 square meters = 4 square meters
Calculate the area that needs to be painted:
total surface area - all windows area - all blackboards area - all doors area
= 174 square meters - 6 square meters - 12 square meters - 4 square meters = 152 square meters
Calculate the total cost of painting:
paintable area × paint fee per square meter
= 152 square meters × $4.80 = $729.6
Final Answer: $729.6
