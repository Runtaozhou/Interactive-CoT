Problem Statement

Marge had a winning lottery ticket for $12006 and wanted to spend some of it wisely before using on any fun things. She paid half of the lottery amount for the taxes on her winnings, then put a third of the leftover money towards her student loans. She put $1000 in savings and invested a fifth of that amount in the stock market. How many dollars does she have left for fun?

Problem Understanding

Lottery winnings: $12006

Tax rate: 1/2 of winnings

Student loan payment: 1/3 of after-tax money

Savings amount: $1000

Investment amount: 1/5 of savings

What we need to find

How many dollars Marge has left for fun after all her expenses.

Step-by-Step Explanation
Calculate how much money Marge has left after paying taxes:
lottery_winnings / 2
= 12000 / 2 = $6000
Calculate how much Marge paid for student loans:
after_tax / 3
= 6000 / 3 = $2000
Calculate how much money Marge has left after paying student loans:
after_tax - student_loan_payment
= 6000 - 2000 = $4000
Calculate how much money Marge has left after putting money in savings:
after_loans - savings
= 4000 - 1000 = $3000
Calculate how much Marge invested in the stock market:
savings / 5
= 1000 / 5 = $200
Calculate how much money Marge has left for fun:
after_savings - investment
= 3000 - 200 = $2800
Final Answer: $2800
