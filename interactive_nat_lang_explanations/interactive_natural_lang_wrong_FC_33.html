Problem Statement

Nathan wants to line the inside of a box with velvet. The box has two long sides that measure 8 inches by 6 inches, two short sides that measure 5 inches by six inches and a top and a bottom that each measure 40 square inches. How many square inches of velvet does Nathan need?

Problem Understanding

Two long sides dimensions: 8 inches by 6 inches

Two short sides dimensions: 5 inches by 6 inches

Top and bottom area: 40 square inches each

What we need to find

The total square inches of velvet Nathan needs to line the inside of the box.

Step-by-Step Explanation
First find the perimeter of each long side by adding the lengths of all sides:
(length + width) * 2
= (8 inches + 6 inches) * 2 = 28 square inches
Then do the same thing for each short side:
(length + width) * 2
= (5 inches + 6 inches) * 2 = 22 square inches
Now add the area of one long side, one short side, and one top/bottom together:
long_side_area + short_side_area + top_bottom_area
= 28 square inches + 22 square inches + 40 square inches = 90 square inches
Now double that quantity because there are two of each kind of side:
one_set_area * 2
= 90 square inches * 2 = 180 square inches
Final Answer: 180 square inches
