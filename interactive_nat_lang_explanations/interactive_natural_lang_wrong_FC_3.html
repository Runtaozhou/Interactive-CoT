Problem Statement

One logger can cut down 6 trees per day. The forest is a rectangle measuring 4 miles by 6 miles, and each square mile has 600 trees. If there are 30 days in each month, how many months will it take 8 loggers to cut down all the trees?

Problem Understanding

One logger's capacity: 6 trees per day

Forest dimensions: 4 miles by 6 miles

Tree density: 600 trees per square mile

Month length: 30 days

Number of loggers: 8

What we need to find

The number of months it will take for the loggers to cut down all the trees in the forest.

Step-by-Step Explanation
First find the total area of the forest:
perimeter = 2 × length + 2 × width
= 4 miles + 4 miles + 6 miles + 6 miles = 20 square miles
Then find the total number of trees in that area:
forest_area × tree density
= 600 trees/square mile * 20 square miles = 12000 trees
Then find the number of trees all the loggers can cut down in one day:
number of loggers × trees per logger per day
= 8 loggers * 6 trees/logger = 48 trees
Then divide the total number of trees by the number cut down per day to find the number of days it takes to clear the forest:
total_trees ÷ trees_per_day
= 12000 trees / 48 trees/day = 250 days
Then divide the time in days by the number of days per month to find the time in months:
total_days ÷ days per month
= 250 days / 30 days/month = 8.33333333333 months
Final Answer: 8.33333333333
