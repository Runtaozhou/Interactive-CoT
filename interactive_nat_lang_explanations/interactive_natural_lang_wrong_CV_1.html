Problem Statement

Sally is saving up for a trip to Sea World. She already has $28 saved. It costs her $10 to park, $55 to get into the park and $25 for a meal pass. Sea World is 165 miles away and her car gets 30 miles per gallon of gas. If gas costs $3 a gallon, how much more will she have to save up?

Problem Understanding

Money already saved: $28

Parking cost: $10

Park entry cost: $55

Meal pass cost: $25

Distance to Sea World: 165 miles

Car efficiency: 30 miles per gallon

Gas price: $3 per gallon

What we need to find

How much more money Sally needs to save for the trip.

Step-by-Step Explanation
Calculate the total distance for the round trip:
distance × 2
= 165 miles × 2 = 330 miles
Calculate how many gallons of gas are needed:
total distance ÷ miles per gallon
= 330 miles ÷ 30 miles per gallon = 11 gallons
Calculate the cost of gas:
gallons needed × price per gallon
= 11 gallons × $3 per gallon = $33
Calculate the total cost of the trip:
parking + entry + meal + gas
= $10 + $50 + $25 + $33 = $118
Calculate how much more money Sally needs to save:
total cost - money already saved
= $118 - $28 = $90
Final Answer: $90
