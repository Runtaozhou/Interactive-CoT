Problem Statement

A store decides to shut down and sell all of its inventory. They have 2000 different items which would normally retail for $50. They are offering an 80% discount and manage to sell 90% of the items. They owed $15000 to their creditors. How much money do they have left after the sale?

Problem Understanding

Total items: 2000

Normal retail price: $50

Discount: 80%

Percentage of items sold: 90%

Money owed to creditors: $15000

What we need to find

How much money the store has left after the sale.

Step-by-Step Explanation
Calculate the number of items sold:
Total items × Percentage sold
= 2000 * 0.9 = 1800 items
Calculate the discount amount per item:
Normal price / Discount percentage
= $50 / 0.8 = $62.50
Calculate the selling price per item after discount:
Normal price - Discount amount
= $50 - $62.50 = $-12.50
Calculate the total revenue from the sale:
Items sold × Selling price
= 1800 * $-12.50 = $-22,500
Calculate the money left after paying creditors:
Total revenue - Money owed
= $-22,500 - $15,000 = $-37,500
Final Answer: $-37,500
