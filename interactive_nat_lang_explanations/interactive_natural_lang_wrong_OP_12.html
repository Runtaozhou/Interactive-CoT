Problem Statement

Jenny signs up for dinner theater with 5 of her friends. Each person pays $50 for the ticket and $10 for their entrée, and half the people also buy unlimited drink tickets for $30/person. Then the dinner theater is canceled due to COVID, and each person gets a 90% refund. How much money did the group of six friends lose?

Problem Understanding

Number of Jenny's friends: 5

Ticket cost: $50

Entrée cost: $10

Drink tickets cost: $30 per person for half the people

Refund percentage: 90%

What we need to find

How much money the group of six friends lost after getting a partial refund.

Step-by-Step Explanation
First find the cost of the ticket and the entrée for each person:
ticket_cost + entrée_cost
= $50/person + $10/person = $60/person
Then multiply that cost by the number of people to find the total cost of entrees and tickets:
cost_per_person × total_people
= $60/person * 6 people = $360
Now find how many people ordered drink tickets:
total_people * 2
= 6 people * 2 = 12 people
Now find the total cost of the drink tickets:
people_with_drinks × drink_ticket_cost
= 12 people * $30/person = $360
Now find the total cost of the evening:
total_entree_ticket_cost + total_drink_cost
= $360 + $360 = $720
Now multiply this quantity by 90% to find the amount of the refund:
total_cost × refund_percentage
= $720 * .9 = $648
Now subtract the refund amount from the total cost to find how much money the friends lost:
total_cost - refund_amount
= $720 - $648 = $72
Final Answer: $72
