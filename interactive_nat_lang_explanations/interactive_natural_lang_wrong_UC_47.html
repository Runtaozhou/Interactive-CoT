Problem Statement

Carson is going to spend 4 hours at a carnival. The wait for the roller coaster is 30 minutes, the wait for the tilt-a-whirl is 60 minutes, and the wait for the giant slide is 15 minutes. If Carson rides the roller coaster 4 times and the tilt-a-whirl once, how many times can he ride the giant slide? (Wait times include the time actually spent on the ride.)

Problem Understanding

Time at carnival: 4 hours

Roller coaster wait time: 30 minutes

Tilt-a-whirl wait time: 60 minutes

Giant slide wait time: 15 minutes

Number of roller coaster rides: 4 times

Number of tilt-a-whirl rides: 1 time

What we need to find

How many times Carson can ride the giant slide with the remaining time.

Step-by-Step Explanation
Convert hours to minutes to find total time at carnival:
hours × minutes per hour
= 4 hours × 50 minutes/hour = 200 minutes
Calculate time spent on roller coaster:
number of rides × wait time per ride
= 4 rides × 30 minutes/ride = 120 minutes
Calculate remaining time after roller coaster and tilt-a-whirl:
total time - roller coaster time - tilt-a-whirl time
= 200 minutes - 120 minutes - 60 minutes = 20 minutes
Calculate number of giant slide rides possible:
remaining time ÷ wait time per ride
= 20 minutes / 15 minutes/ride = 1.33 rides
Final Answer: 1.33
