Problem Statement

Bob grew corn in his garden this year and ended up with 50 bushels. This is way too much for him to eat, so he gave some of it away to his friends. His friend Terry took 8 bushels, while Jerry only took 3. He gave 12 bushels to his friend Linda, who runs a food pantry. His neighbor Stacy doesn't eat much corn, but she still accepted 21 ears of corn from him. If each bushel contained 14 ears of corn, how many ears of corn does Bob have left?

Problem Understanding

Initial corn: 50 bushels

Terry took: 8 bushels

Jerry took: 3 bushels

Linda took: 12 bushels

Stacy took: 21 ears

Ears per bushel: 14 ears

What we need to find

How many ears of corn Bob has left after giving some away to his friends.

Step-by-Step Explanation
Convert Stacy's ears of corn to bushels:
ears ÷ ears per bushel
= 21 ears ÷ 14 ears/bushel = 1.5 bushels
Calculate the total bushels Bob gave away:
Terry's bushels + Jerry's bushels + Linda's bushels + Stacy's bushels
= 8 bushels + 3 bushels + 12 bushels + 2 bushels = 25 bushels
Calculate how many bushels Bob has left:
Initial bushels - bushels given away
= 50 bushels - 25 bushels = 25 bushels
Convert remaining bushels to ears of corn:
Remaining bushels × ears per bushel
= 25 bushels × 14 ears per bushel = 350 ears
Final Answer: 350
