Problem Statement

Sam and Jeff had a skipping competition at recess. The competition was split into four rounds. Sam completed 1 more skip than Jeff in the first round. Jeff skipped 3 fewer times than Sam in the second round. Jeff skipped 4 more times than Sam in the third round. Jeff got tired and only completed half the number of skips as Sam in the last round. If Sam skipped 16 times in each round, what is the average number of skips per round completed by Jeff?

Problem Understanding

Number of rounds: 4

Sam's skips in round 1: 1 more than Jeff

Jeff's skips in round 2: 3 fewer than Sam

Jeff's skips in round 3: 4 more than Sam

Jeff's skips in round 4: half of Sam's

Sam's skips per round: 16

What we need to find

The average number of skips per round completed by Jeff.

Step-by-Step Explanation
Calculate how many skips Jeff completed in round 1:
Sam's skips - 1
= 16 - 1 = 15
Calculate how many skips Jeff completed in round 2:
Sam's skips - 3
= 16 - 3 = 13
Calculate how many skips Jeff completed in round 3:
Sam's skips + 4
= 16 + 4 = 20
Calculate how many skips Jeff completed in round 4:
Sam's skips ÷ 2
= 16 / 2 = 8
Calculate the total number of skips Jeff completed:
jeff_round1 + jeff_round2 + jeff_round3 + jeff_round4
= 15 + 13 + 20 + 8 = 56
Calculate the average number of skips per round for Jeff:
jeff_total ÷ number of rounds
= 56 / 4 = 14
Final Answer: 14
