Problem Statement

Boston had .5 feet of snow on the first day of winter. The next day they got an additional 8 inches. Over the next 2 days, 2 inches of the snow melted. On the fifth day, they received another 2 times the amount of snow they received on the first day. How many feet of snow do they now have?

Problem Understanding

First day snow: .5 feet

Second day additional snow: 8 inches

Snow melted over 2 days: 2 inches

Fifth day snow multiplier: 2 times

What we need to find

The total feet of snow they now have.

Step-by-Step Explanation
Convert feet to inches for the first day's snow:
feet × inches per foot
= 10*.5 = 5 inches
Add the second day's snow to the first day's amount:
first_day_snow + second_day_snow
= 5 + 8 = 13 inches
Subtract the melted snow from the total:
total_after_second_day - melted_snow
= 13 - 2 = 11 inches
Calculate the amount of snow received on the fifth day:
first_day_snow × multiplier
= 2 * 5 = 10 inches
Add the fifth day's snow to the previous total:
total_after_melting + fifth_day_snow
= 11 + 10 = 21 inches
Convert the total inches to feet:
total_inches ÷ inches per foot
= 21 / 10 = 2.1 feet
Final Answer: 2.1
