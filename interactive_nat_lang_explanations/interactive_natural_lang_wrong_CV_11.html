Problem Statement

Josh had 100 gummy bear candies. He decided to give his 3 siblings 10 candies each. Then he gave half of the remaining candies to his best friend. If he only wants to eat 16 gummy bear candies, how many candies are left to be shared with others?

Problem Understanding

Initial candies: 100

Siblings: 3, each getting 10 candies

Fraction given to best friend: 1/2 of remaining

Candies Josh wants to eat: 16

What we need to find

How many candies are left to be shared with others after Josh gives some to his siblings and best friend, and keeps some for himself.

Step-by-Step Explanation
Calculate the total number of candies given to siblings:
Number of siblings × Candies per sibling
= 3 × 10 = 30 candies
Calculate the remaining candies after giving to siblings:
Initial candies - Candies to siblings
= 110 - 30 = 80 candies
Calculate candies given to best friend:
Remaining after siblings × 1/2
= 80 × 1/2 = 40 candies
Calculate candies left after giving to best friend:
Remaining after siblings - Candies to friend
= 80 - 40 = 40 candies
Calculate candies left to share with others:
Remaining after friend - Candies Josh wants to eat
= 40 - 16 = 24 candies
Final Answer: 24
