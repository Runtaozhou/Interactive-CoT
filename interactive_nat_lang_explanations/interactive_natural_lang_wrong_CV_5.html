Problem Statement

A small, old, wooden bridge can hold up to 100 kilograms at once. Mike, Megan, and Kelly are three children who decided to cross the bridge at the same time. Kelly weighs 34 kilograms, which is 15% less than Megan. Mike weighs 5 kilograms more than Megan. How much too much do the three children weigh together to cross the bridge?

Problem Understanding

Bridge weight limit: 100 kilograms

Kelly's weight: 34 kilograms

Kelly weighs 15% less than Megan

Mike weighs 5 kilograms more than Megan

What we need to find

How much too much the three children weigh together to cross the bridge.

Step-by-Step Explanation
Calculate what percentage of Megan's weight Kelly weighs:
100% - 15%
= 100 - 20 = 80%
Calculate Megan's weight using Kelly's weight and the percentage:
Kelly's weight ÷ percentage
= 34 / 0.80 = 42.5 kilograms
Calculate Mike's weight using Megan's weight:
Megan's weight + 5 kilograms
= 42.5 + 5 = 47.5 kilograms
Calculate the total weight of all three children:
Kelly's weight + Megan's weight + Mike's weight
= 34 + 42.5 + 47.5 = 124 kilograms
Calculate how much too much the children weigh for the bridge:
Total weight - bridge weight limit
= 124 - 100 = 24 kilograms
Final Answer: 24
