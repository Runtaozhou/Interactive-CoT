Problem Statement

A local college is offering German lessons and currently has 8 students enrolled. Through advertising, 8 more became interested but a fourth of these dropped out within a day. 2 more got frustrated and left. The class then rallied to show how simple the course actually is and increased enrollment by 5 times the amount of students already enrolled in the class, but 2 had to drop it because of scheduling conflicts. After one last rally, 6 more people enrolled. As the days passed, half of the class eventually dropped, and half of the remaining students graduated. How many are still enrolled?

Problem Understanding

Initial students: 8

Additional interested students: 8

Dropout rate of new students: 1/4

Additional dropouts: 2

Enrollment increase factor: 5

Schedule conflict dropouts: 2

Final rally enrollments: 6

Later dropout rate: 1/2

Graduation rate of remaining: 1/2

What we need to find

How many students are still enrolled in the German lessons after all the changes in enrollment.

Step-by-Step Explanation
Calculate the total number of students after the initial advertising:
initial_students + new_students
= 8 students + 8 students = 16 students
Calculate how many new students dropped out within a day:
new_students × dropout_rate
= 8 students * 0.25 = 2 students
Calculate the number of students after initial dropouts:
total_after_advertising - initial_dropouts - additional_dropouts
= 16 students - 2 students - 2 students = 12 students
Calculate the number of students after the first rally:
students_after_dropouts × (1 + enrollment_increase_factor)
= 12 students * 5 + 12 students = 72 students
Calculate the number of students after scheduling conflicts:
students_after_rally × 2
= 72 students * 2 = 144 students
Calculate the number of students after the final rally:
students_after_conflicts + final_rally_enrollments
= 144 students + 6 students = 150 students
Calculate the number of students after half dropped out:
total_enrolled ÷ 2
= 150 students / 2 = 75 students
Calculate the number of students still enrolled after graduation:
students_after_dropouts_2 ÷ 2
= 75 students / 2 = 37.5 students
Final Answer: 37.5
