Problem Statement

Adam's wardrobe is too crowded so he decides to donate some of his clothes to a charity shop. He takes out 4 pairs of pants, 4 jumpers, 4 pajama sets (top and bottom), and 20 t-shirts, then asks his friends if they have anything they want to donate. 3 of his friends donate the same amount of clothing as Adam each. Then he takes another look over his clothing and decides that he actually wants to keep half of his clothes. How many articles of clothing are being donated in total?

Problem Understanding

Adam's pants: 4 pairs

Adam's jumpers: 4

Adam's pajama sets: 4 (top and bottom)

Adam's t-shirts: 20

Friends donating: 3 (same amount as Adam each)

Adam keeps: half of his clothes

What we need to find

The total number of articles of clothing being donated.

Step-by-Step Explanation
Calculate how many articles of clothing the pajama sets represent:
pajama_sets × 2
= 4 pajama sets × 2 = 8 articles of clothing
Calculate the total number of articles Adam is initially donating:
pants + jumpers + pajama_pieces + t-shirts
= 4 pairs of pants + 4 jumpers + 8 pajama pieces + 20 t-shirts = 36 articles of clothing
Calculate the total number of articles donated by Adam's friends:
number_of_friends × articles_per_friend
= 3 friends × 30 articles of clothing per friend = 90 articles of clothing
Calculate Adam's final donation after he takes back half:
adam_initial_donation ÷ 2
= 36 ÷ 2 = 18 articles of clothing
Calculate the total number of articles being donated:
friends_donation + adam_final_donation
= 90 + 18 = 108 articles of clothing
Final Answer: 108
