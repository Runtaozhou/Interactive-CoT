Problem Statement

Valerie needs to put stamps on the envelopes she is about to mail. She has thank you cards for each of her grandmother, uncle and aunt for the birthday presents they sent. She also has to pay the water bill and the electric bill separately. She wants to send three more mail-in rebates than she does bills and she has twice as many job applications as rebates to mail. How many stamps does she need if everything needs 1 stamp except the electric bill, which needs 2?

Problem Understanding

Thank you cards recipients: 3 people

Bills to pay: 2 bills

Mail-in rebates: 3 more than bills

Job applications: twice as many as rebates

Regular stamp requirement: 1 stamp per mail

Electric bill stamp requirement: 2 stamps

What we need to find

The total number of stamps Valerie needs for all her mail.

Step-by-Step Explanation
Calculate the number of thank you cards Valerie needs to mail:
Number of recipients
= 3 * 1 = 3 thank you cards
Identify the number of bills Valerie has to mail: 2 bills
Calculate the number of mail-in rebates based on the number of bills:
Bills + 3
= 3 + 2 = 5 mail-in rebates
Calculate the number of job applications based on the number of rebates:
Rebates * 2
= 2 * 5 = 10 applications
Calculate the total number of pieces of mail to send:
Thank you cards + Bills + Rebates + Applications
= 3 + 2 + 5 + 10 = 20 pieces of mail
Calculate the total number of stamps needed, accounting for the extra stamp for the electric bill:
Total mail + Extra stamp for electric bill
= 20 + 2 = 22 stamps
Final Answer: 22
