Problem Statement

Marcus has received a commission for as many paintings as possible. Marcus plans out his drawings so that his client can receive some of the paintings as soon as possible but it is still going to take a long time. On the first day, he paints 2 paintings. He then paints every day and each day, he paints twice as many paintings as he painted the day before. If he paints for 5 days, how many paintings will he have painted in total?

Problem Understanding

Commission: as many paintings as possible

First day paintings: 2 paintings

Total painting days: 5 days

What we need to find

The total number of paintings Marcus will have painted after 5 days.

Step-by-Step Explanation
Calculate how many paintings Marcus paints on the second day:
First day paintings × 2
= 2 paintings × 2 = 4 paintings
Calculate how many paintings Marcus paints on the third day:
Second day paintings × 2
= 4 paintings × 2 = 8 paintings
Calculate how many paintings Marcus paints on the fourth day:
Third day paintings × 2
= 8 paintings × 2 = 16 paintings
Calculate how many paintings Marcus paints on the fifth day:
Fourth day paintings × 2
= 16 paintings × 2 = 32 paintings
Add up all the paintings from all five days to find the total:
day_1_paintings + day_2_paintings + day_3_paintings + day_4_paintings + day_5_paintings
= 2 + 4 + 8 + 16 + 32 = 62 paintings
Final Answer: 62
