Problem Statement

Jimmy is at the candy store and buys 2 candy bars for $.75 each. He then buys 4 lollipops that cost $.25 each. He spent 1/6 of the money he earned from shoveling snow. If he charges $1.5 per driveway, how many driveways did he shovel?

Problem Understanding

Candy bars: 2 for $0.75 each

Lollipops: 4 for $0.25 each

Spending ratio: 1/6 of snow shoveling earnings

Charge per driveway: $1.5

What we need to find

How many driveways Jimmy shoveled.

Step-by-Step Explanation
Calculate the total cost of candy bars:
number of candy bars × price per candy bar
= 2 × $.75 = $1.5
Calculate the total cost of lollipops:
number of lollipops × price per lollipop
= 4 × $.25 = $1
Calculate the total amount Jimmy spent at the candy store:
candy_bar_cost + lollipop_cost
= $1.5 + $1 = $2.5
Calculate how much money Jimmy earned from shoveling snow:
total_spent × (1/6)
= $2.5 * (1/6) = $0.4166666667
Calculate the number of driveways Jimmy shoveled:
snow_shoveling_earnings ÷ charge per driveway
= $0.4166666667 ÷ $1.5 = 0.277777778
Final Answer: 0.277777778
