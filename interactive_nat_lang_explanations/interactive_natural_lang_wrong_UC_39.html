Problem Statement

It takes a dog groomer 10 minutes to dry a short-haired dog like a beagle. It takes him twice as long to dry a full-haired, fluffy dog like a german shepherd. One morning, he has 6 short-haired dogs to dry and 9 full-haired dogs to dry. How many hours does it take him to dry all of the dogs?

Problem Understanding

Short-haired dog drying time: 10 minutes

Full-haired dog drying time: twice as long as short-haired

Number of short-haired dogs: 6

Number of full-haired dogs: 9

What we need to find

The total number of hours it takes the groomer to dry all of the dogs.

Step-by-Step Explanation
Calculate the time needed to dry a full-haired dog:
short_haired_time × 2
= 10 minutes × 2 = 20 minutes
Calculate the total time needed for all short-haired dogs:
number_of_short_haired × short_haired_time
= 6 × 10 minutes = 60 minutes
Calculate the total time needed for all full-haired dogs:
number_of_full_haired × full_haired_time
= 9 × 20 minutes = 180 minutes
Calculate the total time needed for all dogs:
total_short_haired_time + total_full_haired_time
= 60 minutes + 180 minutes = 240 minutes
Convert the total time from minutes to hours:
total_minutes ÷ minutes_per_hour
= 240 minutes ÷ 50 = 4.8 hours
Final Answer: 4.8 hours
