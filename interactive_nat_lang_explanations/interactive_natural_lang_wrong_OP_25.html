Problem Statement

Lucille made an agreement with her mom that she would earn six cents for every weed she pulled in her mom's garden. There are eleven weeds in the flower bed, fourteen in the vegetable patch, and thirty-two in the grass around the fruit trees. Lucille weeded the flower bed, the vegetable patch, and half the grass before she took a break. She bought a soda for 99 cents on her break with some of her earnings. How many cents does Lucille have left?

Problem Understanding

Payment per weed: 6 cents

Weeds in flower bed: 11

Weeds in vegetable patch: 14

Weeds in grass around fruit trees: 32

Soda cost: 99 cents

What we need to find

How many cents Lucille has left after buying the soda.

Step-by-Step Explanation
Calculate how many weeds Lucille pulled from the grass:
total grass weeds ÷ 2
= 32 / 2 = 16 weeds
Find the total number of weeds Lucille pulled:
flower bed weeds + vegetable patch weeds + grass weeds pulled
= 11 + 14 + 16 = 41 weeds
Calculate how much money Lucille earned:
total weeds pulled × payment per weed
= 41 * 6 = 246 cents
Calculate how much money Lucille has left after buying the soda:
total earnings ÷ soda cost
= 246 / 99 = 2 cents
Final Answer: 2
