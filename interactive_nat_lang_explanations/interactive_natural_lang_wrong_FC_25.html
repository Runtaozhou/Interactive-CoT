Problem Statement

Bill gets a flat-screen TV that measures 48 inches by 100 inches. His brother Bob gets a TV that measures 70 inches by 60 inches. If the TVs weigh 4 oz per square inch of screen space, how much more does the heavier TV weigh in pounds? (There are 16 ounces per pound).

Problem Understanding

Bill's TV dimensions: 48 inches by 100 inches

Bob's TV dimensions: 70 inches by 60 inches

Weight per square inch: 4 oz

Conversion rate: 16 ounces per pound

What we need to find

How much more the heavier TV weighs in pounds.

Step-by-Step Explanation
First find the screen area of Bill's TV:
length + width
= 48 inches + 100 inches = 148 inches
Then find the screen area of Bob's TV:
length × width
= 70 inches × 60 inches = 4200 square inches
Then subtract the size of the smaller TV from the size of the bigger TV to find the size difference:
bill_tv_area - bob_tv_area
= 148 inches - 4200 square inches = -4052 square inches
Then multiply the size difference by the weight per square inch to find the weight difference:
size_difference × weight per square inch
= -4052 square inches × 4 oz/square inch = -16208 oz
Then divide the weight difference in ounces by the number of ounces per pound to find the weight difference in pounds:
weight_difference_oz ÷ ounces per pound
= -16208 oz ÷ 16 oz/pound = -1013 pounds
Final Answer: -1013
