Problem Statement

James decides to sell 80% of his toys. He bought them for $20 each and sells them for $30 each. If he had 200 toys how much more money did he have compared to before he bought them?

Problem Understanding

Percentage of toys sold: 80%

Purchase price per toy: $20

Selling price per toy: $30

Total number of toys: 200

What we need to find

How much more money James had compared to before he bought the toys.

Step-by-Step Explanation
Calculate the total cost of buying all the toys:
purchase price per toy × total number of toys
= $20 × 200 = $4000
Calculate the number of toys sold:
total number of toys × percentage sold
= 200 × 0.8 = 160 toys
Calculate the money made from selling the toys:
toys sold × selling price per toy
= 160 × $30 = $4800
Calculate the profit ratio:
money made ÷ total cost
= $4800 ÷ $4000 = $1.2 profit
Final Answer: 1.2
