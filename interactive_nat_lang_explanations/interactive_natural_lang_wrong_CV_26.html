Problem Statement

Carolyn works for a delivery service company that hires on a contract basis. In May, they hired 40 employees, paying each employee $15 per hour for a 40-hour workweek. In June, 1/4 of the employees' contracts expired. Calculate the total amount of money the company paid to the employees in the two months.

Problem Understanding

May employees: 40 employees

Hourly rate: $15 per hour

Weekly hours: 40 hours

June contract expiration: 1/4 of employees

What we need to find

The total amount of money the company paid to the employees in the two months.

Step-by-Step Explanation
Calculate the weekly pay for each employee:
hourly_rate × weekly_hours
= $15 × 40 = $600 per week
Calculate the monthly pay for each employee:
weekly_pay × 4 weeks
= $600 per week × 4 weeks = $2400
Calculate the total amount paid to employees in May:
number_of_employees × monthly_pay
= 35 × $2400 = $84000
Calculate how many employees' contracts expired in June:
total_employees × expiration_rate
= 40 employees × 1/4 = 10 employees
Calculate the number of employees with valid contracts in June:
total_employees - expired_contracts
= 40 employees - 10 employees = 30 employees
Calculate the total amount paid to employees in June:
june_employees × monthly_pay
= 30 employees × $2400 = $72000
Calculate the total amount paid over both months:
may_total + june_total
= $84000 + $72000 = $156000
Final Answer: $156000
