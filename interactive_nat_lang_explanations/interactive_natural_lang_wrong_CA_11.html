Problem Statement

A school is buying virus protection software to cover 50 devices. One software package costs $40 and covers up to 5 devices. The other software package costs $60 and covers up to 10 devices. How much money, in dollars, can the school save by buying the $60 software package instead of the $40 software package?

Problem Understanding

Total devices: 50

First package: $40 for 5 devices

Second package: $60 for 10 devices

What we need to find

How much money the school can save by buying the $60 software package instead of the $40 software package.

Step-by-Step Explanation
Calculate how many $40 packages are needed:
total_devices ÷ devices_per_package
= 50 ÷ 5 = 10
Calculate the total cost for the $40 packages:
packages_needed_40 × cost_per_package
= 10 × $40 = $400
Calculate how many $60 packages are needed:
total_devices ÷ devices_per_package
= 50 ÷ 10 = 5
Calculate the total cost for the $60 packages:
packages_needed_60 × cost_per_package
= 5 × $60 = $300
Calculate the savings by using the $60 packages:
total_cost_40 - total_cost_60
= $400 - $300 = $100
Final Answer: $100
