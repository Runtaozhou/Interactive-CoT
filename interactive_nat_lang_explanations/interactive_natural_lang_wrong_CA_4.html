Problem Statement

Harry decided to buy some balloons for his friend's birthday party. One balloon costs $0.5, and a pack of 10 balloons is cheaper and costs only $3. Finally, Harry wants to bring to the party exactly 14 balloons. How much did he need to pay for them?

Problem Understanding

One balloon cost: $0.5

Pack of 10 balloons cost: $3

Number of balloons needed: 14

What we need to find

How much Harry needed to pay for the balloons.

Step-by-Step Explanation
To buy 14 balloons, Harry should buy one pack of 10, and 4 separate balloons:
total_balloons - pack_size = separate_balloons
= 14 - 10 = 4
The price of 4 balloons is calculated by multiplying the number of balloons by the price per balloon:
separate_balloons × price_per_balloon
= 4 * 0.5 = $3
In total, the pack and separate balloons would cost Harry in total by adding the pack cost and separate balloons cost:
pack_cost + separate_balloons_cost
= $3 + $3 = $6
Final Answer: $6
