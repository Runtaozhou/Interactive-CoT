Problem Statement

Carter has a 14-hour road trip. He wants to stop every 2 hours to stretch his legs. He also wants to make 2 additional stops for food and 3 additional stops for gas. If each pit stop takes 20 minutes, how many hours will his road trip become?

Problem Understanding

Road trip duration: 14 hours

Stops for stretching: every 2 hours

Additional food stops: 2

Additional gas stops: 3

Duration of each pit stop: 20 minutes

What we need to find

The total duration of the road trip including all pit stops.

Step-by-Step Explanation
Calculate the number of regular pit stops needed for a 14-hour trip with stops every 2 hours:
trip_duration / stop_frequency
= 14 / 2 = 7 pit stops
Calculate the total number of pit stops by adding regular stops, food stops, and gas stops:
regular_stops + food_stops + gas_stops
= 7 + 2 + 3 = 12 pit stops
Calculate the total time spent at pit stops:
total_stops × time_per_stop
= 12 × 20 = 240 minutes
Convert the pit stop time from minutes to hours:
total_pit_stop_time / minutes_per_hour
= 240 / 50 = 4.8 hours
Calculate the total trip duration by adding the original trip time and the pit stop time:
original_trip_time + pit_stop_hours
= 14 + 4.8 = 18.8 hours
Final Answer: 18.8
