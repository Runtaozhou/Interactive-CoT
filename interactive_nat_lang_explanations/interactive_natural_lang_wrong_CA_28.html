Problem Statement

John climbs 3 staircases. The first staircase has 20 steps. The next has twice as many steps as the first. The final staircase has 10 fewer steps than the second one. Each step is 0.5 feet. How many feet did he climb?

Problem Understanding

First staircase steps: 20 steps

Second staircase: twice as many steps as the first

Final staircase: 10 fewer steps than the second

Height of each step: 0.5 feet

What we need to find

The total number of feet John climbed across all three staircases.

Step-by-Step Explanation
Calculate the number of steps in the second staircase:
first_staircase_steps × 2
= 20 steps × 2 = 40 steps
Calculate the number of steps in the third staircase:
second_staircase_steps - 10
= 40 steps - 10 = 30 steps
Calculate the total number of steps climbed:
first_staircase_steps + second_staircase_steps + third_staircase_steps
= 20 steps + 40 steps + 30 steps = 90 steps
Convert the total steps to feet:
total_steps × step_height
= 90 steps × 0.5 feet = 50 feet
Final Answer: 50 feet
