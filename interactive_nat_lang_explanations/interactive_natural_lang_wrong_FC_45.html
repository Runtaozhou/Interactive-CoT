Problem Statement

John has 3 boxes. Each box is 5 inches by 6 inches by 4 inches. The walls are 1 inch thick. What is the total inner volume of all 3 boxes?

Problem Understanding

Number of boxes: 3

Box dimensions: 5 inches by 6 inches by 4 inches

Wall thickness: 1 inch

What we need to find

The total inner volume of all 3 boxes.

Step-by-Step Explanation
Calculate how much the walls subtract from each dimension:
2 * wall thickness
= 2 * 1 = 2 inches
Calculate the inner width of each box:
width - dimension_reduction
= 5 - 2 = 3 inches
Calculate the inner height of each box:
height - dimension_reduction
= 6 - 2 = 4 inches
Calculate the inner depth of each box:
depth - dimension_reduction
= 4 - 2 = 2 inches
Calculate the inner volume of one box:
2*(inner_width+inner_height+inner_width+inner_height)+2*(inner_depth+inner_width+inner_depth+inner_width)+2*(inner_width+inner_depth+inner_width+inner_depth)
= 2*(3+4+3+4)+2*(2+3+2+3)+2*(3+2+3+2)=100 cubic inches
Calculate the total inner volume of all boxes:
number_of_boxes * inner_volume_one_box
= 3 * 100 = 300 cubic inches
Final Answer: 300
