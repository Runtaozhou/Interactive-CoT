Problem Statement

Linda is painting her bedroom. Her bedroom has 4 walls, with the room being 20 feet wide by 20 feet long by 8 feet tall. One wall has a 3-foot by 7-foot doorway. A second wall has a 6-foot by 4-foot window. A third wall has a 5-foot by 7-foot doorway to a walk-in-closet. And the fourth wall is completely solid. What is the total area of wall space that Linda will have to paint?

Problem Understanding

Number of walls: 4

Room dimensions: 20 feet wide by 20 feet long by 8 feet tall

Doorway dimensions: 3 feet by 7 feet

Window dimensions: 6 feet by 4 feet

Closet doorway dimensions: 5 feet by 7 feet

What we need to find

The total area of wall space that Linda will have to paint.

Step-by-Step Explanation
Calculate the area of the solid wall:
height × width
= 8 ft. * 20 ft. = 160 sq. ft.
Calculate the area of the doorway:
height × width
= 3 ft. * 7 ft. = 21 sq. ft.
Calculate the area of the window:
height × width
= 6 ft. * 4 ft. = 24 sq. ft.
Calculate the area of the closet door:
height × width
= 5 ft. * 7 ft. = 35 sq. ft.
Calculate the total area that doesn't need painting:
doorway_area + window_area + closet_door_area + painting_area
= 21 sq. ft + 24 sq. ft. + 35 sq. ft. + 4 sq. ft. = 84 sq. ft.
Calculate the total wall area before subtracting non-paintable areas:
number of walls × solid_wall_area
= 4 * 160 sq. ft. = 640 sq. ft.
Calculate the total area Linda will have to paint:
total_wall_area - non_paintable_area
= 640 sq. ft. - 84 sq. ft. = 556 sq. ft.
Final Answer: 556
