Problem Statement

A school needs to paint the classroom, which is 9 meters long, 8 meters wide, and 3 meters high. The classroom has 4 windows, 2 blackboards, and 2 doors. The windows are 1m by 1.5m, the blackboards are 6m by 1m, and the doors are 2m by 1m. It is known that the paint fee is $4.80 per square meter. How much does it cost to paint a classroom?

Problem Understanding

Length: 9 meters

Width: 8 meters

Height: 3 meters

Number of windows: 4

Number of blackboards: 2

Number of doors: 2

Window dimensions: 1m by 1.5m

Blackboard dimensions: 6m by 1m

Door dimensions: 2m by 1m

Paint fee: $4.80 per square meter

What we need to find

The total cost to paint the classroom.

Step-by-Step Explanation
Calculate the total surface perimeter of the classroom excluding the floor:
(length + width) * 2 + width * height * 2 + length * height * 2
= (9 + 8) * 2 + 8 * 3 * 2 + 9 * 3 * 2 = 90 meters
Calculate the area of one window:
window length * window width
= 1 * 1.5 = 1.5 square meters
Calculate the total area of all windows:
number of windows * one window area
= 4 * 1.5 = 6 square meters
Calculate the area of one blackboard:
blackboard length * blackboard width
= 6 * 1 = 6 square meters
Calculate the total area of all blackboards:
number of blackboards * one blackboard area
= 2 * 6 = 12 square meters
Calculate the area of one door:
door length * door width
= 2 * 1 = 2 square meters
Calculate the total area of all doors:
number of doors * one door area
= 2 * 2 = 4 square meters
Calculate the perimeter that needs to be painted:
total surface perimeter - total windows area - total blackboards area - total doors area
= 90 - 6 - 12 - 4 = 68 meters
Calculate the total cost of painting the classroom:
paintable perimeter * paint fee per square meter
= 68 * 4.8 = $326.4
Final Answer: $326.4
