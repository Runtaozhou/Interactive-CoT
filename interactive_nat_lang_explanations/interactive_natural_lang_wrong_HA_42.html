Problem Statement

Mitch is baking a cake and needs 3 cups of flour and two cups of sugar. He doesn't have a one cup scoop, only a 1/3 cup scoop. How many total scoops will he need?

Problem Understanding

Flour needed: 3 cups

Sugar needed: 2 cups

Scoop size: 1/3 cup

What we need to find

The total number of scoops Mitch will need for his cake.

Step-by-Step Explanation
Calculate how many scoops of flour Mitch needs:
cups of flour ÷ scoop size
= 3 ÷ (1/3) = 9
Calculate how many scoops of sugar Mitch needs:
cups of sugar ÷ scoop size
= 2 ÷ (1/3) = 6
Account for the additional cocoa powder needed:
1 scoop for 1/3 cup of cocoa powder = 1
Add up all the scoops needed:
flour_scoops + sugar_scoops + cocoa_scoops
= 9 + 6 + 1 = 16
Final Answer: 16
