Problem Statement

A fixer uses 30% of the nails in a container to repair the kitchen. He also used 70% of the remaining nails in the container to repair the fence. If there were 400 nails in the container, how many nails are remaining?

Problem Understanding

Percentage of nails used for kitchen: 30%

Percentage of remaining nails used for fence: 70%

Total nails in container: 400

What we need to find

The number of nails remaining after both repairs.

Step-by-Step Explanation
Calculate how many nails were used to fix the kitchen:
Total nails × Percentage used for kitchen
= 400 × 30/100 = 120 nails
Calculate how many nails remain after fixing the kitchen:
Total nails - Kitchen nails
= 400 - 120 = 280 nails
Calculate how many nails were used to fix the fence:
Remaining nails × Percentage used for fence
= 280 × 70/100 = 196 nails
Calculate the final number of remaining nails:
Remaining after kitchen - Fence nails
= 280 - 190 = 90 nails
Final Answer: 90
