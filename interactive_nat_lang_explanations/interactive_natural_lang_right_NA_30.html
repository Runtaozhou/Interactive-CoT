Problem Statement

James has a room that is 13 feet by 18 feet. He increases each dimension by 2 feet. He then builds 3 more rooms of equal size and 1 room of twice that size. How much area does he have?

Problem Understanding

Original room dimensions: 13 feet by 18 feet

Dimension increase: 2 feet

Additional equal-sized rooms: 3

Additional larger room: 1 room of twice the size

What we need to find

The total area of all the rooms combined.

Step-by-Step Explanation
He increases the length of the room:
original length + increase
= 13 feet + 2 feet = 15 feet
He increases the width of the room:
original width + increase
= 18 feet + 2 feet = 20 feet
Calculate the area of each room:
new_length × new_width
= 15 feet × 20 feet = 300 square feet
Calculate the total number of equal-sized rooms:
original room + additional rooms
= 1 + 3 = 4 rooms
Calculate the total area of equal-sized rooms:
equal_rooms × room_area
= 4 rooms × 300 square feet = 1200 square feet
Calculate the area of the larger room:
room_area × 2
= 300 square feet × 2 = 600 square feet
Calculate the total area of all rooms:
equal_rooms_area + larger_room_area
= 1200 square feet + 600 square feet = 1800 square feet
Final Answer: 1800 square feet
