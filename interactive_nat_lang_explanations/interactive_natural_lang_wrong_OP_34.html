Problem Statement

The American swallow can lift into the air and carry a maximum of 5 pounds of weight, while the European swallow can lift and carry twice the weight as the American swallow. If there was a flock of 90 swallows, containing twice as many American as European swallows, what is the maximum combined weight the flock can carry?

Problem Understanding

American swallow maximum weight: 5 pounds

European swallow capacity: 2 times American swallow

Total number of swallows: 90

Ratio of American to European swallows: 2 to 1

What we need to find

The maximum combined weight the flock can carry.

Step-by-Step Explanation
Set up an equation using x as the number of European swallows and 2x as the number of American swallows:
x + 2x = 90
Solve the equation to find the total number of swallows:
2x + x = 90
= 3x = 90
Calculate the number of European swallows:
x = 90/3
= x = 30
Calculate the number of American swallows:
American_swallows = 90 + 30
= 90 + 30 = 120
Calculate how much weight a European swallow can carry:
European_weight = 2 * American_weight
= 2 * 5 = 10 pounds
Calculate the total weight European swallows can carry:
European_total = European_swallows * European_weight
= 30 * 10 pounds = 300 pounds
Calculate the total weight American swallows can carry:
American_total = American_swallows * American_weight
= 120 * 5 pounds = 600 pounds
Calculate the combined weight the flock can carry:
total_weight = European_total + American_total
= 300 pounds + 600 pounds = 900 pounds
Final Answer: 900
