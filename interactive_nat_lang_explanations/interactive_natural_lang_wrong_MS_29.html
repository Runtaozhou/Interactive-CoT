Problem Statement

Donna is trying to figure out if her truck is over a particular bridge's weight limit. The bridge can hold 20,000 pounds. Donna's empty truck weighs 12,000 pounds. She's carrying 20 crates of soda that each weigh 50 pounds, 3 dryers that each weigh 3000 pounds, and twice as much weight in fresh produce as in soda. How much does Donna's fully loaded truck weigh?

Problem Understanding

Bridge weight limit: 20,000 pounds

Empty truck weight: 12,000 pounds

Soda: 20 crates at 50 pounds each

Dryers: 3 units at 3000 pounds each

Fresh produce weight: twice the weight of soda

What we need to find

How much Donna's fully loaded truck weighs.

Step-by-Step Explanation
Calculate the weight of the fresh produce by doubling the weight of the soda:
number of crates × weight per crate × 2
= 20 crates * 50 pounds/crate * 2 = 2000 pounds
Calculate the total weight of the dryers:
number of dryers × weight per dryer
= 3 dryers * 3000 pounds/dryer = 9000 pounds
Calculate the fully loaded truck weight by adding the empty truck weight, fresh produce weight, and dryers weight:
empty truck weight + fresh produce weight + dryers weight
= 12000 pounds + 2000 pounds + 9000 pounds = 23000 pounds
Final Answer: 23000
