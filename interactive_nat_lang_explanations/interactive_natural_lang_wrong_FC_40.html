Problem Statement

Bob is building a vegetable garden. It is 220 feet long by 120 feet wide. Half of the garden is going to be tilled land. A third of the rest of it will have trellises for vining plants, and the rest will be devoted to raised beds. How big will the raised bed section of the garden be?

Problem Understanding

Garden dimensions: 220 feet long by 120 feet wide

Tilled land: 1/2 of garden

Trellises: 1/3 of remaining land

What we need to find

The size of the raised bed section of the garden.

Step-by-Step Explanation
Calculate the total area of the garden:
perimeter = 2 × length + 2 × width
= 220 + 220 + 120 + 120 = 680 square feet
Calculate the area for tilled land and the remaining area:
garden_area ÷ 2
= 680 / 2 = 340 square feet for tilled land and 340 square feet remaining
Calculate the fraction of remaining land for raised beds:
1 - 1/3
= 1 - 1/3 = 2/3
Calculate the area of the raised beds:
remaining_land × raised_bed_fraction
= 340 square feet * (2/3) = 226.66666666666666 square feet
Final Answer: 226.66666666666666 square feet
