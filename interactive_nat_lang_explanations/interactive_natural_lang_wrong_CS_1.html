Problem Statement

Joe buys 3 oranges, 7 juices, 3 jars of honey, and 4 plants at the market. The fruit costs $4.50 each, the juice was 50 cents each, the jars of honey were $5, and the plants were 2 for $18. How much does Joe spend at the market?

Problem Understanding

Oranges: 3

Juices: 7

Jars of honey: 3

Plants: 4

Orange price: $4.50 each

Juice price: $0.50 each

Honey price: $5 each

Plants price: 2 for $18

What we need to find

How much Joe spends at the market in total.

Step-by-Step Explanation
Calculate the cost of oranges:
number of oranges × price per orange
= 3 × $4.50 = $13.50
Calculate the cost of juice:
number of juices × price per juice
= 7 × $0.50 = $3.50
Calculate the cost of honey:
number of honey jars × price per jar
= 3 × $5 = $15
Calculate the cost per plant:
price for 2 plants ÷ 2
= $18 ÷ 2 = $9
Calculate the total cost of plants:
number of plants × cost per plant
= 4 × $9 = $36
Calculate the total cost of all items:
orange_cost + juice_cost + honey_cost + plant_cost
= $13.50 + $3.50 + $15 + $40 = $72
Final Answer: $72
