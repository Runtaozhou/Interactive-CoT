Problem Statement

A cheetah can run at a top speed of 60 mph. The gazelle can run for speeds of up to 40 miles per hour. If one mile per hour is about 1.5 feet per second, then how many seconds would it take for a cheetah traveling at top speed to catch up to a fleeing gazelle also running at top speed if the two animals were initially 210 feet apart and they both traveled in the same direction?

Problem Understanding

Cheetah speed: 60 mph

Gazelle speed: 40 mph

Conversion rate: 1 mph = 1.5 feet per second

Initial distance: 210 feet

What we need to find

The time in seconds it takes for the cheetah to catch up to the gazelle.

Step-by-Step Explanation
Convert the cheetah's speed from mph to feet per second:
mph × conversion rate
= 60 × 1.5 = 90 feet per second
Convert the gazelle's speed from mph to feet per second:
mph × conversion rate
= 40 × 1.5 = 60 feet per second
Calculate the relative speed at which the cheetah catches up to the gazelle:
cheetah_speed - gazelle_speed
= 90 - 60 = 30 feet per second
Calculate the time it takes for the cheetah to catch the gazelle:
initial_distance ÷ relative_speed
= 210 / 20 = 10.5 seconds
Final Answer: 10.5
