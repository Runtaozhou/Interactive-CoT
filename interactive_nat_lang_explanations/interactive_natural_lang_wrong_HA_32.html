Problem Statement

Patrick has 4 hours less than twice the amount of time that Greg has left to finish his homework. Greg has six hours less than Jacob left to finish his homework. If Jacob has 18 hours left to finish his homework, calculate the total number of hours they all have left to finish their homework?

Problem Understanding

Patrick's time: 4 hours less than twice Greg's time

Greg's time: 6 hours less than Jacob's time

Jacob's time: 18 hours

What we need to find

The total number of hours they all have left to finish their homework.

Step-by-Step Explanation
Calculate how much time Greg has left by subtracting from Jacob's time:
Jacob's time - 6 hours - 2 hours
= 18 - 6 - 2 = 10 hours
Calculate the total time for Jacob and Greg:
Jacob's time + Greg's time
= 10 + 18 = 28 hours
Calculate Patrick's time based on Greg's time:
(2 × Greg's time) - 4 hours
= (2 × 10) - 4 = 20 - 4 = 16 hours
Calculate the total time for all three people:
Jacob and Greg's total + Patrick's time
= 28 + 16 = 44 hours
Final Answer: 44
