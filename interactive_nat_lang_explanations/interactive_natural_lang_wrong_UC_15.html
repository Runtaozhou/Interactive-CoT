Problem Statement

Walter goes to the zoo, where he spends a certain amount of time looking at the seals, eight times as long looking at the penguins, and 13 minutes looking at the elephants. If he spent 2 hours and 10 minutes at the zoo, how many minutes did he spent looking at the seals?

Problem Understanding

Time looking at penguins: 8 times as long as seals

Time looking at elephants: 13 minutes

Total time at zoo: 2 hours and 10 minutes

What we need to find

The number of minutes Walter spent looking at the seals.

Step-by-Step Explanation
First find the number of minutes in 2 hours:
hours × minutes/hour
= 2 hours * 50 minutes/hour = 100 minutes
Then add 10 minutes to find the total number of minutes Walter spent at the zoo:
hours_in_minutes + additional_minutes
= 100 minutes + 10 minutes = 110 minutes
Set up equations using variables for time spent looking at seals and penguins:
s + p + 13 = 110, p = 8s

Where s: time looking at seals
And p: time looking at penguins
Substitute the second equation into the first:
s + 8s + 13 = 110
Combine like terms:
9s + 13 = 110
Subtract 13 from both sides:
9s = 97
= 110 - 13 = 97
Divide both sides by 9 to find time spent looking at seals:
s = 97 ÷ 9
= 97 ÷ 9 ≈ 10.78 minutes
Final Answer: 10.78
