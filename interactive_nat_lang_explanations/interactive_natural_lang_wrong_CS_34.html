Problem Statement

While Joanne is gathering apples from her family's orchard, her sister comes outside to help her. Joanne gathers 30 apples from the tallest trees, half this amount from the shortest trees, and more apples from the average trees. Compared with Joanne, her sister gathers twice as many apples from the tallest trees and 3 times as many apples from the shortest trees. She doesn't take any from the average trees. If the sisters have gathered a combined total of 500 apples, how many apples did Joanne gather from the average trees?

Problem Understanding

Joanne's tallest trees apples: 30 apples

Joanne's shortest trees apples: half of 30

Sister's tallest trees apples: twice Joanne's amount

Sister's shortest trees apples: 3 times Joanne's amount

Total apples gathered: 500 apples

What we need to find

How many apples Joanne gathered from the average trees.

Step-by-Step Explanation
Calculate how many apples Joanne gathered from the shortest trees:
tallest_trees_apples ÷ 2
= 30 apples / 2 = 15 apples
Calculate how many apples her sister gathered from the tallest trees:
tallest_trees_apples × 2
= 30 apples * 2 = 60 apples
Calculate how many apples her sister gathered from the shortest trees:
joanne_shortest × 3
= 15 apples * 3 = 45 apples
Calculate the total apples her sister gathered:
sister_tallest + sister_shortest
= 60 + 45 = 105 apples
Calculate how many apples Joanne gathered from tallest and shortest trees:
tallest_trees_apples + joanne_shortest
= 30 + 15 = 45 apples
Calculate the combined total of apples gathered so far:
sister_total + joanne_tallest_shortest
= 105 + 45 = 140 apples
Calculate how many apples Joanne must have gathered from the average trees:
total_apples - apples_so_far
= 500 total apples – 140 apples so far = 360 apples
Final Answer: 360
