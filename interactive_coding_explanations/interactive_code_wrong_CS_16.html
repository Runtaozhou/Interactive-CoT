Problem Statement

Nick is choosing between two jobs. Job A pays $15 an hour for 2000 hours a year, and is in a state with a 20% total tax rate. Job B pays $42,000 a year and is in a state that charges $6,000 in property tax and a 10% tax rate on net income after property tax. How much more money will Nick make at the job with a higher net pay rate, compared to the other job?

Problem Summary
  • Job A hourly pay: $15
  • Job A annual hours: 2000
  • Job A tax rate: 20%
  • Job B annual salary: $42,000
  • Job B property tax: $6,000
  • Job B income tax rate: 10%

What we need to find:

How much more money Nick will make at the job with a higher net pay rate, compared to the other job.

Python Solution
1 # First calculate the gross annual salary for Job A
2 job_A_gross = 2000 * 15
3 # Next, calculate the amount of taxes Nick pays at Job A
4 job_A_taxes = 0.2 * job_A_gross
5 # Now subtract Nick's taxes from his net pay to find his gross pay at Job A
6 job_A_net = job_A_gross - job_A_taxes
7 # Now subtract Nick's property taxes from his gross income at Job B
8 job_B_after_property_tax = 42000 - 6000
9 # Now multiply Nick's income after property tax by 10% to find his income tax at Job B
10 job_B_income_tax = job_B_after_property_tax * 0.1
11 # Now subtract the income tax amount from Nick's earnings after property tax to find his net income at Job B
12 job_B_net = job_B_after_property_tax - job_B_income_tax
13 # Now subtract the net income from the lower-paying job (A) from the net income from the higher-paying job (B)
14 difference = job_B_net - job_A_net
