Problem Statement:

Everyone working at Meta has a high income. A person with a high income will not take a bus to their destination. People will either take a bus or drive to their destination. Everyone who has a car will choose to drive to their destination. No students drive to their destination. James has a car or works at Meta. Based on the given facts above, determine whether the following statement is true, false, or uncertain using deductive reasoning and give simple explanations. The statement is: James either drives to their destination or is a student.

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Analyze what we know about James

We know that James has a car or works at Meta.

Step 2: Consider if James works at Meta

If James works at Meta, then everyone working at Meta has a high income, so James has a high income.

Step 3: Determine what happens if James has a high income

If James has a high income, then a person with a high income will not take a bus to their destination. So James will not take a bus.

Step 4: Determine how James gets to his destination

Since people will either take a bus or drive to their destination, and James will not take a bus, James must drive to his destination.

Step 5: Consider if James has a car

If James has a car, then everyone who has a car will choose to drive to their destination. So James will drive to his destination.

Step 6: Combine the two possibilities

So whether James has a car or works at Meta, he will drive to his destination.

Step 7: Consider the fact about students

We also know that no students drive to their destination. Since James drives to his destination, he cannot be a student.

Step 8: Evaluate the given statement

Therefore, the statement "James either drives to their destination or is a student" is false, because James drives to his destination and is not a student.

Final Answer:

False
