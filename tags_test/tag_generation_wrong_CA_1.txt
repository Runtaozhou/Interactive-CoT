## 1. Problem statement

A basket of green food costs <fact1>$25</fact1> and a basket of red food costs <fact2>$18</fact2>. If you buy <fact3>3 baskets of green food and red food</fact3>, how much will you have to pay in total if you get <fact4>$2 off for each basket of red food</fact4>?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Cost of green food basket: $25</fact1>
<fact2>Cost of red food basket: $18</fact2>
<fact3>Number of each type of basket: 3</fact3>
<fact4>Discount per red food basket: $2</fact4>

## 3. Steps

<step1> Calculate the cost of green food baskets </step1>
<formula> green_basket_cost × number_of_baskets </formula>
<calculation> $25 × 3 = $75 </calculation>
<var> green_baskets_total : $75 </var>

<step2> Calculate the initial cost of red food baskets </step2>
<formula> red_basket_cost × number_of_baskets </formula>
<calculation> $18 × 3 = $54 </calculation>
<var> red_baskets_initial : $54 </var>

<step3> Calculate the total discount for red food baskets </step3>
<formula> discount_per_basket × number_of_baskets </formula>
<calculation> $2 × 3 = $6 </calculation>
<var> red_baskets_discount : $6 </var>

<step4> Calculate the final cost of red food baskets after discount </step4>
<formula> red_baskets_initial - red_baskets_discount </formula>
<calculation> $54 - $6 = $46 </calculation>
<var> red_baskets_final : $46 </var>

<step5> Calculate the total cost of all baskets </step5>
<formula> green_baskets_total + red_baskets_final </formula>
<calculation> $75 + $46 = $121 </calculation>
<var> total_cost : $121 </var>

<output> 121 </output>
