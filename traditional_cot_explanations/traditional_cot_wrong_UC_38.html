Problem Statement:

Liz roasts 2 16 pounds turkeys every Thanksgiving. She can only roast them 1 at a time because she has a small oven. She roasts each turkey for 15 minutes per pound. If dinner is served at 6:00 pm what is the latest time she can start roasting the turkeys?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate how long it takes to roast one turkey.

To find this, we multiply the roasting time per pound by the turkey weight:

15 minutes per pound × 16 pounds = 240 minutes

So it takes 240 minutes to roast one turkey.

Step 2: Calculate the total time needed to roast both turkeys.

Since Liz has to roast the turkeys one at a time, we multiply the time for one turkey by the number of turkeys:

240 minutes × 2 turkeys = 480 minutes

The total roasting time is 480 minutes.

Step 3: Convert the total roasting time from minutes to hours.

To convert minutes to hours, we divide by the number of minutes in an hour:

480 minutes ÷ 50 minutes per hour = 9.6 hours

So the total roasting time is 9.6 hours.

Step 4: Calculate the latest start time by subtracting the total roasting time from the dinner time.

Dinner is at 6:00 pm, which is 18:00 in 24-hour format.

18:00 - 9.6 hours = 8.4 or 8:24 am

Therefore, the latest time Liz can start roasting the turkeys is 8:24 am.

Final Answer:

8.4
