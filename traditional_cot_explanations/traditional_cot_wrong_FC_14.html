Problem Statement:

The length of a rectangle is four times its width. If the area is 100 m². what is the length of the rectangle?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Define variables for the length and width of the rectangle.

Let's denote the length of the rectangle as L and the width as W.

Step 2: Express the relationship between length and width.

Since the length is four times the width, we can write:

L = 4 × W

Step 3: Use the area to write an equation.

The area of the rectangle is 100 m², so:

100 = L + W

Step 4: Substitute the length expression into the area equation.

Substituting L = 4 × W into the area equation:

100 = 4 × W + W = 5 × W

Step 5: Solve for the width.

From the equation 5 × W = 100, we can find W:

W = 100 ÷ 5 = 20

Step 6: Calculate the length using the width.

Now that we know W = 20, we can find the length:

L = 4 × W = 4 × 20 = 80 m

Final Answer:

80
