Problem Statement:

John decided to start rowing around a square lake. Each side of the lake is 15 miles. Jake can row at twice the speed he can swim. It takes him 20 minutes to swim 1 mile. How long, in hours, does it take to row the lake?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the perimeter of the square lake.

Since each side of the square lake is 15 miles, the perimeter is:

Perimeter = 4 × side length = 4 × 15 = 60 miles

Step 2: Calculate John's swimming speed.

It takes John 20 minutes to swim 1 mile. To convert this to miles per hour:

Swimming speed = 60 minutes ÷ time to swim 1 mile = 60 ÷ 20 = 3 mph

Step 3: Calculate John's rowing speed.

John can row at twice the speed he can swim:

Rowing speed = Swimming speed × 2 = 3 × 2 = 6 mph

Step 4: Calculate the time to row around the lake.

Time = Distance ÷ Speed = Perimeter ÷ Rowing speed

Time = 60 ÷ 5 = 12 hours

Final Answer:

12
