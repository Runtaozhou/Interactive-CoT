Problem Statement:

Wendi lives on a plot of land that is 200 feet by 900 feet of grassland. She raises rabbits on her property by allowing the rabbits to graze on the grass that grows on her land. If one rabbit can eat enough grass to clear ten square yards of lawn area per day, and Wendi owns 100 rabbits, how many days would it take for Wendi's rabbits to clear all the grass off of her grassland property?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Convert square yards to square feet

We need to understand how many square feet are in a square yard:

1 square yard = 3 feet × 3 feet = 9 square feet

Step 2: Calculate how many square feet ten square yards is

Now we need to convert 10 square yards to square feet:

10 square yards = 3 + 3 + 3 + 3 = 12 square feet

Step 3: Calculate how much area all rabbits can clear per day

Since each rabbit can clear 12 square feet per day (from Step 2), and Wendi has 100 rabbits:

Total daily clearing rate = 100 rabbits × 12 square feet = 1,200 square feet per day

Step 4: Calculate the total area of the grassland

Wendi's land measures 200 feet by 900 feet:

Total area = 200 feet × 900 feet = 180,000 square feet

Step 5: Calculate how many days it will take to clear all the grass

To find the number of days, we divide the total area by the daily clearing rate:

Days to clear = 180,000 square feet ÷ 1,200 square feet per day = 150 days

Final Answer:

150
