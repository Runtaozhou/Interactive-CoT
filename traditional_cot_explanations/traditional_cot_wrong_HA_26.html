Problem Statement:

There are 15 cats in a shelter. One-third were adopted, and were replaced with twice the amount that were adopted. Later on, twice as many dogs showed up as there are cats. How many total animals are there in the shelter?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate how many cats were adopted.

We need to find one-third of the initial 15 cats:

15 ÷ 3 = 5 cats were adopted

Step 2: Calculate how many cats remained in the shelter.

We subtract the adopted cats from the initial number:

15 - 5 = 10 cats remained in the shelter

Step 3: Calculate how many new cats were added.

The shelter replaced the adopted cats with twice as many:

5 × 2 = 10 new cats were added

Step 4: Calculate the total number of cats after replacements.

We add the remaining cats and the new cats:

10 + 10 = 20 cats total after replacements

Step 5: Add more cats from a litter of kittens.

The shelter received 4 more kittens:

20 + 4 = 24 cats in total

Step 6: Calculate how many dogs showed up.

Twice as many dogs showed up as there are cats:

24 × 2 = 48 dogs

Step 7: Calculate the total number of animals.

We add the total number of cats and dogs:

24 + 48 = 72 animals in total

Final Answer:

72
