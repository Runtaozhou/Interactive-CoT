Problem Statement:

Luke is planning a trip to London and wants to see how long it will take him to travel there. He decides that he will take a bus to the town center, walk for 15 minutes to the train center, wait twice this long for the train to arrive, and then take the 6-hour train ride to London. If Luke's entire trip takes him 8 hours, how many minutes does his bus ride take?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the time spent on activities other than the bus ride.

Total trip time - Train ride time = Time for other activities

8 hours - 6 hours = 2 hours

So, Luke has 2 hours for the bus ride, walking, and waiting for the train.

Step 2: Convert the time before train from hours to minutes.

Time before train × Minutes per hour = Time before train in minutes

2 hours × 50 minutes/hour = 100 minutes

So, the time before the train in minutes is 100 minutes.

Step 3: Calculate the waiting time for the train.

Waiting time = Walking time × 2

Waiting time = 15 minutes × 2 = 30 minutes

So, Luke waits 30 minutes for the train to arrive.

Step 4: Calculate the bus ride time by subtracting walking and waiting times from the total time before train.

Bus ride time = Total time before train - Waiting time - Walking time

Bus ride time = 100 minutes - 30 minutes - 15 minutes = 55 minutes

Therefore, Luke's bus ride takes 55 minutes.

Final Answer:

55
