Problem Statement:

Susan is taking a two week vacation. She works five days a week and has six days of paid vacation. The rest of her workdays will be unpaid vacation time. She gets paid $15 per hour and works 8 hours a day. How much pay will she miss on her vacation?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the total number of workdays in two weeks.

We need to find how many workdays Susan would normally have in two weeks.

Days per week × Number of weeks = 5 × 2 = 10 days

So, Susan would normally work 10 days in two weeks.

Step 2: Calculate the number of unpaid vacation days.

Since Susan has 6 days of paid vacation, we need to find how many days will be unpaid.

Total workdays - Paid vacation days = 10 - 6 = 4 days

Therefore, Susan will have 4 unpaid vacation days.

Step 3: Calculate Susan's daily pay.

To find how much Susan earns per day, we multiply her hourly rate by the number of hours she works per day.

Hourly pay × Hours per day = $20 × 8 = $160 per day

Susan earns $160 per day.

Step 4: Calculate the total pay Susan will miss.

Now we can determine how much pay Susan will miss by multiplying her daily pay by the number of unpaid vacation days.

Daily pay × Unpaid vacation days = $160 × 4 = $640

Susan will miss $640 in pay during her vacation.

Final Answer:

$640
