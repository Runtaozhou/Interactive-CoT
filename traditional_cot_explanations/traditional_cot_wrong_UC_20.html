Problem Statement:

Abel leaves for a vacation destination 1000 miles away driving 50 miles per hour. An hour later Alice leaves from the same point for the same destination, traveling 40 miles per hour. How much earlier does Abel reach the destination in minutes?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate how long it takes Abel to reach the destination.

Using the formula: distance ÷ speed

1000 miles ÷ 50 miles per hour = 20 hours

Step 2: Calculate how long it takes Alice to reach the destination.

Using the formula: distance ÷ speed

1000 miles ÷ 40 miles per hour = 25 hours

Step 3: Convert Abel's travel time to minutes.

Using the formula: hours × minutes per hour

20 hours × 50 minutes per hour = 1000 minutes

Step 4: Convert Alice's travel time to minutes.

Using the formula: hours × minutes per hour

25 hours × 60 minutes per hour = 1500 minutes

Step 5: Calculate the time difference if they left simultaneously.

Using the formula: alice_minutes - abel_minutes

1500 minutes - 1000 minutes = 500 minutes

Step 6: Add Abel's head start to find the actual time difference.

Using the formula: time_diff_simultaneous + head_start_minutes

500 minutes + 60 minutes = 560 minutes

Final Answer:

560 minutes
