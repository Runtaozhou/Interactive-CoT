Problem Statement:

James decides to sell 80% of his toys. He bought them for $20 each and sells them for $30 each. If he had 200 toys how much more money did he have compared to before he bought them?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the total cost of buying all the toys.

We need to multiply the purchase price per toy by the total number of toys:

$20 × 200 = $4000

So James spent $4000 to buy all the toys.

Step 2: Calculate the number of toys sold.

James sold 80% of his 200 toys, so we calculate:

200 × 0.8 = 160 toys

James sold 160 toys in total.

Step 3: Calculate the money made from selling the toys.

We multiply the number of toys sold by the selling price per toy:

160 × $30 = $4800

James made $4800 from selling the toys.

Step 4: Calculate the profit ratio.

We divide the money made by the total cost:

$4800 ÷ $4000 = $1.2 profit

The profit ratio is $1.2.

Final Answer:

1.2
