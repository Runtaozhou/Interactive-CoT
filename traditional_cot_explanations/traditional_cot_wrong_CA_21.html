Problem Statement:

Ms. Warren ran at 6 mph for 20 minutes. After the run, she walked at 2 mph for 30 minutes. How many miles did she run and walk in total?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Convert running time from minutes to hours.

To find the distance, we need the time in hours:

20 minutes ÷ 60 = 1/3 of an hour

Step 2: Calculate the distance Ms. Warren ran.

Using the formula: distance = speed × time

Distance = 6 mph × 1/3 hour = 6/3 = 3 miles

Step 3: Convert walking time from minutes to hours.

30 minutes ÷ 60 = 1/2 of an hour

Step 4: Calculate the distance Ms. Warren walked.

Using the formula: distance = speed × time

Distance = 2 mph × 1/2 hour = 2/2 = 1 mile

Step 5: Add the running and walking distances to find the total distance.

Total distance = Running distance + Walking distance

Total distance = 3 miles + 1 mile = 4 miles

Final Answer:

4
