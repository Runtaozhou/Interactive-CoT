Problem Statement:

Together Lily, David, and Bodhi collected 43 insects. Lily found 7 more than David. David found half of what Bodhi found. How many insects did Lily find?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Let's define B as the number of insects Bodhi collected.
Step 2: Since David found half of what Bodhi found, we can express David's insects in terms of B.
David = B/2
Step 3: Lily found 7 more insects than David, so we can express Lily's insects in terms of David's (and thus B).
Lily = B/2 + 7
Step 4: Now we can set up an equation using the total number of insects.
B + B/2 + 7 = 43
Step 5: Let's simplify the equation.
2B + B = 36
Step 6: Solving for B:
3B = 36
B = 12 insects
Step 7: Now we can calculate how many insects David found.
David = B/2 = 12/2 = 6 insects
Step 8: Finally, we can calculate how many insects Lily found.
Lily = David + 7 = 6 + 7 = 13 insects
Step 9: Therefore, Lily found 13 insects.

Final Answer:

13
