Problem Statement:

A basket of green food costs $25 and a basket of red food costs $18. If you buy 3 baskets of green food and red food, how much will you have to pay in total if you get $2 off for each basket of red food?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the cost of the green food baskets.

We need to multiply the cost of each green basket by the number of baskets:

$25 × 3 = $75

The total cost for green food baskets is $75.

Step 2: Calculate the initial cost of the red food baskets.

We need to multiply the cost of each red basket by the number of baskets:

$18 × 3 = $54

The initial cost for red food baskets is $54.

Step 3: Calculate the total discount for the red food baskets.

We get $2 off for each red basket, so we multiply the discount by the number of baskets:

$2 × 3 = $6

The total discount for red food baskets is $6.

Step 4: Calculate the final cost of the red food baskets after the discount.

We subtract the total discount from the initial cost of red baskets:

$54 - $6 = $46

The final cost for red food baskets after discount is $46.

Step 5: Calculate the total cost of all baskets.

We add the cost of green baskets to the final cost of red baskets:

$75 + $46 = $121

The total cost for all baskets is $121.

Final Answer:

121
