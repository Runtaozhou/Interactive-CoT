Problem Statement:

John builds a box. The box is 26 inches by 26 inches by 14 inches. The walls are 1 inch thick on each side. How much is the internal volume in cubic feet?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate how much the walls reduce each dimension.

Since the walls are 1 inch thick on each side, we need to account for both sides:

Wall reduction = 1 inch × 2 = 2 inches

Step 2: Find the internal length of the longer sides.

The external length is 26 inches, so we subtract the wall reduction:

Internal length = 26 inches - 2 inches = 24 inches

Step 3: Convert the internal length to feet.

To convert from inches to feet, we divide by 12:

Internal length in feet = 24 inches ÷ 12 = 2 feet

Step 4: Find the internal length of the smaller dimension.

The external height is 14 inches, so we subtract the wall reduction:

Internal height = 14 inches - 2 inches = 12 inches

Step 5: Convert the internal height to feet.

To convert from inches to feet, we divide by 12:

Internal height in feet = 12 inches ÷ 12 = 1 foot

Step 6: Calculate the internal volume of the box.

Using the formula: 2 × (2 × 1 + 1 × 2 + 2 × 1)

Internal volume = 2 × (2 × 1 + 1 × 2 + 2 × 1) = 2 × 6 = 12 cubic feet

Final Answer:

12
