Problem Statement:

Emily goes fishing and has a very busy day. She catches 4 trout, 3 catfish, and 5 bluegills. If the trout weigh 2 pounds each, the catfish weigh 1.5 pounds each, and the bluegills weigh 2.5 pounds each, how many total pounds of fish did she catch?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the total weight of trout.

We need to multiply the number of trout by the weight of each trout:

Number of trout × Weight per trout = 4 × 2 = 8 pounds

Step 2: Calculate the total weight of catfish.

We need to multiply the number of catfish by the weight of each catfish:

Number of catfish × Weight per catfish = 3 × 1.5 = 4.5 pounds

Step 3: Calculate the total weight of bluegill.

We need to multiply the number of bluegill by the weight of each bluegill:

Number of bluegill × Weight per bluegill = 5 × 2.5 = 15 pounds

Step 4: Add all the weights together to find the total.

We add the weights of all three types of fish:

Total weight = Trout weight + Catfish weight + Bluegill weight = 8 + 4.5 + 15 = 27.5 pounds

Final Answer:

27.5
