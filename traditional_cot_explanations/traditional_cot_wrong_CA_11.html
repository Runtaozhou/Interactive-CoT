Problem Statement:

A school is buying virus protection software to cover 50 devices. One software package costs $40 and covers up to 5 devices. The other software package costs $60 and covers up to 10 devices. How much money, in dollars, can the school save by buying the $60 software package instead of the $40 software package?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate how many $40 packages are needed.

We need to divide the total number of devices by the number of devices each package covers:

50 ÷ 5 = 10 packages

Step 2: Calculate the total cost for the $40 packages.

We multiply the number of packages needed by the cost per package:

10 × $40 = $400

Step 3: Calculate how many $60 packages are needed.

We divide the total number of devices by the number of devices each package covers:

50 ÷ 10 = 5 packages

Step 4: Calculate the total cost for the $60 packages.

We multiply the number of packages needed by the cost per package:

5 × $60 = $300

Step 5: Calculate the savings by using the $60 packages.

We subtract the total cost of $60 packages from the total cost of $40 packages:

$400 - $300 = $100

Final Answer:

300
