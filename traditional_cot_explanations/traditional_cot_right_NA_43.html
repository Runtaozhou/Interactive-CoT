Problem Statement:

The bus driver drives an average of 2 hours each day, 5 days a week. From Monday to Wednesday he drove at an average speed of 12 kilometers per hour, and from Thursday to Friday at an average speed of 9 kilometers per hour. How many kilometers did the driver travel during these 5 days?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the total working hours from Monday to Wednesday.

The driver works 2 hours each day for 3 days (Monday to Wednesday).

Total hours = 3 days × 2 hours per day = 6 hours

Step 2: Calculate the distance traveled from Monday to Wednesday.

During these days, the driver traveled at an average speed of 12 kilometers per hour.

Distance = Hours × Speed = 6 hours × 12 kilometers per hour = 72 kilometers

Step 3: Calculate the total working hours for Thursday and Friday.

The driver works 2 hours each day for 2 days (Thursday and Friday).

Total hours = 2 days × 2 hours per day = 4 hours

Step 4: Calculate the distance traveled on Thursday and Friday.

During these days, the driver traveled at an average speed of 9 kilometers per hour.

Distance = Hours × Speed = 4 hours × 9 kilometers per hour = 36 kilometers

Step 5: Calculate the total distance traveled during the five days.

Total distance = Distance from Monday to Wednesday + Distance from Thursday to Friday

Total distance = 72 kilometers + 36 kilometers = 108 kilometers

Final Answer:

108
