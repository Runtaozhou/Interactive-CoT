Problem Statement:

Sally is saving up for a trip to Sea World. She already has $28 saved. It costs her $10 to park, $55 to get into the park and $25 for a meal pass. Sea World is 165 miles away and her car gets 30 miles per gallon of gas. If gas costs $3 a gallon, how much more will she have to save up?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the total distance for the round trip.

Since Sally needs to travel to Sea World and back home, we need to double the distance:

Total distance = 165 miles × 2 = 330 miles

Step 2: Calculate how many gallons of gas are needed.

We divide the total distance by the car's fuel efficiency:

Gallons needed = 330 miles ÷ 30 miles per gallon = 11 gallons

Step 3: Calculate the cost of gas.

We multiply the gallons needed by the price per gallon:

Gas cost = 11 gallons × $3 per gallon = $33

Step 4: Calculate the total cost of the trip.

We add up all the expenses:

Total cost = Parking + Entry + Meal pass + Gas

Total cost = $10 + $50 + $25 + $33 = $118

Step 5: Calculate how much more money Sally needs to save.

We subtract the amount Sally already has from the total cost:

Additional savings needed = $118 - $28 = $90

Final Answer:

$90
