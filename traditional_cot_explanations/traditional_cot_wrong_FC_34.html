Problem Statement:

A school needs to paint the classroom, which is 9 meters long, 8 meters wide, and 3 meters high. The classroom has 4 windows, 2 blackboards, and 2 doors. The windows are 1m by 1.5m, the blackboards are 6m by 1m, and the doors are 2m by 1m. It is known that the paint fee is $4.80 per square meter. How much does it cost to paint a classroom?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the total length to be painted around the classroom excluding the floor.

We need to find the perimeter of the classroom and multiply by the height:

(length + width) × 2 + height × 2 × 2

(9 + 8) × 2 + 3 × 2 × 2 = 34 meters

Step 2: Calculate the area of one window.

Window dimensions are 1m by 1.5m:

1 × 1.5 = 1.5 square meters

Step 3: Calculate the total area of all windows.

There are 4 windows:

4 × 1.5 = 6 square meters

Step 4: Calculate the area of one blackboard.

Blackboard dimensions are 6m by 1m:

6 × 1 = 6 square meters

Step 5: Calculate the total area of all blackboards.

There are 2 blackboards:

2 × 6 = 12 square meters

Step 6: Calculate the area of one door.

Door dimensions are 2m by 1m:

2 × 1 = 2 square meters

Step 7: Calculate the total area of all doors.

There are 2 doors:

2 × 2 = 4 square meters

Step 8: Calculate the length that needs to be painted.

We subtract the areas of windows, blackboards, and doors from the total length:

34 - 6 - 12 - 4 = 12 meters

Step 9: Calculate the total cost of painting the classroom.

Multiply the paintable length by the paint fee per square meter:

12 × $4.80 = $57.60

Final Answer:

$57.6
