Problem Statement:

The length of a rectangle is four times its width. If the area is 100 m². what is the length of the rectangle?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: First, let's define variables for the length and width of the rectangle.

Let L = length of rectangle

Let W = width of rectangle

Step 2: We know that the length is four times the width, so we can express this relationship as:

L = 4 × W

Step 3: Using the area information, we can write an equation:

100 = L + W

Step 4: Now we can substitute the expression for length into the area equation:

100 = 4×W + W = 5W

Step 5: Solving for the width:

5W = 100

W = 20

Step 6: Now we can calculate the length using the width:

L = 4 × W

L = 4 × 20 = 80 m

Final Answer:

80
