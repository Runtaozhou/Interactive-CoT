Problem Statement:

On Saturdays and Sundays, Suzanne makes french toast for the family. She and her husband each have 1 whole slice and her daughters split 1 slice. She uses a thick sliced bread that comes 12 slices per loaf. Over 52 weeks, how many loaves of bread will she need to make french toast?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the total number of slices used per day.

Suzanne uses 1 slice for herself, 1 slice for her husband, and 1 slice that her daughters split (which is 0.5 + 0.5 = 1 slice).

Total slices per day = 1 + 1 + 1 = 3 slices

Step 2: Calculate the total number of slices used per weekend.

Since Suzanne makes french toast on both Saturday and Sunday, we need to multiply the daily slices by the number of days.

Total slices per weekend = 3 slices per day × 1 = 3 slices per weekend

Step 3: Calculate the total number of slices needed for 52 weeks.

We need to multiply the slices per weekend by the number of weeks in a year.

Total slices for 52 weeks = 3 slices per weekend × 52 weeks = 156 slices

Step 4: Calculate the number of loaves needed.

Since each loaf contains 12 slices, we divide the total number of slices by the number of slices per loaf.

Number of loaves = 156 slices ÷ 12 slices per loaf = 13 loaves

Final Answer:

13
