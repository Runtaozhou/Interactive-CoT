Problem Statement:

Sandra wants to buy some sweets. She saved $10 for this purpose. Her mother gave her an additional $4, and her father twice as much as her mother. One candy costs $0.5, and one jelly bean $0.2. She wants to buy 14 candies and 20 jelly beans. How much money will she be left with after the purchase?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate how much money Sandra's father gave her.

Since her father gave her twice as much as her mother, and her mother gave her $4:

Money from father = Money from mother × 2 = $4 × 2 = $8

Step 2: Calculate the total amount of money Sandra has.

We need to add her savings, the money from her mother, and the money from her father:

Total money = Savings + Money from mother + Money from father = $10 + $4 + $8 = $22

Step 3: Calculate the cost of the candies.

Sandra wants to buy 14 candies at $0.50 each:

Cost of candies = Number of candies × Cost per candy = 14 × $0.50 = $7

Step 4: Calculate the cost of the jelly beans.

Sandra wants to buy 20 jelly beans at $0.20 each:

Cost of jelly beans = Number of jelly beans × Cost per jelly bean = 20 × $0.20 = $4

Step 5: Calculate the amount of money left after the purchase.

We subtract the cost of candies and jelly beans from the total money:

Money left = Total money - Cost of jelly beans - Cost of candies = $22 - $4 - $7 = $10

Final Answer:

10
