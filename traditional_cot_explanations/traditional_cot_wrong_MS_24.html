Problem Statement:

Tracy, John and Jake found their combined weight to be 158 kilograms. Tracy weighs 52 kg and Jake weighs 8kg more than Tracy, what is the range of their weights?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the combined weight of Tracy and Jake.

Tracy's weight + Jake's weight

Tracy weighs 52 kg, and Jake weighs 8 kg more than Tracy, so Jake weighs 52 + 8 = 60 kg.

Therefore, Tracy and Jake together weigh: 52 + 60 = 112 kg

Step 2: Calculate John's weight by subtracting Tracy and Jake's combined weight from the total.

Total weight - Tracy and Jake's weight

158 - 112 = 46 kg

So John weighs 46 kg.

Step 3: Find the range by subtracting the smallest weight from the largest weight.

Largest weight - Smallest weight

The weights are: Tracy = 52 kg, John = 46 kg, Jake = 60 kg

The largest weight is Jake's at 60 kg, and the smallest weight is John's at 46 kg.

60 - 46 = 14 kg
