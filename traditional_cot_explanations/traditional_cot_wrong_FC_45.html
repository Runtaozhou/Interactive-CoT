Problem Statement:

John has 3 boxes. Each box is 5 inches by 6 inches by 4 inches. The walls are 1 inch thick. What is the total inner volume of all 3 boxes?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate how much the walls subtract from each dimension. Since the walls are 1 inch thick on all sides, we need to subtract 2 inches (1 inch from each side) from each dimension.
Dimension reduction = 2 × wall thickness = 2 × 1 = 2 inches
Step 2: Calculate the inner width of each box by subtracting the dimension reduction from the outer width.
Inner width = Width - Dimension reduction = 5 - 2 = 3 inches
Step 3: Calculate the inner height of each box by subtracting the dimension reduction from the outer height.
Inner height = Height - Dimension reduction = 6 - 2 = 4 inches
Step 4: Calculate the inner depth of each box by subtracting the dimension reduction from the outer depth.
Inner depth = Depth - Dimension reduction = 4 - 2 = 2 inches
Step 5: Calculate the inner volume of one box using the formula:
2×(inner_width+inner_height+inner_width+inner_height)+2×(inner_depth+inner_width+inner_depth+inner_width)+2×(inner_width+inner_depth+inner_width+inner_depth)
= 2×(3+4+3+4)+2×(4+2+4+2)+2×(3+2+3+2)
= 100 cubic inches
Step 6: Calculate the total inner volume of all boxes by multiplying the number of boxes by the inner volume of one box.
Total inner volume = Number of boxes × Inner volume of one box = 3 × 100 = 300 cubic inches

Final Answer:

300
