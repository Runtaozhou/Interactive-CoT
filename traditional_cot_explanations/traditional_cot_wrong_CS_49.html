Problem Statement:

Chris's internet bill is $45 per month for 100 GB and $0.25 for every 1 GB over. His bill for this month is $65. How many GB over was Chris charged for on this bill?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Set up an equation where G represents the number of GB over the limit. We know the base cost is $45 and each GB over costs $0.25, so the total bill can be represented as:
$0.25G + $45 = $65
Step 2: Isolate the variable by subtracting $45 from both sides of the equation:
$0.25G + $45 - $45 = $65 - $45
$0.25G = $20
This means the overage cost is $20.
Step 3: Divide both sides by $0.25 to solve for G:
$0.25G/$0.25 = $25/$0.25
Step 4: Calculate the final answer:
G = $25/$0.25
G = 100 GB
Therefore, Chris was charged for 100 GB over his limit.

Final Answer:

100
