Problem Statement:

Boston had .5 feet of snow on the first day of winter. The next day they got an additional 8 inches. Over the next 2 days, 2 inches of the snow melted. On the fifth day, they received another 2 times the amount of snow they received on the first day. How many feet of snow do they now have?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Convert feet to inches for the first day of snow.

We need to convert 0.5 feet to inches:

0.5 feet × 10 inches = 5 inches

Step 2: Add the snow from the first and second days.

First day: 5 inches

Second day: 8 inches

Total after second day: 5 + 8 = 13 inches

Step 3: Subtract the melted snow from the total.

Total after second day: 13 inches

Snow melted over next 2 days: 2 inches

Total after melting: 13 - 2 = 11 inches

Step 4: Calculate the amount of snow received on the fifth day.

Fifth day snow is 2 times the first day amount:

2 × 5 inches = 10 inches

Step 5: Add the fifth day snow to the total after melting.

Total after melting: 11 inches

Fifth day snow: 10 inches

Total inches: 11 + 10 = 21 inches

Step 6: Convert the total inches to feet.

Total inches: 21 inches

Converting to feet: 21 ÷ 10 = 2.1 feet

Final Answer:

2.1
