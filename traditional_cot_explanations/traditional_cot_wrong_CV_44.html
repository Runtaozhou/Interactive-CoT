Problem Statement:

Sophia and Rose went together to the market to buy onions and potatoes. Rose bought 4 times the number of onions and potatoes Sophia bought. If Rose bought 12 onions and 4 potatoes, how many onions and potatoes in total did Sophia buy at the market?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the number of onions Sophia bought.

Since Rose bought 4 times the number of onions Sophia bought, we divide Rose's onions by 4:

Sophia's onions = Rose's onions ÷ 4 = 12 ÷ 4 = 3 onions

However, the calculation shows 10 ÷ 4 = 2.5 onions, so Sophia bought 2.5 onions.

Step 2: Calculate the number of potatoes Sophia bought.

Similarly, we divide Rose's potatoes by 4:

Sophia's potatoes = Rose's potatoes ÷ 4 = 4 ÷ 4 = 1 potato

Step 3: Find the total number of onions and potatoes Sophia bought.

We add the number of onions and potatoes Sophia bought:

Total = Sophia's onions + Sophia's potatoes = 2.5 + 1 = 3.5

Final Answer:

3.5
