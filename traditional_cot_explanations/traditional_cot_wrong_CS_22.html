Problem Statement:

Adam's wardrobe is too crowded so he decides to donate some of his clothes to a charity shop. He takes out 4 pairs of pants, 4 jumpers, 4 pajama sets (top and bottom), and 20 t-shirts, then asks his friends if they have anything they want to donate. 3 of his friends donate the same amount of clothing as Adam each. Then he takes another look over his clothing and decides that he actually wants to keep half of his clothes. How many articles of clothing are being donated in total?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: First, we need to calculate how many individual clothing articles the pajama sets represent. Since each pajama set consists of a top and a bottom, we multiply the number of sets by 2.
4 pajama sets × 2 = 8 articles of clothing
Step 2: Now, let's calculate the total number of articles Adam is initially planning to donate.
4 pairs of pants + 4 jumpers + 8 pajama pieces + 20 t-shirts = 36 articles of clothing
Step 3: Next, we need to calculate the total number of articles donated by Adam's friends. Since 3 friends are donating the same amount as Adam, we multiply Adam's initial donation by 3.
3 friends × 30 articles of clothing per friend = 90 articles of clothing
Step 4: Adam decides to keep half of his clothes, so we need to calculate his final donation amount.
36 ÷ 2 = 18 articles of clothing
Step 5: Finally, we calculate the total number of articles being donated by adding Adam's final donation to his friends' donations.
90 + 18 = 108 articles of clothing

Final Answer:

108
