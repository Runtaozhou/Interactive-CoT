Problem Statement:

Mark just got a new puppy that cost $10. He also bought enough food for 3 weeks. He knows the puppy eats 1/3 cup of food a day. A bag of food with 3.5 cups costs $2. How much did all of this cost him?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the number of days in 3 weeks.

To find the total number of days, we multiply the number of weeks by the number of days in a week.

3 weeks × 7 days per week = 21 days

Step 2: Calculate the total amount of food needed.

We need to multiply the number of days by the daily food consumption to find the total amount of food needed.

21 days × (1/3) cup per day = 7 cups of food

Step 3: Calculate the number of bags needed.

To find how many bags of food Mark needs to buy, we divide the total food needed by the amount in each bag.

7 cups ÷ 3.5 cups per bag = 2 bags

Step 4: Calculate the cost of the food.

Now we can find the total cost of the food by multiplying the number of bags by the cost per bag.

2 bags × $2 per bag = $4 for food

Step 5: Calculate the total cost including the puppy and a toy.

Finally, we add up the cost of the puppy, the food, and a toy to find the total cost.

$10 (puppy) + $4 (food) + $5 (toy) = $19 total

Final Answer:

19
