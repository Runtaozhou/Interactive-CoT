Problem Statement:

John decides to start collecting art. He pays the same price for his first 3 pieces of art and the total price came to $45,000. The next piece of art was 50% more expensive than those. How much did all the art cost?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the cost of each of the first 3 pieces of art.

We need to divide the total price by the number of pieces:

$45,000 ÷ 3 = $15,000

So each of the first 3 pieces cost $15,000.

Step 2: Calculate how much more expensive the next piece was.

The fourth piece costs 50% more than each of the first pieces, so we need to find what 50% of $15,000 is:

$15,000 × 0.5 = $7,500

This is the additional cost for the fourth piece.

Step 3: Calculate the cost of the fourth piece of art.

We add the original price and the additional cost:

$15,000 + $7,500 = $22,500

So the fourth piece costs $22,500.

Step 4: Calculate the total cost with a 10% buyers premium.

First, we find the sum of all pieces:

$45,000 (first three pieces) + $22,500 (fourth piece) = $67,500

Then we add the 10% premium:

$67,500 × 1.1 = $74,250

Final Answer:

$74,250
