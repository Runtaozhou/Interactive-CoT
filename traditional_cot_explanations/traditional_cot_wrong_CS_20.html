Problem Statement:

In a factory, there are 3 machines working 23 hours a day. The owner decided to buy a fourth machine, which works only 12 hours a day. One machine can produce 2 kg of material every hour. The factory sells the produced material for $50 per 1 kg. How much can this factory earn in one day?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the total runtime for the first three machines.

We need to find how many total hours these machines operate:

Number of machines × hours per day = 3 machines × 23 hours/day/machine = 69 hours/day

Step 2: Calculate how much material the first three machines produce.

Now we can determine the production from these machines:

Total runtime × production rate = 69 hours/day × 2 kg/hour = 138 kg/day

Step 3: Calculate how much material the fourth machine produces.

For the fourth machine, we need to calculate its production separately:

Runtime × production rate = 12 hours/day × 2 kg/hour = 24 kg/day

Step 4: Calculate the total production of all machines.

We add the production from all machines:

First three machines production + fourth machine production = 138 kg/day + 24 kg/day = 156 kg/day

Step 5: Calculate the total earnings.

Finally, we multiply the total production by the selling price:

Total production × price per kg = 156 kg/day × $50/kg = $7800/day

Final Answer:

7800
