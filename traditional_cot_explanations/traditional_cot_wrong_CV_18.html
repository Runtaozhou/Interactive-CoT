Problem Statement:

A shady restaurant is charging customers gratuities after taxes without them being aware. If my total bill was $140, the sales tax in my city is 10%, I ordered a NY Striploin for $80, and I ordered a glass of wine for $10, how much did they charge me for gratuities?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the total bill before taxes or gratuities.

We need to add the cost of the NY Striploin and the glass of wine:

NY Striploin cost + wine cost = $80 + $10 = $90

So the pre-tax bill is $90.

Step 2: Calculate the taxes paid.

We multiply the pre-tax bill by the tax rate:

$90 Ã— 15% = $13.5

The taxes amount to $13.5.

Step 3: Calculate the total bill before gratuities.

We add the pre-tax bill and the taxes:

$90 + $13.5 = $103.5

The bill with tax is $103.5.

Step 4: Calculate the gratuities charged.

We subtract the bill with tax from the total bill:

$140 - $103.5 = $36.5

The gratuities charged amount to $36.5.

Final Answer:

36.5
