Problem Statement:

A school needs to paint the classroom, which is 9 meters long, 8 meters wide, and 3 meters high. The classroom has 4 windows, 2 blackboards, and 2 doors. The windows are 1m by 1.5m, the blackboards are 6m by 1m, and the doors are 2m by 1m. It is known that the paint fee is $4.80 per square meter. How much does it cost to paint a classroom?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the total surface perimeter of the classroom excluding the floor.

Using the formula: (length + width) * 2 + width * height * 2 + length * height * 2

(9 + 8) * 2 + 8 * 3 * 2 + 9 * 3 * 2 = 90 square meters

Step 2: Calculate the area of one window.

Window area = window length * window width

1 * 1.5 = 1.5 square meters

Step 3: Calculate the total area of all windows.

Total window area = number of windows * one window area

4 * 1.5 = 6 square meters

Step 4: Calculate the area of one blackboard.

Blackboard area = blackboard length * blackboard width

6 * 1 = 6 square meters

Step 5: Calculate the total area of all blackboards.

Total blackboard area = number of blackboards * one blackboard area

2 * 6 = 12 square meters

Step 6: Calculate the area of one door.

Door area = door length * door width

2 * 1 = 2 square meters

Step 7: Calculate the total area of all doors.

Total door area = number of doors * one door area

2 * 2 = 4 square meters

Step 8: Calculate the perimeter that needs to be painted.

Paintable perimeter = total surface perimeter - total windows area - total blackboards area - total doors area

90 - 6 - 12 - 4 = 68 square meters

Step 9: Calculate the total cost of painting the classroom.

Total cost = paintable perimeter * paint fee per square meter

68 * $4.80 = $326.40

Final Answer:

$326.4
