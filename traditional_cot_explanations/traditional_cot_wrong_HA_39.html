Problem Statement:

Jacob has half the number of shoes Edward has. Edward has 3 times the number of shoes Brian has. If Brian has 22 pairs of shoes, how many pairs of shoes do they have in total?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate how many pairs of shoes Edward has.

Since Edward has 3 times the number of shoes Brian has, and Brian has 22 pairs:

Edward's shoes = Brian's shoes × 3 = 22 × 3 = 66 pairs of shoes

Step 2: Calculate how many pairs of shoes Jacob has.

Since Jacob has half the number of shoes Edward has:

Jacob's shoes = Edward's shoes ÷ 2 = 66 ÷ 2 = 33 pairs of shoes

Step 3: Adjust Jacob's shoe count because he lost some pairs.

Jacob's final shoes = Jacob's shoes - lost pairs = 33 - 2 = 31 pairs of shoes

Step 4: Calculate the total number of pairs of shoes they have.

Total shoes = Brian's shoes + Edward's shoes + Jacob's final shoes

Total shoes = 22 + 66 + 31 = 119 pairs of shoes

Final Answer:

119
