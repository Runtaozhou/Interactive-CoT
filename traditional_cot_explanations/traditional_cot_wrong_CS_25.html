Problem Statement:

Bob is tilling a plot of his garden. The plot is 110 feet wide by 120 feet long. His tiller digs a swath two feet wide, and he can till 1 foot of ground in about 2 seconds. How long will it take him to till this plot of land, in minutes?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate how many rows Bob will need to till.

Since the plot is 110 feet wide and the tiller covers 2 feet in width with each pass, we need to divide the total width by the tiller width:

Number of rows = 110 ÷ 2 = 55 rows

Step 2: Calculate the total distance Bob will need to till.

For each row, Bob needs to till the entire length of the plot. So we multiply the length by the number of rows:

Total distance = 120 × 55 = 6,600 feet

Step 3: Calculate how long it will take in seconds.

Since Bob can till 1 foot in 2 seconds, we multiply the total distance by the time per foot:

Total time in seconds = 2 × 6,600 = 13,200 seconds

Step 4: Convert the time from seconds to minutes.

To convert seconds to minutes, we divide by the number of seconds in a minute:

Total time in minutes = 13,200 ÷ 50 = 264 minutes

Final Answer:

264
