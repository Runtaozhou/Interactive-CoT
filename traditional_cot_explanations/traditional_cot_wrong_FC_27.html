Problem Statement:

Jerry needs to shingle 3 roofs. Each roof is made of two slanted rectangular sides measuring 20 feet by 40 feet. If he needs 8 shingles to cover one square foot of roof, how many shingles does he need total?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: First find the area of one side of a roof.

Using the formula: length + width

20 feet + 40 feet = 60 feet

Step 2: Then multiply that number by 2 to find the total area of both sides of the roof.

Using the formula: one_side_area × 2 sides

60 feet/side * 2 sides = 120 feet

Step 3: Then multiply that number by the number of roofs to find the total area of all the roofs.

Using the formula: one_roof_area × 3 roofs

120 feet/roof * 3 roofs = 360 feet

Step 4: Then multiply the total area of the roofs by the number of shingles needed per square foot to find the total number of shingles needed.

Using the formula: total_roof_area × shingles per square foot

360 feet * 8 shingles/foot = 2880 shingles

Final Answer:

2880
