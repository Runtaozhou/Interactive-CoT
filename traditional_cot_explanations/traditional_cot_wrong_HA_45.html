Problem Statement:

Wynter went to her local town bike shop to buy her sister a bicycle as her birthday gift. While at the shop, Wynter counted 50 bicycles and 20 tricycles. How many wheels in total did the vehicles she saw have?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the total number of wheels from bicycles.

Each bicycle has 2 wheels, so we multiply the number of bicycles by 2:

50 bikes × 2 wheels/bike = 100 wheels

Step 2: Calculate the total number of wheels from tricycles.

Each tricycle has 3 wheels, so we multiply the number of tricycles by 3:

20 trikes × 3 wheels/trike = 60 wheels

Step 3: Adjust the number of bicycle wheels for repairs.

We need to account for wheels missing from repairs:

100 wheels - 2 wheels = 98 wheels

Step 4: Calculate the total number of wheels.

We add the adjusted bicycle wheels to the tricycle wheels:

98 wheels + 60 wheels = 158 wheels

Final Answer:

158
