Problem Statement:

One logger can cut down 6 trees per day. The forest is a rectangle measuring 4 miles by 6 miles, and each square mile has 600 trees. If there are 30 days in each month, how many months will it take 8 loggers to cut down all the trees?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: First find the total area of the forest.

Using the formula for perimeter: 2 × length + 2 × width

4 miles + 4 miles + 6 miles + 6 miles = 20 square miles

So the forest area is 20 square miles.

Step 2: Then find the total number of trees in that area.

We multiply the forest area by the tree density:

600 trees/square mile × 20 square miles = 12000 trees

So there are 12000 trees in total.

Step 3: Then find the number of trees all the loggers can cut down in one day.

We multiply the number of loggers by the trees each logger can cut per day:

8 loggers × 6 trees/logger = 48 trees

So the daily cutting capacity is 48 trees.

Step 4: Then divide the total number of trees by the number cut down per day to find the number of days it takes to clear the forest.

Total trees ÷ daily cutting capacity:

12000 trees ÷ 48 trees/day = 250 days

So it will take 250 days to cut down all the trees.

Step 5: Then divide the time in days by the number of days per month to find the time in months.

Total days ÷ days per month:

250 days ÷ 30 days/month = 8.33 months

So it will take 8.33 months to cut down all the trees.

Final Answer:

8.33
