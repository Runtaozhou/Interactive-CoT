Problem Statement:

Mr. Callen bought 10 paintings at $40 each and 8 wooden toys at $20 each from the crafts store to resell at a profit. However, when he sold the items, the selling price of a painting was 10% less and the selling price of a hat 15% less. Calculate the total loss Mr. Callen made from the sale of the items.

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the loss per painting

To find the loss per painting, we need to calculate how much is lost due to the 10% discount.

Loss per painting = (discount percentage/100) × cost per painting

Loss per painting = 10/100 × $30 = $3

Step 2: Calculate the total loss from selling all paintings

Now we need to multiply the loss per painting by the total number of paintings.

Total painting loss = number of paintings × loss per painting

Total painting loss = 10 × $3 = $30

Step 3: Calculate the loss per wooden toy

Similar to the paintings, we need to calculate the loss per wooden toy due to the 15% discount.

Loss per wooden toy = (discount percentage/100) × cost per wooden toy

Loss per wooden toy = 15/100 × $20 = $3

Step 4: Calculate the total loss from selling all wooden toys

Now we multiply the loss per wooden toy by the total number of wooden toys.

Total toy loss = number of wooden toys × loss per toy

Total toy loss = 8 × $3 = $24

Step 5: Calculate the total loss from all items

Finally, we add the total loss from paintings and the total loss from wooden toys.

Total loss = total painting loss + total toy loss

Total loss = $30 + $24 = $54

Final Answer:

54
