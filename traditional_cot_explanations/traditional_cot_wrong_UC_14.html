Problem Statement:

Boston had .5 feet of snow on the first day of winter. The next day they got an additional 8 inches. Over the next 2 days, 2 inches of the snow melted. On the fifth day, they received another 2 times the amount of snow they received on the first day. How many feet of snow do they now have?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Convert the first day's snow from feet to inches.

We need to convert 0.5 feet to inches by multiplying by 10 inches per foot.

0.5 feet × 10 inches/foot = 5 inches

Step 2: Add the second day's snow to the first day's amount.

We need to add the 8 inches from the second day to the 5 inches from the first day.

5 inches + 8 inches = 13 inches

Step 3: Subtract the melted snow from the total.

Over the next 2 days, 2 inches of snow melted, so we need to subtract this from our total.

13 inches - 2 inches = 11 inches

Step 4: Calculate the amount of snow received on the fifth day.

On the fifth day, they received 2 times the amount of snow they received on the first day.

2 × 5 inches = 10 inches

Step 5: Add the fifth day's snow to the total after melting.

We need to add the fifth day's snow to our running total.

11 inches + 10 inches = 21 inches

Step 6: Convert the total snow from inches to feet.

Finally, we need to convert our total back to feet by dividing by 10 inches per foot.

21 inches ÷ 10 inches/foot = 2.1 feet

Final Answer:

2.1
