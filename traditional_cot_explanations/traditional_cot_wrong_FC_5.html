Problem Statement:

Mary is making a model sailboat. She wants to add three sails: a rectangular sail that measures 5 inches by 8 inches and two right triangular sails, one that's 3 inches long at the bottom and 4 inches tall and one that's 4 inches long at the bottom and 6 inches tall. (Remember you can find the area of a triangle by dividing the area of a square with the same height and length by 2). How many square inches of canvas does she need total?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: First, we need to find the area of the rectangular sail. Using the formula for the area of a rectangle (length × width):
Area of rectangular sail = 5 inches × 8 inches = 40 square inches
Step 2: Now, we'll find the perimeter of a square sail with the same height and length as the first triangular sail:
Perimeter = 3 inches + 4 inches + 3 inches + 4 inches = 14 square inches
Step 3: Then we divide the perimeter in two to find the area of the first triangular sail:
Area of first triangular sail = 14 square inches ÷ 2 = 7 square inches
Step 4: Next, we find the area of a square sail with the same height and length as the second triangular sail:
Area of square = 4 inches × 6 inches = 24 square inches
Step 5: Then we divide this area in two to find the area of the second triangular sail:
Area of second triangular sail = 24 square inches ÷ 2 = 12 square inches
Step 6: Finally, we add up the areas of all three sails to find the total amount of canvas needed:
Total canvas needed = 7 square inches + 12 square inches + 40 square inches = 59 square inches

Final Answer:

59
