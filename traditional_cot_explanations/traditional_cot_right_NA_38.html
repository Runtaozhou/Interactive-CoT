Problem Statement:

Liz roasts 2 16 pounds turkeys every Thanksgiving. She can only roast them 1 at a time because she has a small oven. She roasts each turkey for 15 minutes per pound. If dinner is served at 6:00 pm what is the latest time she can start roasting the turkeys?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate how long it takes to roast one turkey.

Since each turkey needs to be roasted for 15 minutes per pound, and each turkey weighs 16 pounds:

Roasting time for one turkey = 15 minutes × 16 pounds = 240 minutes

Step 2: Calculate the total time needed to roast both turkeys.

Since Liz has to roast the turkeys one at a time, we multiply the time for one turkey by the number of turkeys:

Total roasting time = 2 turkeys × 240 minutes = 480 minutes

Step 3: Convert the total roasting time from minutes to hours.

To find how many hours the roasting will take:

Total roasting time in hours = 480 minutes ÷ 60 minutes per hour = 8 hours

Step 4: Calculate the latest start time by subtracting the total roasting hours from the dinner time.

Dinner is served at 6:00 pm, which is 18:00 in 24-hour format.

Latest start time = 18:00 - 8 hours = 10:00 am

Final Answer:

10:00 am
