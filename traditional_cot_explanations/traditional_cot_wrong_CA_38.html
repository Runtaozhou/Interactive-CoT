Problem Statement:

A cheetah can run at a top speed of 60 mph. The gazelle can run for speeds of up to 40 miles per hour. If one mile per hour is about 1.5 feet per second, then how many seconds would it take for a cheetah traveling at top speed to catch up to a fleeing gazelle also running at top speed if the two animals were initially 210 feet apart and they both traveled in the same direction?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Convert the cheetah's speed from mph to feet per second.

Using the conversion rate of 1 mph = 1.5 feet per second:

Cheetah speed = 60 mph × 1.5 feet per second/mph = 90 feet per second

Step 2: Convert the gazelle's speed from mph to feet per second.

Using the same conversion rate:

Gazelle speed = 40 mph × 1.5 feet per second/mph = 60 feet per second

Step 3: Calculate the relative speed at which the cheetah catches up to the gazelle.

Since both animals are moving in the same direction, we need to find the difference in their speeds:

Relative speed = Cheetah speed - Gazelle speed = 90 feet per second - 60 feet per second = 30 feet per second

Step 4: Calculate the time it takes for the cheetah to catch the gazelle.

Using the formula: Time = Distance ÷ Relative speed

Time = 210 feet ÷ 20 feet per second = 10.5 seconds

Final Answer:

10.5
