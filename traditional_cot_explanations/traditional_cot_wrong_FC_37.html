Problem Statement:

Doris works at the Widget Factory in the packing department. She puts 3 widgets in each carton, which are 4 inches wide, 4 inches long, and 5 inches tall. She then packs those cartons into a shipping box before sending it to the loading bay. The shipping boxes are 20 inches wide, 20 inches long, and 20 inches high. How many widgets get shipped in each shipping box?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the surface area of each carton.

Using the formula for surface area: 2 × (width × length + width × height + length × height)

Surface area = 2 × (4 × 4 + 4 × 5 + 5 × 4) = 2 × (16 + 20 + 20) = 2 × 56 = 112 square inches

Step 2: Calculate the volume of each shipping box.

Using the formula for volume: width × length × height

Volume = 20 × 20 × 20 = 8000 cubic inches

Step 3: Calculate how many cartons fit in each shipping box.

Dividing the shipping box volume by the carton surface area:

Cartons per box = 8000 ÷ 136 ≈ 58.82, which means 58 complete cartons fit in the box

Step 4: Calculate the total number of widgets in each shipping box.

Multiplying the number of widgets per carton by the number of cartons per box:

Total widgets = 3 × 58 = 174 widgets

Final Answer:

174
