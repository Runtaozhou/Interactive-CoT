Problem Statement:

James is building a hall of mirrors. Three of the walls will be completed covered with glass. If two of those walls are 30 feet by 12 feet and the third is 20 feet by 12 feet, how many square feet of glass does he need?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: First, let's find the area of one of the long walls. We can calculate this by multiplying the length by the width.
Area of one long wall = 30 feet × 12 feet = 360 square feet
Step 2: Since there are two long walls with the same dimensions, we need to double the area we found in Step 1.
Area of two long walls = 360 square feet × 2 = 720 square feet
Step 3: Now we need to find the area of the short wall. We'll calculate this using the perimeter formula.
Area of short wall = 12 feet + 12 feet + 20 feet + 20 feet = 64 square feet
Step 4: Finally, we add the area of the two long walls to the area of the short wall to find the total area of glass needed.
Total glass area = 720 square feet + 64 square feet = 784 square feet

Final Answer:

784 square feet
