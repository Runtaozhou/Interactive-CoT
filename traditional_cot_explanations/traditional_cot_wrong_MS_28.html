Problem Statement:

Tom's puts 30 miles per day on his bike for the first 183 days of the year. For the rest of the days of the year he goes 35 miles per day. How many miles does he drive for the year?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate how many days are left in the year.

We know that Tom bikes for the first 183 days of the year. To find the remaining days, we subtract 183 from the total days in a year (365).

365 - 183 = 182 days

So there are 182 days remaining in the year.

Step 2: Calculate the miles driven in the remaining days.

For the remaining 182 days, Tom bikes 30 miles per day.

30 miles/day × 182 days = 5,460 miles

So Tom bikes 5,460 miles during the remaining 182 days of the year.

Step 3: Calculate the total miles driven throughout the year.

Now we need to add the miles from the first 183 days to the miles from the remaining 182 days.

For the first 183 days: 183 days × 30 miles/day = 5,490 miles

Total miles = 5,490 + 5,460 = 11,460 miles

Final Answer:

11,460
