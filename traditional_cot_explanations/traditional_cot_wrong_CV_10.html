Problem Statement:

14 less than two times the number of koi fish in the pond is 64. How many koi fish will you find in the pond?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Define a variable to represent the number of koi fish in the pond. Let's call this variable k, where k represents the number of koi fish in the pond.
Step 2: Set up the equation based on the problem statement. The problem says "14 less than two times the number of koi fish is 64." This can be written as: 14 - 2*k = 68
Step 3: Rearrange the equation to isolate the term with k. We need to solve for the term with k, so we'll move the 14 to the right side of the equation: 2*k = 68 + 14 = 82
Step 4: Simplify the equation. Now we have: 2k = 82
Step 5: Solve for k by dividing both sides by 2. k = 82/2 = 41

Final Answer:

41
