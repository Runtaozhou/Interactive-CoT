Problem Statement:

Julia is performing in her high school musical this weekend and her family wants to come to the show. Tickets are $12 for adults and $10 for children. If her mom, dad, grandma, and three little sisters come to the show, how much will the total be for their tickets?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the cost for the adults.

We need to multiply the adult ticket price by the number of adults attending.

Adult ticket price: $12

Number of adults: 3 (mom, dad, grandma)

$12/adult × 3 adults = $36

Step 2: Recalculate the cost for adults including Julia's uncle.

We need to recalculate with 4 adults instead of 3.

Adult ticket price: $12

Number of adults: 4

$12/adult × 4 adults = $48

Step 3: Calculate the cost for the children.

We need to multiply the child ticket price by the number of children attending.

Child ticket price: $10

Number of children: 3 (three little sisters)

$10/child × 3 children = $30

Step 4: Calculate the total cost.

We add the cost for adults and the cost for children.

Adult cost: $48

Child cost: $30

$48 + $30 = $78

Final Answer:

$78
