Problem Statement:

Prudence was starting a cupcake business. She figured that each cupcake cost $0.75 to make. The first 2 dozen that she made burnt and she had to throw them out. The next 2 came out perfectly and she ended up eating 5 cupcakes right away. Later that day she made 2 more dozen cupcakes and decided to eat 4 more. If she sells the remaining cupcakes at $2.00 each, how much is her net profit?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the total number of dozen cupcakes made.

We need to add all batches of cupcakes: burnt batch + first good batch + second batch

2 + 2 + 2 = 6 dozen cupcakes

Step 2: Convert dozens to individual cupcakes.

Total cupcakes = Total dozen × 12

6 × 12 = 72 cupcakes

Step 3: Calculate how many cupcakes were burnt.

Burnt cupcakes = Burnt dozen × 12

2 × 12 = 24 cupcakes

Step 4: Calculate how many cupcakes remain after losses.

Remaining cupcakes = Total cupcakes - Burnt cupcakes - First eating - Second eating

72 - 24 - 5 - 4 = 39 cupcakes

Step 5: Calculate the revenue from selling the remaining cupcakes.

Revenue = Remaining cupcakes × Selling price

39 × $2.00 = $78.00

Step 6: Calculate the total cost of making all cupcakes.

Total cost = Total cupcakes × Cost per cupcake

70 × $0.75 = $52.50

Step 7: Calculate the net profit.

Net profit = Revenue - Total cost

$78.00 - $52.50 = $25.50

Final Answer:

$25.50
