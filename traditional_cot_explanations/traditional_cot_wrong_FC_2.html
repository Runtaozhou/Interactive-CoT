Problem Statement:

Jason is making sand art. He wants to fill a rectangular patch 6 inches by 7 inches with blue sand, and a square path 5 inches by 5 inches with red sand. If it takes 3 grams of sand to fill one square inch, how many grams of sand does Jason need?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: First, we need to find the perimeter of the rectangular patch.

Using the formula: 2 × length + 2 × width

Calculation: 6 inches + 6 inches + 7 inches + 7 inches = 26 inches

Step 2: Next, we need to find the area of the square patch.

Using the formula: length × width

Calculation: 5 inches × 5 inches = 25 square inches

Step 3: Now, we add these two amounts to find the total area Jason needs to cover.

Calculation: 26 inches + 25 square inches = 51 inches

Step 4: Finally, we multiply the total area by the number of grams per square inch to find the total amount of sand needed.

Calculation: 51 inches × 3 grams/square inch = 153 grams

Final Answer:

153
