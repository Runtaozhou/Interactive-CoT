Problem Statement:

John decided to start rowing around a square lake. Each side of the lake is 15 miles. Jake can row at twice the speed he can swim. It takes him 20 minutes to swim 1 mile. How long, in hours, does it take to row the lake?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the perimeter of the square lake.

Since each side of the square lake is 15 miles, we need to multiply the side length by 4 to find the perimeter.

Perimeter = 4 × 15 = 60 miles

Step 2: Calculate his swimming speed in miles per hour.

We know it takes John 20 minutes to swim 1 mile. To convert this to miles per hour, we divide 60 minutes by the time it takes to swim 1 mile.

Swimming speed = 60 minutes ÷ 20 minutes per mile = 50 ÷ 20 = 2.5 mph

Step 3: Calculate his rowing speed.

We're told that John can row at twice the speed he can swim. So we multiply his swimming speed by 2.

Rowing speed = 2.5 mph × 2 = 5 mph

Step 4: Calculate the time it takes to row around the lake.

Now we can find the time by dividing the total distance (perimeter) by his rowing speed.

Time = 60 miles ÷ 5 mph = 12 hours

Final Answer:

12
