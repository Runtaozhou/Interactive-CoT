Problem Statement:

James is building a hall of mirrors. Three of the walls will be completed covered with glass. If two of those walls are 30 feet by 12 feet and the third is 20 feet by 12 feet, how many square feet of glass does he need?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: First find the area of one of the long walls. Using the perimeter calculation: 30 feet + 12 feet + 30 feet + 12 feet = 84 square feet
Step 2: Then double that amount since there are two long walls: 84 square feet × 2 = 168 square feet
Step 3: Then find the area of the short wall by multiplying length × width: 20 feet × 12 feet = 240 square feet
Step 4: Then add the area of the short wall to the area of the two long walls to find the total area: 168 square feet + 240 square feet = 408 square feet

Final Answer:

408
