Problem Statement:

A school needs to paint the classroom, which is 9 meters long, 8 meters wide, and 3 meters high. The classroom has 4 windows, 2 blackboards, and 2 doors. The windows are 1m by 1.5m, the blackboards are 6m by 1m, and the doors are 2m by 1m. It is known that the paint fee is $4.80 per square meter. How much does it cost to paint a classroom?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the total surface area of the classroom excluding the floor.

We need to find the area of all walls and ceiling:

Surface area = (length × width) + (width × height × 2) + (length × height × 2)

Surface area = (9 × 8) + (8 × 3 × 2) + (9 × 3 × 2)

Surface area = 72 + 48 + 54 = 174 square meters

Step 2: Calculate the area of one window.

Window area = window length × window width

Window area = 1 × 1.5 = 1.5 square meters

Step 3: Calculate the area of all windows.

Total window area = number of windows × one window area

Total window area = 4 × 1.5 = 6 square meters

Step 4: Calculate the area of one blackboard.

Blackboard area = blackboard length × blackboard width

Blackboard area = 6 × 1 = 6 square meters

Step 5: Calculate the area of all blackboards.

Total blackboard area = number of blackboards × one blackboard area

Total blackboard area = 2 × 6 = 12 square meters

Step 6: Calculate the area of one door.

Door area = door length × door width

Door area = 2 × 1 = 2 square meters

Step 7: Calculate the area of all doors.

Total door area = number of doors × one door area

Total door area = 2 × 2 = 4 square meters

Step 8: Calculate the area that needs to be painted.

Paintable area = total surface area - all windows area - all blackboards area - all doors area

Paintable area = 174 - 6 - 12 - 4 = 152 square meters

Step 9: Calculate the total cost of painting.

Total cost = paintable area × paint fee per square meter

Total cost = 152 × $4.80 = $729.60

Final Answer:

$729.6
