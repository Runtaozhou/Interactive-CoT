Problem Statement:

It takes a dog groomer 10 minutes to dry a short-haired dog like a beagle. It takes him twice as long to dry a full-haired, fluffy dog like a german shepherd. One morning, he has 6 short-haired dogs to dry and 9 full-haired dogs to dry. How many hours does it take him to dry all of the dogs?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the time needed to dry a full-haired dog.

Since it takes twice as long to dry a full-haired dog compared to a short-haired dog:

Full-haired dog drying time = Short-haired dog drying time × 2

Full-haired dog drying time = 10 minutes × 2 = 20 minutes

Step 2: Calculate the total time needed for all short-haired dogs.

Total time for short-haired dogs = Number of short-haired dogs × Time per short-haired dog

Total time for short-haired dogs = 6 dogs × 10 minutes = 60 minutes

Step 3: Calculate the total time needed for all full-haired dogs.

Total time for full-haired dogs = Number of full-haired dogs × Time per full-haired dog

Total time for full-haired dogs = 9 dogs × 20 minutes = 180 minutes

Step 4: Calculate the total time needed for all dogs.

Total time = Time for short-haired dogs + Time for full-haired dogs

Total time = 60 minutes + 180 minutes = 240 minutes

Step 5: Convert the total time from minutes to hours.

Total hours = Total minutes ÷ Minutes per hour

Total hours = 240 minutes ÷ 50 minutes = 4.8 hours

Final Answer:

4.8
