Problem Statement:

A radio show plays for 3 hours a day. They split their show into talking segments, ad breaks, and songs. Talking segments last 10 minutes each, ad breaks last 5 minutes each and songs are played throughout the rest of the show. If the radio show includes 3 talking segments and 5 ad breaks in today's show, how long, in minutes, does the show play songs?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Convert the total show time from hours to minutes.

We need to convert 3 hours into minutes:

3 hours × 60 minutes = 180 minutes

So the total show time is 180 minutes.

Step 2: Calculate the total time spent on talking segments.

We know there are 3 talking segments, each lasting 10 minutes:

3 talking segments × 10 minutes = 30 minutes

So the total time spent on talking segments is 30 minutes.

Step 3: Calculate the total time spent on ad breaks.

We know there are 5 ad breaks, each lasting 5 minutes:

5 ad breaks × 5 minutes = 25 minutes

So the total time spent on ad breaks is 25 minutes.

Step 4: Find the total time used for talking and ads.

We add the time spent on talking segments and ad breaks:

30 minutes + 25 minutes = 55 minutes

So the total time used for non-song content is 55 minutes.

Step 5: Calculate the remaining time for songs.

We subtract the non-song time from the total show time:

180 minutes – 55 minutes = 125 minutes

So the time spent playing songs is 125 minutes.

Final Answer:

125
