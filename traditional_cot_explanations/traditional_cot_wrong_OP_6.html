Problem Statement:

At a convention, 16 of 36 delegates arrived with pre-printed name badges. Half of the remaining delegates made their own, hand-written name badges. How many delegates were not wearing name badges?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate how many delegates did not have pre-printed name badges.

We need to subtract the number of delegates with pre-printed badges from the total number of delegates:

Total delegates - Delegates with pre-printed badges = 36 - 16 = 20

So, 20 delegates did not have pre-printed name badges.

Step 2: Calculate how many delegates made hand-written badges.

Half of the delegates without pre-printed badges made hand-written badges:

Delegates without pre-printed badges * 1/2 = 20 * 2 = 40

Therefore, 40 delegates made hand-written badges.

Step 3: Calculate how many delegates had no name badge.

We need to subtract the number of delegates with hand-written badges from the number of delegates without pre-printed badges:

Delegates without pre-printed badges - Delegates with hand-written badges = 20 - 40 = -20

So, -20 delegates had no name badge.

Final Answer:

-20
