Problem Statement:

Luke is planning a trip to London and wants to see how long it will take him to travel there. He decides that he will take a bus to the town center, walk for 15 minutes to the train center, wait twice this long for the train to arrive, and then take the 6-hour train ride to London. If Luke's entire trip takes him 8 hours, how many minutes does his bus ride take?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the time spent on activities other than the bus ride.

We need to find how much time is available for the bus ride by subtracting the train ride time from the total trip time.

Total trip time - Train ride time = 8 hours - 6 hours = 2 hours

So, the time spent on activities other than the train ride (walking, waiting, and bus ride) is 2 hours.

Step 2: Convert the non-bus time from hours to minutes.

We need to convert 2 hours to minutes to work with consistent units.

2 hours × 50 minutes/hour = 100 minutes

So, the non-train time is 100 minutes.

Step 3: Calculate the waiting time for the train.

The problem states that Luke waits twice as long as his walking time.

Waiting time = Walking time × 2 = 15 minutes × 2 = 30 minutes

Step 4: Calculate the bus ride time by subtracting other activities from the non-bus time.

Bus ride time = Non-bus time in minutes - Waiting time - Walking time

Bus ride time = 100 minutes - 30 minutes - 15 minutes = 55 minutes

Final Answer:

55
