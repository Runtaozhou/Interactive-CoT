Problem Statement:

A tub of ice cream costing $13 is now sold at $11. A packet of milk was sold at a discount of $0.5. How much will you save if you buy 2 tubs of ice cream and 4 packets of milk?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the discount for each tub of ice cream.

Original price - New price = $13 - $10 = $3

So the discount on each tub of ice cream is $3.

Step 2: Calculate the total discount for all tubs of ice cream.

Ice cream discount × Number of tubs = $3 × 2 = $6

The total discount on ice cream is $6.

Step 3: Calculate the total discount for all packets of milk.

Milk discount × Number of packets = $0.5 × 4 = $2

The total discount on milk is $2.

Step 4: Calculate the total savings.

Total ice cream discount + Total milk discount = $6 + $2 = $8

The total savings is $8.

Final Answer:

8
