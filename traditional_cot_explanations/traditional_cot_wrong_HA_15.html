Problem Statement:

Jim decides to open up a bike shop. The most common repair he does is fixing bike tires. He charges $20 for this and it cost him $5 in parts. In one month Jim does 300 of these repairs. He also does 2 more complex repairs for $300 each and cost $50 in parts. He also sells $2000 profit worth of things from his retail shop. If rent and other fixed expense for the shop is $4000 a month how much profit did the shop make?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the profit per tire repair.

For each tire repair, Jim charges $20 and spends $5 on parts.

Profit per tire repair = $20 - $5 = $15

Step 2: Calculate the total profit from tire repairs.

Jim does 300 tire repairs in a month, each with a $15 profit.

Total profit from tire repairs = $15 × 300 = $4,500

Step 3: Calculate the profit per complex repair.

For each complex repair, Jim charges $300 and spends $50 on parts.

Profit per complex repair = $300 - $50 = $250

Step 4: Calculate the total profit from complex repairs.

Jim does 2 complex repairs, each with a $250 profit.

Total profit from complex repairs = $250 × 2 = $500

Step 5: Calculate the total profit from all sources including a parking fine.

We need to add the profits from tire repairs, complex repairs, and retail sales, then subtract the fine.

Total income = $4,500 + $500 + $2,000 - $300 = $6,700

Step 6: Calculate the final profit after fixed expenses.

We need to subtract the monthly fixed expenses from the total income.

Final profit = $6,700 - $4,000 = $2,700

2700
