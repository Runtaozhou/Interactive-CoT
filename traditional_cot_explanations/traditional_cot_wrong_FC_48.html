Problem Statement:

Jill is going to resod her front yard. The plot of land in front of her house is 200 feet wide by 50 feet. Her house has a sidewalk that is 3 feet wide and 50 feet long. There are also four flower beds: two right in front of the house that are 4 feet deep by 25 feet long, one that is 10 feet by 12 feet, and a fourth that is 7 feet by 8 feet. How many square feet of sod will she need to cover her yard, minus the sidewalk and flower beds?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the total area of the front yard.

We need to multiply the width by the length of the yard:

200 feet × 50 feet = 10,000 square feet

Step 2: Calculate the area of the sidewalk.

The sidewalk dimensions are 3 feet wide by 50 feet long:

3 feet × 50 feet = 150 square feet

Step 3: Calculate the area of the two flower beds in front of the house.

Each flower bed is 4 feet deep by 25 feet long:

Area of one flower bed: 4 feet × 25 feet = 100 square feet

Total area of two flower beds: 100 square feet × 2 = 200 square feet

Step 4: Calculate the perimeter of the third flower bed.

The third flower bed is 10 feet by 12 feet:

Perimeter = 2 × (length + width) = 2 × (10 feet + 12 feet) = 2 × 22 feet = 44 feet

Step 5: Calculate the area of the fourth flower bed.

The fourth flower bed is 7 feet by 8 feet:

7 feet × 8 feet = 56 square feet

Step 6: Calculate the total area to subtract from the yard.

We need to add the areas of the sidewalk, the front flower beds, the perimeter of the third flower bed, and the area of the fourth flower bed:

150 square feet + 200 square feet + 44 square feet + 56 square feet = 450 square feet

Step 7: Calculate the area that needs sod.

We subtract the total area to be excluded from the total yard area:

10,000 square feet - 450 square feet = 9,550 square feet

Final Answer:

9550
