Problem Statement:

Lisa sold three and a half boxes of chocolate bars, and Peter sold four and a half boxes. They sold 64 chocolate bars together. How many chocolate bars are in a box?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Let's define a variable to represent the number of chocolate bars in a box. We'll call this variable B.
Step 2: Now we can calculate how many chocolate bars Lisa sold. Since Lisa sold 3 1/2 boxes, she sold 3 1/2 × B chocolate bars.
Lisa's chocolate bars = 3 1/2 × B = 3.5B bars
Step 3: Similarly, we can calculate how many chocolate bars Peter sold. Since Peter sold 4 1/2 boxes, he sold 4 1/2 × B chocolate bars.
Peter's chocolate bars = 4 1/2 × B = 4.5B bars
Step 4: Now we can set up an equation based on the total number of bars sold.
Lisa's bars - Peter's bars = -64
3 1/2 × B - 4 1/2 × B = -1B = -64 bars
Step 5: Finally, we can solve for B to find the number of chocolate bars in a box.
B = -64 ÷ -1
B = 64

Final Answer:

64
