Problem Statement:

Loisa wants to buy a tablet that costs $450 cash. Since she does not have enough money, the store offered her an installment plan for 12 months. She will pay $100 as a down payment and pay $40 for the first 4 months; then $35 for the next four months; then $30 for the last four months. How much will Loisa save if she will buy the tablet in cash instead of on installment?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the total payment for the first 4 months.

We need to multiply the monthly payment by the number of months:

$40 × 4 = $160

So, the total payment for the first 4 months is $160.

Step 2: Calculate the total payment for the next 4 months.

We need to multiply the monthly payment by the number of months:

$35 × 4 = $140

So, the total payment for the next 4 months is $140.

Step 3: Calculate the total payment for the last 4 months.

We need to multiply the monthly payment by the number of months:

$30 × 4 = $120

So, the total payment for the last 4 months is $120.

Step 4: Calculate the total installment payment including down payment.

We add the down payment and all monthly payments:

$100 + $160 + $160 + $120 = $540

So, the total cost of the tablet on installment is $540.

Step 5: Calculate how much Loisa will save by paying cash.

We subtract the cash price from the total installment cost:

$540 - $450 = $90

So, Loisa will save $90 by paying cash instead of using the installment plan.

Final Answer:

90
