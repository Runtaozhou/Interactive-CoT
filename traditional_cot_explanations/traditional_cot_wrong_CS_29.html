Problem Statement:

A fixer uses 30% of the nails in a container to repair the kitchen. He also used 70% of the remaining nails in the container to repair the fence. If there were 400 nails in the container, how many nails are remaining?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate how many nails were used to fix the kitchen.

We need to find 30% of the total 400 nails:

30/100 × 400 = 120 nails

So, 120 nails were used for the kitchen repair.

Step 2: Calculate how many nails remain after fixing the kitchen.

We subtract the nails used for the kitchen from the total:

400 - 120 = 280 nails

After the kitchen repair, 280 nails remain in the container.

Step 3: Calculate how many nails were used to fix the fence.

The fixer used 70% of the remaining 280 nails for the fence:

70/100 × 280 = 196 nails

So, 196 nails were used for the fence repair.

Step 4: Calculate the final number of remaining nails.

We subtract the nails used for the fence from the nails that remained after the kitchen repair:

280 - 190 = 90 nails

Therefore, 90 nails remain in the container after both repairs.

Final Answer:

90
