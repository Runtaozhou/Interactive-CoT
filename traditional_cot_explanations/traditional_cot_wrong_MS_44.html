Problem Statement:

A gallon of whole milk that normally costs $3 is now sold at $2. A box of cereal was sold at a discount of $1. How much will you save via discounts if you buy 3 gallons of whole milk and 5 boxes of cereal?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the total discount for the cereal boxes.

We need to multiply the discount per box by the number of boxes purchased:

$1/box × 5 boxes = $5

So the total discount on cereal is $5.

Step 2: Calculate the total savings from both milk and cereal.

For milk, we save $1 per gallon (original price $3 - sale price $2). With 3 gallons, we save $1 × 3 = $3.

Now we add the milk discount and the cereal discount:

$3 + $5 = $8

Therefore, the total savings from buying 3 gallons of milk and 5 boxes of cereal is $8.

Final Answer:

8
