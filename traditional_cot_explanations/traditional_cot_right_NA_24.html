Problem Statement:

Two stores sell the same model of smartphone, but they offer it at different prices and with different discounts. Store A lists the smartphone at a full price of $125 and offers an additional discount of 8%. Store B lists the same smartphone for $130 and offers an additional discount of 10%. After both stores' discounts are applied, how much cheaper is store A's smartphone than store B's smartphone?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the discount amount for Store A.

We need to find how much discount Store A offers on the smartphone.

Store A discount = Store A price × discount percentage / 100

Store A discount = $125 × 8/100 = $10

Step 2: Calculate the final price at Store A after discount.

Now we subtract the discount from the original price to get the final price at Store A.

Store A final price = Store A price - Store A discount

Store A final price = $125 - $10 = $115

Step 3: Calculate the discount amount for Store B.

Similarly, we need to find how much discount Store B offers on the smartphone.

Store B discount = Store B price × discount percentage / 100

Store B discount = $130 × 10/100 = $13

Step 4: Calculate the final price at Store B after discount.

Now we subtract the discount from the original price to get the final price at Store B.

Store B final price = Store B price - Store B discount

Store B final price = $130 - $13 = $117

Step 5: Find the price difference between Store B and Store A.

To determine how much cheaper Store A's smartphone is compared to Store B's, we subtract Store A's final price from Store B's final price.

Price difference = Store B final price - Store A final price

Price difference = $117 - $115 = $2

Final Answer:

2
