Problem Statement:

A local college is offering German lessons and currently has 8 students enrolled. Through advertising, 8 more became interested but a fourth of these dropped out within a day. 2 more got frustrated and left. The class then rallied to show how simple the course actually is and increased enrollment by 5 times the amount of students already enrolled in the class, but 2 had to drop it because of scheduling conflicts. After one last rally, 6 more people enrolled. As the days passed, half of the class eventually dropped, and half of the remaining students graduated. How many are still enrolled?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the total number of students after the initial advertising.

We start with 8 students, and 8 more became interested through advertising.

Initial students + New interested students = 8 + 8 = 16 students

Step 2: Calculate how many new students dropped out within a day.

A fourth (25%) of the 8 new interested students dropped out within a day.

New students × Dropout rate = 8 × 0.25 = 2 students dropped out

Step 3: Calculate the number of students after initial dropouts.

From our 16 students, 2 dropped out within a day, and 2 more got frustrated and left.

Total after advertising - Initial dropouts - Additional dropouts = 16 - 2 - 2 = 12 students

Step 4: Calculate the number of students after the first rally.

The enrollment increased by 5 times the amount of students already enrolled (12 students).

Students after dropouts × (1 + Enrollment increase factor) = 12 × 5 + 12 = 72 students

Step 5: Calculate the number of students after scheduling conflicts.

After the rally, 2 students had to drop due to scheduling conflicts.

Students after rally × 2 = 72 × 2 = 144 students

Step 6: Calculate the number of students after the final rally.

After the final rally, 6 more people enrolled.

Students after conflicts + Final rally enrollments = 144 + 6 = 150 students

Step 7: Calculate the number of students after half dropped out.

As days passed, half of the class eventually dropped out.

Total enrolled ÷ 2 = 150 ÷ 2 = 75 students

Step 8: Calculate the number of students still enrolled after graduation.

Half of the remaining students graduated, leaving the other half still enrolled.

Students after dropouts ÷ 2 = 75 ÷ 2 = 37.5 students

Final Answer:

37.5
