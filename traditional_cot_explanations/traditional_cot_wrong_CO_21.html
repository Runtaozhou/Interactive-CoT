Problem Statement:

On the weekend, Tony will walk to the store. On weekdays, he runs to the store. When he walks, he goes 2 MPH. When he runs he goes 10 MPH. The store is 4 miles away. If he goes on Sunday, Tuesday, and Thursday, what is the average time in minutes that he spends to get to the store?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate how long it takes Tony to walk to the store on Sunday.

Using the formula: distance ÷ walking speed

4 miles ÷ 2 MPH = 2 hours

Step 2: Convert the Sunday walking time from hours to minutes.

Using the formula: hours × 60

2 hours × 60 = 120 minutes

Step 3: Calculate how long it takes Tony to run to the store on Tuesday and Thursday.

Using the formula: distance ÷ running speed

4 miles ÷ 10 MPH = 0.4 hours

Step 4: Convert the weekday running time from hours to minutes.

Using the formula: hours × 60

0.4 hours × 60 = 24 minutes

Step 5: Calculate the total time spent traveling to the store on all three days.

Using the formula: sunday_time_minutes + weekday_time_minutes + weekday_time_minutes

120 + 12 + 12 = 144 minutes

Step 6: Calculate the average time by dividing the total time by the number of trips.

Using the formula: total_time ÷ number of trips

144 ÷ 3 = 48 minutes

Final Answer:

48
