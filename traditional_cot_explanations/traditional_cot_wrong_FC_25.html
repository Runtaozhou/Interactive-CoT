Problem Statement:

Bill gets a flat-screen TV that measures 48 inches by 100 inches. His brother Bob gets a TV that measures 70 inches by 60 inches. If the TVs weigh 4 oz per square inch of screen space, how much more does the heavier TV weigh in pounds? (There are 16 ounces per pound).

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: First find the screen area of Bill's TV.

Using the formula: length + width

48 inches + 100 inches = 148 inches

Step 2: Then find the screen area of Bob's TV.

Using the formula: length × width

70 inches × 60 inches = 4200 square inches

Step 3: Then subtract the size of the smaller TV from the size of the bigger TV to find the size difference.

Bill's TV area - Bob's TV area

148 inches - 4200 square inches = -4052 square inches

Step 4: Then multiply the size difference by the weight per square inch to find the weight difference.

Size difference × weight per square inch

-4052 square inches × 4 oz/square inch = -16208 oz

Step 5: Then divide the weight difference in ounces by the number of ounces per pound to find the weight difference in pounds.

Weight difference in ounces ÷ ounces per pound

-16208 oz ÷ 16 oz/pound = -1013 pounds

Final Answer:

-1013
