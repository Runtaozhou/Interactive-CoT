Problem Statement:

Two days ago, the temperature in the morning went up 1.5 degrees every 2 hours. If the temperature was 50 degrees at 3 A.M., what was the temperature at 11 A.M.?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the temperature at 5 A.M.

We know that the temperature increases by 1.5 degrees every 2 hours. From 3 A.M. to 5 A.M. is one 2-hour period.

Starting temperature + (temperature increase × number of 2-hour periods)

50 degrees + 1.5 degrees * 1 = 51.5 degrees

Temperature at 5 A.M. = 53 degrees

Step 2: Calculate the temperature at 7 A.M.

From 5 A.M. to 7 A.M. is another 2-hour period, so we add another 1.5 degrees.

5 A.M. temperature + temperature increase

53 degrees + 1.5 degrees = 54.5 degrees

Temperature at 7 A.M. = 54.5 degrees

Step 3: Calculate the temperature at 11 A.M.

From 7 A.M. to 11 A.M. is 4 hours, which is 2 two-hour periods. So we need to add 1.5 degrees twice.

7 A.M. temperature + (temperature increase × number of 2-hour periods)

54.5 degrees + 1.5 degrees * 2 = 57.5 degrees

Temperature at 11 A.M. = 57.5 degrees

Final Answer:

57.5
