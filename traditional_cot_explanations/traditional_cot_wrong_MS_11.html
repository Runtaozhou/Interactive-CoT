Problem Statement:

Jude bought three chairs for his house, all at the same price. He also bought a table that costs $50 and two sets of plates at $20 for each set. After giving the cashier $130, Jude got a $4 change. How much did each of the chairs cost?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the total amount Jude paid.

To find this, we subtract the change received from the amount given to the cashier:

Amount given - Change received = $130 - $4 = $126

So Jude paid a total of $126.

Step 2: Calculate the cost of the chairs and plates by subtracting the table cost.

We know the table costs $50, so:

Total paid - Table cost = $126 - $50 = $76

The combined cost of the chairs and plates is $76.

Step 3: Calculate the cost of the chairs by subtracting the cost of one set of plates.

Each set of plates costs $20, and Jude bought 2 sets, so the total cost of plates is $40.

Chairs and plates cost - Plates cost = $76 - $40 = $36

The total cost of all chairs is $36.

Step 4: Calculate the cost of each chair.

Since Jude bought 3 chairs at the same price, we divide the total cost of chairs by 3:

Each chair costs $12.
