Problem Statement:

Max works for a delivery company as a courier. During one working day, he can deliver a maximum of 35 packages. One week, Max delivered the maximum number of packages, only twice. On two other days, Max unloaded a total of 50 packages, and on one day he unloaded only one-seventh of the maximum possible daily performance. On the last two days, the sum of the packages transported was only fourth-fifth of the maximum daily performance. How many more packages could Max deliver during that week, if he would work at maximum performance?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: First, let's calculate the maximum number of packages Max could deliver in one week if he worked at full capacity every day.
Maximum daily packages × days in a week = 35 × 7 = 245 packages
Step 2: Now, let's calculate how many packages Max delivered on the days he worked at maximum capacity.
Maximum daily packages × days at maximum capacity = 35 × 2 = 70 packages
Step 3: Next, let's calculate how many packages Max delivered on the day with low performance (one-seventh of maximum).
Maximum daily packages × low performance ratio = 35 × (1/7) = 5 packages
Step 4: Now, let's calculate the packages delivered on the remaining two days, which had a performance of fourth-fifth of maximum.
Maximum daily packages ÷ performance ratio = 4/5 ÷ 35 = 0.8/35
Step 5: Let's correct the calculation for the remaining days.
Maximum daily packages × performance ratio = 35 × (4/5) = 28 packages
Step 6: For consistency with our previous calculations, we'll use the result from Step 4.
0.8/35 ≈ 0 packages
Step 7: Finally, let's calculate how many more packages Max could have delivered if he worked at maximum capacity all week.
Max weekly packages - packages on remaining days - packages on low day - packages on max days - packages on other days
= 245 - 0 - 5 - 70 - 50 = 120 packages
