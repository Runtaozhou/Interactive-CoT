Problem Statement:

The last time Bob cut his hair he cut it to 6 inches. His hair is now 36 inches long. If hair grows at a rate of .5 inches per month how many years did it take him to grow out his hair?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate how much his hair grew in total.

We need to find the total growth by taking the current length minus the initial length.

Current length = 36 inches

Initial length = 6 inches

Total growth = 36 inches - 6 inches = 30 inches

However, the calculation shows: 36 inches * 12 months/year = 432 inches

Step 2: Calculate how many months it took for his hair to grow that much.

We divide the total growth by the growth rate per month.

Total growth = 432 inches

Growth rate = 0.5 inches/month

Months taken = 432 inches รท 0.5 inches/month = 864 months

Step 3: Convert the months to years.

We divide the number of months by 12 (months per year).

Months taken = 864 months

Years taken = 864 months รท 12 months/year = 72 years

Final Answer:

72
