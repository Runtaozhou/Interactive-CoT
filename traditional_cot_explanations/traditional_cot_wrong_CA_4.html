Problem Statement:

Harry decided to buy some balloons for his friend's birthday party. One balloon costs $0.5, and a pack of 10 balloons is cheaper and costs only $3. Finally, Harry wants to bring to the party exactly 14 balloons. How much did he need to pay for them?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: To buy 14 balloons, Harry should buy one pack of 10, and 4 separate balloons. We can calculate the number of separate balloons needed by subtracting the pack size from the total number of balloons needed:
Total balloons - Pack size = Separate balloons
14 - 10 = 4 separate balloons
Step 2: The price of 4 balloons is calculated by multiplying the number of balloons by the price per balloon:
Separate balloons × Price per balloon
4 × $0.5 = $3
Step 3: In total, the pack and separate balloons would cost Harry by adding the pack cost and separate balloons cost:
Pack cost + Separate balloons cost
$3 + $3 = $6

Final Answer:

6
