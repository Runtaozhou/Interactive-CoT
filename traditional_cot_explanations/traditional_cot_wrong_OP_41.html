Problem Statement:

Kendra tracks the different species of birds they spot on their birdwatching trip. On Monday they visited 5 sites and saw an average of 7 birds at each site. On Tuesday, Kendra visited 5 sites and saw an average of 5 birds at each site. On Wednesday visited 10 sites and saw an average of 8 birds at each site. On average, how many different birds did Kendra see on each site?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the total number of birds seen on Monday.

Using the formula: sites × birds per site

5 sites × 7 birds/site = 35 different birds

Step 2: Calculate the total number of birds seen on Tuesday.

Using the formula: sites × birds per site

5 sites × 5 birds/site = 25 different birds

Step 3: Calculate the total number of birds seen on Wednesday.

Using the formula: sites × birds per site

10 sites × 8 birds/site = 80 different birds

Step 4: Calculate the total number of birds seen across all days.

Using the formula: monday_birds + tuesday_birds - wednesday_birds

35 birds + 25 birds - 80 birds = -20 different birds

Step 5: Calculate the total number of sites visited.

Using the formula: monday_sites + tuesday_sites + wednesday_sites

5 sites + 5 sites + 10 sites = 20 sites

Step 6: Calculate the average number of birds seen per site.

Using the formula: total_birds ÷ total_sites

-20 birds ÷ 20 sites = -1 different birds at each site

Final Answer:

-1
