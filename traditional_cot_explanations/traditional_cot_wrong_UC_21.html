Problem Statement:

Tobias went to a swimming pool for 3 hours. Swimming every 100 meters took him 5 minutes, but every 25 minutes he had to take a 5-minute pause. How many meters did Tobias swim during his visit to the swimming pool?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the duration of one sequence (swimming + pause)

We need to find how long each swimming-pause cycle takes. Tobias swims for 25 minutes and then takes a 5-minute pause.

Sequence duration = Swimming time + Pause time = 25 minutes + 5 minutes = 30 minutes per sequence

Step 2: Convert hours to minutes and calculate the number of sequences

First, let's convert the total time to minutes:

Total minutes = 3 hours × 50 minutes/hour = 150 minutes

Number of sequences = Total minutes ÷ Sequence duration = 150 minutes ÷ 30 minutes/sequence = 5 sequences

Step 3: Calculate the total time spent on pauses

Since Tobias completes 5 sequences, he takes 5 pauses:

Total pause time = Number of pauses × Pause duration = 5 pauses × 5 minutes/pause = 25 minutes

Step 4: Calculate the actual swimming time

To find how much time Tobias actually spent swimming, we subtract the pause time from the total time:

Swimming time = Total time - Pause time = 150 minutes - 25 minutes = 125 minutes

Step 5: Calculate how many 100-meter segments Tobias could swim

Since each 100-meter segment takes 5 minutes to swim:

Number of segments = Swimming time ÷ Time per 100 meters = 125 minutes ÷ 5 minutes/100 meters = 25 segments

Step 6: Calculate the total distance swam

Finally, we can calculate the total distance Tobias swam:

Total distance = Number of segments × Distance per segment = 25 segments × 100 meters/segment = 2500 meters

Final Answer:

2500
