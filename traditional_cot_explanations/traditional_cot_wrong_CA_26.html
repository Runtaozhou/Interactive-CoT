Problem Statement:

Janice can type 6 sentences per minute. Today at work, Janice continued working on a paper she started typing yesterday. She typed for 20 minutes, took a break, and typed 15 minutes longer. She then had to erase 40 sentences she had typed incorrectly. After a meeting, she typed for 18 minutes more. In all, the paper had 536 sentences by the end of today. How many sentences did she start with today?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Let's define the unknown variable X as the number of sentences Janice started with today (carried over from yesterday).
Step 2: Calculate how many sentences Janice typed in her first session.
Typing speed × time = 6 sentences/minute × 20 minutes = 120 sentences
Step 3: Calculate how many sentences Janice typed in her second session.
Typing speed × time = 6 sentences/minute × 15 minutes = 90 sentences
Step 4: Calculate how many sentences Janice typed in her third session.
Typing speed × time = 6 sentences/minute × 18 minutes = 100 sentences
Step 5: Calculate the total number of sentences Janice typed today.
First session + Second session + Third session = 120 + 90 + 100 = 310 sentences
Step 6: Calculate the net sentences added today after erasing the incorrect ones.
Total typed - Erased sentences = 310 - 40 = 270 sentences
Step 7: Set up an equation to find the starting number of sentences.
Starting sentences + Net added = Final sentences
X + 270 = 536
Step 8: Solve for the number of sentences Janice started with today.
X = Final sentences - Net added
X = 536 - 270 = 266 sentences

Final Answer:

266
