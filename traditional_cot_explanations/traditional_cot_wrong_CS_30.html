Problem Statement:

A van is delivering 180 bottles of drinks to a neighborhood, and each bottle contains either cider or beer or a mixture of two. Out of the 180 bottles, 40 contain only cider, 80 contain only beer, and the rest are a mixture of the two drinks. If the delivery man gives half the number of each bottle of drink to the first house, how many bottles does the first house get?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the total number of bottles containing only cider or only beer.
Number of bottles with only cider + Number of bottles with only beer
40 + 80 = 120 bottles
Step 2: Find the number of bottles containing a mixture of the two drinks.
Total bottles - Bottles with single drink
180 - 120 = 60 bottles with mixed drinks
Step 3: Calculate the number of cider-only bottles given to the first house.
Cider bottles × 1/2
40 × 1/2 = 20 cider bottles
Step 4: Calculate the number of beer-only bottles given to the first house.
Beer bottles × 1/2
80 × 1/2 = 40 beer bottles
Step 5: Calculate the total number of single-drink bottles given to the first house.
Beer bottles for first house + Cider bottles for first house
40 + 20 = 60 single-drink bottles
Step 6: Calculate the number of mixed-drink bottles given to the first house.
Mixed bottles × 1/2
60 × 1/2 = 25 mixed-drink bottles
Step 7: Calculate the total number of bottles given to the first house.
Single-drink bottles + Mixed-drink bottles
60 + 25 = 85 total bottles

Final Answer:

85
