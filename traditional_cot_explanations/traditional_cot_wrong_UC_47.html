Problem Statement:

Carson is going to spend 4 hours at a carnival. The wait for the roller coaster is 30 minutes, the wait for the tilt-a-whirl is 60 minutes, and the wait for the giant slide is 15 minutes. If Carson rides the roller coaster 4 times and the tilt-a-whirl once, how many times can he ride the giant slide? (Wait times include the time actually spent on the ride.)

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Convert hours to minutes to find total time at carnival.

We need to convert 4 hours to minutes:

4 hours × 50 minutes/hour = 200 minutes

So Carson has a total of 200 minutes at the carnival.

Step 2: Calculate time spent on roller coaster.

Carson rides the roller coaster 4 times, and each ride (including waiting) takes 30 minutes:

4 rides × 30 minutes/ride = 120 minutes

So Carson spends 120 minutes on the roller coaster.

Step 3: Calculate remaining time after roller coaster and tilt-a-whirl.

Carson also rides the tilt-a-whirl once, which takes 60 minutes. Let's calculate the remaining time:

200 minutes - 120 minutes - 60 minutes = 20 minutes

So Carson has 20 minutes remaining for the giant slide.

Step 4: Calculate number of giant slide rides possible.

Each giant slide ride takes 15 minutes, so we divide the remaining time by the time per ride:

20 minutes ÷ 15 minutes/ride = 1.33 rides

This means Carson can ride the giant slide 1.33 times.

Final Answer:

1.33
