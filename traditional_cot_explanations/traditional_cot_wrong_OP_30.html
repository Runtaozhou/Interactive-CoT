Problem Statement:

Frank picked 36 apples. Susan picked 3 times as many apples as Frank. Susan gave out half of her apples, and Frank sold a third of his, how many in total do they have left?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate how many apples Susan picked.

Susan picked 3 times as many apples as Frank.

Frank picked 36 apples.

So, Susan picked: 3 × 36 = 108 apples

Step 2: Calculate how many apples Susan gave out.

Susan gave out half of her apples.

Susan gave out: 108 ÷ (1/2) = 216 apples

Step 3: Calculate how many apples Susan has left.

Susan's remaining apples = Total apples - Apples given out

Susan's remaining apples = 108 - 216 = -108 apples

Step 4: Calculate how many apples Frank sold.

Frank sold a third of his apples.

Frank sold: 36 × (1/3) = 12 apples

Step 5: Calculate how many apples Frank has left.

Frank's remaining apples = Total apples - Apples sold

Frank's remaining apples = 36 - 12 = 24 apples

Step 6: Calculate the total number of apples they have left.

Total apples left = Susan's remaining apples + Frank's remaining apples

Total apples left = -108 + 24 = -84 apples

Final Answer:

-84
