Problem Statement:

There is a playground that is surrounded by a square fence that has a side length of 27 yards. There is a 12 yard by 9 yard garden that has fencing around it. How many yards of fencing do the playground and garden have together?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the total length of the playground fence.

Since the playground is surrounded by a square fence with a side length of 27 yards, we need to multiply the side length by 4 to find the total fence length.

Playground fence length = 27 yards × 4 = 108 yards

Step 2: Calculate the perimeter of the garden.

The garden has dimensions of 12 yards by 9 yards. To find the perimeter, we use the formula: 2 × (length + width).

Garden fence length = 2 × (12 yards + 9 yards) = 2 × 21 yards = 42 yards

Step 3: Add the playground fence and garden fence lengths together.

To find the total fencing, we add the playground fence length and the garden fence length.

Total fence length = 108 yards + 42 yards = 150 yards

Step 4: State the final answer.

The playground and garden have a combined fence length of 150 yards.

Final Answer:

150
