Problem Statement:

James has a room that is 13 feet by 18 feet. He increases each dimension by 2 feet. He then builds 3 more rooms of equal size and 1 room of twice that size. How much area does he have?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the new length after increasing it.

Original length + increase = 13 + 2 = 15 feet

Step 2: Calculate the new width after increasing it.

Original width + increase = 18 + 2 = 20 feet

Step 3: Calculate the area of each room.

New length + new width + new length + new width = 15 + 20 + 15 + 20 = 70 square feet

Step 4: Calculate the total number of equal-sized rooms.

Original room + additional equal rooms = 1 + 3 = 4 rooms

Step 5: Calculate the total area of equal-sized rooms.

Total equal rooms × room area = 4 × 70 = 280 square feet

Step 6: Calculate the area of the larger room.

Room area × 2 = 70 × 2 = 140 square feet

Step 7: Calculate the total area of all rooms.

Equal rooms area + larger room area = 280 + 140 = 420 square feet

Final Answer:

420
