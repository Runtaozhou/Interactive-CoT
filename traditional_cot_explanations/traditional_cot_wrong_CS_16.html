Problem Statement:

Nick is choosing between two jobs. Job A pays $15 an hour for 2000 hours a year, and is in a state with a 20% total tax rate. Job B pays $42,000 a year and is in a state that charges $6,000 in property tax and a 10% tax rate on net income after property tax. How much more money will Nick make at the job with a higher net pay rate, compared to the other job?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the gross annual salary for Job A.

To find the annual salary, we multiply the hourly rate by the number of hours worked per year.

$15/hour × 2000 hours = $30,000 per year

Step 2: Calculate the amount of taxes Nick pays at Job A.

The tax rate for Job A is 20% of the gross salary.

$30,000 × 0.2 = $6,000 in taxes

Step 3: Calculate Nick's net pay at Job A.

Net pay is the gross salary minus taxes.

$30,000 - $6,000 = $24,000 net pay

Step 4: Calculate Job B income after property tax.

For Job B, we first subtract the property tax from the gross salary.

$42,000 - $6,000 = $36,000 after property tax

Step 5: Calculate income tax at Job B.

The income tax rate for Job B is 10% on the income after property tax.

$36,000 × 0.1 = $3,600 in income tax

Step 6: Calculate net income at Job B.

Net income is the income after property tax minus the income tax.

$36,000 - $3,600 = $32,400 net income

Step 7: Calculate the difference between the higher and lower net income.

Job B has a higher net income ($32,400) than Job A ($20,000).

$32,400 - $20,000 = $12,400

Final Answer:

$12,400
