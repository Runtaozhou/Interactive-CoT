Problem Statement:

It takes 1 hour for refrigerated dough to come to room temperature. It takes 15 minutes to shape the dough and 2 hours to proof. The bread takes 30 minutes to bake and 15 minutes to cool. If the bakery opens at 6:00 am, what is the latest time the head baker can make it to the store to start working?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the time needed for dough temperature and proofing.

The dough needs 1 hour to reach room temperature and 2 hours to proof.

Total time for temperature adjustment and proofing = 1 hour + 2 hours = 3 hours

Step 2: Calculate the time needed for shaping, baking and cooling.

Shaping takes 15 minutes, baking takes 30 minutes, and cooling takes 15 minutes.

Total time for shaping, baking, and cooling = 15 + 30 + 15 = 60 minutes

Step 3: Convert the minutes to hours.

To convert minutes to hours, we divide by the number of minutes in an hour.

Step 4: Calculate the total time needed from start to finish.

We add the time for temperature adjustment and proofing to the time for shaping, baking, and cooling.

Total preparation time = 3 hours + 0.857 hours = 3.857 hours

Step 5: Calculate the latest time the baker can arrive.

We subtract the total preparation time from the opening time to find when the baker must start.

Latest arrival time = 6:00 am - 3.857 hours = 2:08.6 am

Rounding to the nearest minute gives us 2:09 am

Final Answer:

2:09 am
