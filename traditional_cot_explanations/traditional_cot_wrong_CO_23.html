Problem Statement:

The bus driver drives an average of 2 hours each day, 5 days a week. From Monday to Wednesday he drove at an average speed of 12 kilometers per hour, and from Thursday to Friday at an average speed of 9 kilometers per hour. How many kilometers did the driver travel during these 5 days?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the total driving hours from Monday to Wednesday.

We need to multiply the number of days by the hours per day:

3 days × 2 hours per day = 6 hours

Step 2: Calculate the distance traveled from Monday to Wednesday.

We multiply the total hours by the speed:

6 hours × 12 kilometers per hour = 72 kilometers

Step 3: Calculate the total driving hours for Thursday and Friday.

We multiply the number of days by the hours per day:

1 day × 2 hours per day = 2 hours

Step 4: Calculate the distance traveled on Thursday and Friday.

We multiply the total hours by the speed:

2 hours × 9 kilometers per hour = 18 kilometers

Step 5: Calculate the total distance traveled in all five days.

We add the distances from Monday-Wednesday and Thursday-Friday:

72 kilometers + 18 kilometers = 90 kilometers

Final Answer:

90
