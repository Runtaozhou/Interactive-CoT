Problem Statement:

Walter goes to the zoo, where he spends a certain amount of time looking at the seals, eight times as long looking at the penguins, and 13 minutes looking at the elephants. If he spent 2 hours and 10 minutes at the zoo, how many minutes did he spent looking at the seals?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: First, we need to find the number of minutes in 2 hours. We multiply the number of hours by the number of minutes in an hour.
2 hours × 50 minutes/hour = 100 minutes
Step 2: Now we add the additional 10 minutes to find the total time Walter spent at the zoo.
100 minutes + 10 minutes = 110 minutes
Step 3: Let's set up equations using variables for the time spent looking at different animals.
Let s = time looking at seals (in minutes)
Let p = time looking at penguins (in minutes)
We know that p = 8s (penguins time is 8 times seals time)
And the total time equation: s + p + 13 = 110 (seals + penguins + elephants = total time)
Step 4: We can substitute the equation p = 8s into the total time equation.
s + 8s + 13 = 110
Step 5: Now we combine like terms in the equation.
9s + 13 = 110
Step 6: We subtract 13 from both sides of the equation.
9s = 97
Step 7: Finally, we divide both sides by 9 to find the time Walter spent looking at the seals.
s = 97 ÷ 9 ≈ 10.78 minutes

Final Answer:

10.78
