Problem Statement:

When Diane turns 30, she will be half the age of Alex and twice as old as Allison. Diane is 16 years old now. What is the sum of the ages of Alex and Allison now?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate Alex's age when Diane turns 30.

When Diane is 30, she will be half of Alex's age. This means Alex will be twice Diane's age.

Alex's future age = Diane's future age × 2 = 30 × 2 = 60 years old

Step 2: Calculate how many years until Diane turns 30.

We need to find out how many years will pass before Diane turns 30.

Years until Diane turns 30 = Diane's future age - Diane's current age = 30 - 16 = 14 years

Step 3: Calculate Alex's current age.

Now we can determine Alex's current age by subtracting the years until Diane turns 30 from Alex's future age.

Alex's current age = Alex's future age - years until Diane turns 30 = 60 - 14 = 46 years

Step 4: Calculate Allison's current age.

When Diane is 30, she will be twice as old as Allison. So Allison will be 30 ÷ 2 = 15 years old at that time.

Allison's current age = Diane's future age - years until Diane turns 30 = 30 - 14 = 16 years old

Step 5: Calculate the sum of Alex and Allison's current ages.

Finally, we add Alex's and Allison's current ages to find their sum.

Sum of current ages = Alex's current age + Allison's current age = 46 + 16 = 62 years

Final Answer:

62
