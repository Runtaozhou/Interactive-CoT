Problem Statement:

Steve bought $25 worth of groceries. He bought a gallon of milk for $3, two boxes of cereal for $3.5 each, 4 bananas for $.25 each, four apples that cost $.5 each and a number of boxes of cookies. The cookies cost twice as much per box as the gallon of milk. How many boxes of cookies did he get?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the total cost of cereal.

We need to multiply the number of cereal boxes by the cost per box.

2 boxes × $3.5 per box = $7

Step 2: Calculate the total cost of bananas.

We need to multiply the number of bananas by the cost per banana.

4 bananas × $0.25 per banana = $1

Step 3: Calculate the total cost of apples.

We need to multiply the number of apples by the cost per apple.

4 apples × $0.5 per apple = $2

Step 4: Calculate the cost of everything except cookies.

We need to add the cost of milk, cereal, bananas, and apples.

$3 (milk) + $7 (cereal) + $1 (bananas) + $2 (apples) = $11

Step 5: Calculate the amount spent on cookies.

We subtract the non-cookie cost from the total grocery cost.

$25 (total) - $11 (non-cookie items) = $14

Step 6: Calculate the cost of one box of cookies.

The problem states that cookies cost twice as much per box as the gallon of milk.

$3 (milk cost) × 2 = $6 per box of cookies

Step 7: Calculate the number of cookie boxes purchased.

We divide the total cookie cost by the cost per cookie box.

$14 (total cookie cost) ÷ $6 (cost per box) = 2.33 boxes

Final Answer:

2.33
