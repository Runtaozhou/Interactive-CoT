Problem Statement:

At Kaleb's Restaurant, a group with six adults and two children came in to eat. If each adult meal cost six dollars and each children's meal was $4, and every person ordered a soda for $2 how much was the bill?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the cost for children's meals

We need to multiply the number of children by the cost per children's meal:

2 children × $4 = $8

The total cost for children's meals is $8.

Step 2: Calculate the total number of people ordering drinks

We need to add the number of adults and the number of children:

6 adults + 2 children = 8 people

There are 8 people in total who will order drinks.

Step 3: Calculate the cost for all drinks

We multiply the total number of people by the cost per soda:

8 people × $2 = $16

The total cost for all drinks is $16.

Step 4: Calculate the total cost of the meal

We add the cost of adult meals, children's meals, and drinks:

$6 + $8 + $16 = $30

The total bill comes to $30.

Final Answer:

30
