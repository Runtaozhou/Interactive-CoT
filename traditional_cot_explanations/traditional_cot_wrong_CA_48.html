Problem Statement:

A tomato plant has 100 tomatoes. Jane picks 1/4 of that number for use in their house. After a week, she goes back and picks 20 more tomatoes, and the following week picks twice that number. What's the total number of fruits remaining on the tomato plant?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate how many tomatoes Jane picks in the first round.

We need to find 1/4 of the total 100 tomatoes:

1/4 × 100 = 25 tomatoes

So Jane picks 25 tomatoes in the first round.

Step 2: Calculate how many tomatoes remain after the first picking.

We subtract the first picking from the total number of tomatoes:

100 - 25 = 75 tomatoes

After the first picking, 75 tomatoes remain on the plant.

Step 3: Calculate how many tomatoes remain after the second picking.

We subtract the second picking (20 tomatoes) from the remaining tomatoes after the first picking:

75 - 20 = 50 tomatoes

After the second picking, 50 tomatoes remain on the plant.

Step 4: Calculate how many tomatoes Jane picks in the third round.

The third picking is twice the second picking:

20 × 2 = 40 tomatoes

So Jane picks 40 tomatoes in the third round.

Step 5: Calculate the final number of tomatoes remaining.

We subtract the third picking from the remaining tomatoes after the second picking:

50 - 40 = 10 tomatoes

After all three pickings, 10 tomatoes remain on the plant.

Final Answer:

10
