Problem Statement:

Bob is tilling a plot of his garden. The plot is 110 feet wide by 120 feet long. His tiller digs a swath two feet wide, and he can till 1 foot of ground in about 2 seconds. How long will it take him to till this plot of land, in minutes?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the number of rows needed to till the entire width.

We need to divide the width of the plot by the width of the tiller:

Width of plot ÷ Width of tiller = 110 feet ÷ 2 feet = 55 rows

Step 2: Calculate the total distance Bob will need to till.

We multiply the length of each row by the number of rows:

Length × Number of rows = 120 feet × 55 rows = 6,600 feet

Step 3: Calculate the total time needed in seconds.

Since Bob can till 1 foot in 2 seconds, we multiply the total distance by the time per foot:

Time per foot × Total distance = 2 seconds × 6,600 feet = 13,200 seconds

Step 4: Convert the time from seconds to minutes.

We divide the total time in seconds by 60:

Total time in seconds ÷ 60 = 13,200 seconds ÷ 60 = 220 minutes

Final Answer:

220
