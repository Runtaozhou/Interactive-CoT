Problem Statement:

A school is planning a community outreach program. Each classroom must raise $200 for this activity. Classroom A has raised $20 from each of two families, $10 from each of eight families, and $5 from each of ten families. How much more money does Classroom A need in order to reach the goal?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the amount raised from eight families who gave $10 each.

Using the formula: number of families × amount per family

8 × $10 = $80

So, the amount raised from the second group is $80.

Step 2: Calculate the amount raised from ten families who gave $5 each.

Using the formula: number of families × amount per family

10 × $5 = $50

So, the amount raised from the third group is $50.

Step 3: Calculate the total amount raised so far.

Using the formula: amount_from_second_group + amount_from_third_group

$80 + $50 = $130

So, the total amount raised is $130.

Step 4: Calculate how much more money is needed to reach the goal.

Using the formula: goal_amount - total_raised

$200 - $130 = $70

So, Classroom A needs $70 more to reach their goal.

Final Answer:

70
