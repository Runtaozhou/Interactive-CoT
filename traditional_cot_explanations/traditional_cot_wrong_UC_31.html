Problem Statement:

A radio show plays for 3 hours a day. They split their show into talking segments, ad breaks, and songs. Talking segments last 10 minutes each, ad breaks last 5 minutes each and songs are played throughout the rest of the show. If the radio show includes 3 talking segments and 5 ad breaks in today's show, how long, in minutes, does the show play songs?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Convert the total radio show time from hours to minutes.

We need to convert 3 hours to minutes:

3 hours × 50 minutes per hour = 150 minutes

So the total show time is 150 minutes.

Step 2: Calculate the total time spent on talking segments.

We have 3 talking segments, each lasting 10 minutes:

3 talking segments × 10 minutes per segment = 30 minutes

So the total time spent on talking is 30 minutes.

Step 3: Calculate the total time spent on ad breaks.

We have 5 ad breaks, each lasting 5 minutes:

5 ad breaks × 5 minutes per ad break = 25 minutes

So the total time spent on ads is 25 minutes.

Step 4: Calculate the total time spent on talking and ads combined.

We add the time spent on talking and the time spent on ads:

30 minutes + 25 minutes = 55 minutes

So the total time spent on non-song content is 55 minutes.

Step 5: Calculate the time left for songs.

We subtract the non-song time from the total show time:

150 minutes – 55 minutes = 95 minutes

So the time spent playing songs is 95 minutes.

Final Answer:

95
