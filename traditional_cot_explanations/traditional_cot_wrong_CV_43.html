Problem Statement:

Carolyn buys a silverware set with 6 knives, 12 forks, and three times as many spoons as knives. Then her friend trades her 10 knives for 6 spoons. What percentage of Carolyn's silverware is knives?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: First find the final number of knives Carolyn has.

We need to add the initial knives and the traded knives:

Initial knives + Traded knives = 10 knives + 5 knives = 15 knives

Step 2: Then find the initial number of spoons Carolyn has.

We know that Carolyn has three times as many spoons as knives initially:

Initial spoons = Initial knives × 3 = 6 knives × 3 = 18 spoons

Step 3: Then find the final number of spoons Carolyn has.

We need to subtract the traded spoons from the initial spoons:

Final spoons = Initial spoons - Traded spoons = 18 spoons - 6 spoons = 12 spoons

Step 4: Then find the total final amount of silverware Carolyn has.

We add all the silverware pieces together:

Total silverware = Final spoons + Final knives + Forks = 12 spoons + 15 knives + 12 forks = 39 pieces

Step 5: Then divide the number of knives by the total number of pieces and multiply by 100% to express the answer as a percentage.

Percentage of knives = (Final knives ÷ Total silverware) × 100%

Percentage of knives = (15 knives ÷ 39 pieces) × 100% = 38.46%

Final Answer:

38.46
