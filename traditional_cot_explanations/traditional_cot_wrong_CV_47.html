Problem Statement:

Mark decides to buy packs of old magic cards and open them to sell. He buys 3 packs for $1800 each. He gets 1 card that is worth $4000 and another card worth $1000. There are 30 more cards worth an average of $50 each. How much money profit did he make?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the total value of the cheaper cards.

We need to find the value of the 30 cards that are worth $50 each on average.

30 cards × $50 per card = $1,500

However, the calculation shows: 30 × 30 = $900

Step 2: Calculate the total value of all cards.

We add the value of the cheaper cards to the values of the two special cards.

$900 (cheaper cards) + $4,000 (first special card) + $1,000 (second special card) = $5,900

Step 3: Calculate the total cost of buying the packs.

Mark bought 3 packs at $1,800 each.

3 packs × $1,800 per pack = $5,400

Step 4: Calculate the profit made.

Profit is the difference between the total value of the cards and the cost of the packs.

$5,900 (total value) - $5,400 (total cost) = $500 profit

Final Answer:

$500
