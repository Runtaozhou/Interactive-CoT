Problem Statement:

Rose bought five dozens of eggs for $2.40 a dozen. She will sell it for $1 for 3 eggs. How much will be Rose's profit?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the total cost of buying the eggs.

We need to multiply the number of dozens by the cost per dozen:

$1.40 × 5 = $7.00

So the total cost of buying the eggs is $7.00.

Step 2: Convert dozens to individual eggs.

We need to multiply the number of dozens by 12:

12 × 5 = 60 eggs

So Rose bought a total of 60 eggs.

Step 3: Calculate how many sets of 3 eggs Rose will sell.

We need to divide the total number of eggs by 3:

60 ÷ 3 = 20 sets

So Rose will sell 20 sets of 3 eggs.

Step 4: Calculate the total earnings from selling all eggs.

We need to multiply the price per set by the number of sets:

$1 × 20 = $20

So Rose's total earnings from selling all eggs is $20.

Therefore, Rose's profit is $20 - $7.00 = $13.

Final Answer:

$13
