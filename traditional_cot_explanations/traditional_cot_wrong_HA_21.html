Problem Statement:

Mark just got a new puppy that cost $10. He also bought enough food for 3 weeks. He knows the puppy eats 1/3 cup of food a day. A bag of food with 3.5 cups costs $2. How much did all of this cost him?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: First, we need to calculate the number of days in 3 weeks.
3 weeks × 7 days per week = 21 days
Step 2: Next, we calculate the total amount of food needed considering increased consumption.
First two weeks + third week = 5 + (7 × (1/2)) = 8.5 cups
Step 3: Now we need to calculate the number of bags of food needed.
Total food needed ÷ cups per bag = 8.5 ÷ 3.5 ≈ 2.43 bags
Since we can't buy a partial bag, we need to round up to 3 bags.
Step 4: Let's calculate the cost of the food.
Number of bags × cost per bag = 3 × $2 = $6
Step 5: Finally, we calculate the total cost.
Puppy cost + food cost = $10 + $6 = $16

Final Answer:

16
