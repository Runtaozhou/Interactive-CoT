Problem Statement:

Vikki worked 42 hours in one week. Her hourly pay rate is $10. From her weekly earnings, 20% is deducted as tax, 5% is deducted as insurance cover, and $5 is deducted for union dues. How much money, in dollars, does Vikki take home after deductions?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate Vikki's total earnings for the week.

We multiply the hours worked by the hourly rate:

10 × $42 = $420

Vikki's total earnings for the week are $420.

Step 2: Calculate the tax deduction.

We multiply the total earnings by the tax rate:

$420 × 0.2 = $84

The tax deduction is $84.

Step 3: Calculate the insurance deduction.

We multiply the total earnings by the insurance rate:

$420 × 0.05 = $21

The insurance deduction is $21.

Step 4: Note the union dues deduction.

The union dues deduction is $5.

Step 5: Calculate the total deductions.

We add all the deductions together:

$80 + $21 + $5 = $106

The total deductions are $106.

Step 6: Calculate Vikki's take-home pay.

We subtract the total deductions from the total earnings:

$420 - $106 = $314

Vikki's take-home pay is $314.

Final Answer:

314
