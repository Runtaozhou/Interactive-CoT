Problem Statement:

The Llesis family drove and hiked 6 hours to their vacation spot. They drove an average of 50 miles per hour and hiked an average of 5 miles per hour less than half their speed when they drive. If it took them 1.5 hours to hike, how far was their vacation spot?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate half the average speed of Llesis family driving.

We need to find half of their driving speed:

Half of driving speed = 60 ÷ 2 = 30 miles per hour

Step 2: Calculate their average hiking speed.

Their hiking speed is 5 miles per hour less than half their driving speed:

Hiking speed = 30 - 5 = 25 miles per hour

Step 3: Calculate the distance hiked.

We can find the hiking distance by multiplying the hiking speed by the time spent hiking:

Hiking distance = 25 × 1.5 = 37.5 miles

Step 4: Calculate the time spent driving.

The total travel time was 6 hours, and they spent 1.5 hours hiking:

Driving time = 6 - 1.5 = 4.5 hours

Step 5: Calculate the distance driven.

We can find the driving distance by multiplying the driving speed by the time spent driving:

Driving distance = 50 × 4.5 = 225 miles

Step 6: Calculate the total distance to the vacation spot.

The total distance is the sum of the hiking distance and the driving distance:

Total distance = 37.5 + 225 = 262.5 miles

Final Answer:

262.5
