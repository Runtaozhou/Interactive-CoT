Problem Statement:

John builds a box. The box is 26 inches by 26 inches by 14 inches. The walls are 1 inch thick on each side. How much is the internal volume in cubic feet?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: The walls take away 2*1=2 inches from each dimension. Since the walls are 1 inch thick on each side, we need to subtract 2 inches (1 inch from each side) from each dimension.
Step 2: So the longer sides are 26-2=24 inches. We subtract the thickness reduction from the original length to find the internal length.
Step 3: That is 24/12=2 feet. We convert the internal length from inches to feet by dividing by 12.
Step 4: The smaller dimension is 14-2=12 inches. We subtract the thickness reduction from the original height to find the internal height.
Step 5: That is 12/12=1 foot. We convert the internal height from inches to feet by dividing by 12.
Step 6: So the internal volume is 2*2*1=4 cubic feet. We multiply the internal length, width, and height (all in feet) to calculate the internal volume.

Final Answer:

4
