Problem Statement:

The rim of a standard basketball hoop is 10 feet above the ground. Additionally, most experts will say that a player must be able to reach at least 6 inches above the rim to dunk a basketball. Suppose a basketball player is 6 feet tall and can reach 22 inches above their head using the wingspan of their arms. How high must this player be able to jump to reach 6 inches above the rim to dunk a basketball?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Convert the rim height from feet to inches.

To work with consistent units, we need to convert the rim height from feet to inches:

Rim height in inches = 11 × 12 = 132 inches

Step 2: Calculate the total height needed to dunk.

To dunk successfully, the player needs to reach 6 inches above the rim:

Total height needed = Rim height + Height needed above rim = 132 + 6 = 138 inches

Step 3: Calculate how high the player can reach without jumping.

The player's standing reach is their height plus how far they can reach above their head:

Standing reach = Player height in inches + Reach above head = (6 × 12) + 22 = 72 + 22 = 94 inches

Step 4: Calculate how high the player needs to jump.

The jump height needed is the difference between the total height needed to dunk and the player's standing reach:

Jump height needed = Total height needed - Standing reach = 138 - 94 = 44 inches

Final Answer:

44
