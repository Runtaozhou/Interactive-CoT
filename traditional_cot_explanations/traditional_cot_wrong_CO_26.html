Problem Statement:

A baker bakes 5 loaves of bread an hour in one oven. He has 4 ovens. From Monday to Friday, he bakes for 5 hours, but on Saturday and Sunday, he only bakes for 2 hours. How many loaves of bread does the baker bake in 3 weeks?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate how many loaves the baker can bake in one hour using all ovens.

The baker can bake 5 loaves per hour in one oven. With 4 ovens, he can bake:

5 loaves per oven × 4 ovens = 20 loaves per hour

Step 2: Calculate how many loaves the baker bakes each weekday (Monday to Friday).

On weekdays, the baker bakes for 5 hours per day. In each hour, he bakes 20 loaves, so:

5 hours × 20 loaves per hour = 100 loaves per weekday

Step 3: Calculate the total loaves baked on all weekdays (Monday to Friday).

There are 5 weekdays (Monday to Friday), and the baker bakes 100 loaves each weekday:

100 loaves per weekday × 5 weekdays = 500 loaves on weekdays

Step 4: Calculate how many loaves the baker bakes each weekend day (Saturday and Sunday).

On weekend days, the baker bakes for 2 hours. In each hour, he bakes 20 loaves, so:

2 hours × 20 loaves per hour = 40 loaves per weekend day

Step 5: Calculate the total loaves baked on Saturday.

The baker bakes 40 loaves on Saturday:

40 loaves per weekend day × 1 Saturday = 40 loaves on Saturday

Step 6: Calculate the total loaves baked in one week.

The total loaves baked in one week is the sum of loaves baked on weekdays and Saturday:

500 loaves on weekdays + 40 loaves on Saturday = 540 loaves per week

Step 7: Calculate the total loaves baked in three weeks.

The baker bakes 540 loaves per week, so in 3 weeks he bakes:

540 loaves per week × 3 weeks = 1620 loaves in 3 weeks

Final Answer:

1620
