Problem Statement:

Tom buys 20 shares of a stock that costs $3 each. He sells 10 of those shares for $4 each. The rest of the shares end up doubling in value. How much profit does he make?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the total cost of buying the shares.

Using the formula: number of shares × price per share

20 shares × $3 per share = $60

Total cost = $60

Step 2: Calculate the revenue from selling the first batch of shares.

Using the formula: number of shares sold × selling price

10 shares × $4 per share = $40

First sale revenue = $40

Step 3: Calculate how many shares are left.

Using the formula: total shares - shares sold

20 shares - 10 shares = 10 shares

Remaining shares = 10

Step 4: Calculate the new price of the remaining shares after doubling.

Using the formula: original price × 2

$3 × 2 = $6

Doubled price = $6 per share

Step 5: Calculate the revenue from selling the remaining shares.

Using the formula: remaining shares × doubled price

10 shares × $6 per share = $60

Second sale revenue = $60

Step 6: Calculate the total revenue from both sales.

Using the formula: first sale revenue + second sale revenue

$60 + $45 = $105

Total revenue = $105

Step 7: Calculate the profit by subtracting the cost from the revenue.

Using the formula: total revenue - total cost

$105 - $60 = $45

Profit = $45
