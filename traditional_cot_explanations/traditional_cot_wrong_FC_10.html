Problem Statement:

John has 3 boxes. Each box is 5 inches by 6 inches by 4 inches. The walls are 1 inch thick. What is the total inner volume of all 3 boxes?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate how much the walls subtract from each dimension.

Since the walls are 1 inch thick on all sides, we need to subtract 2 inches from each dimension (1 inch from each side).

Dimension reduction = 2 × wall thickness = 2 × 1 = 2 inches

Step 2: Calculate the inner width of each box.

Inner width = Outer width - Dimension reduction

Inner width = 5 - 2 = 3 inches

Step 3: Calculate the inner height of each box.

Inner height = Outer height - Dimension reduction

Inner height = 6 - 2 = 4 inches

Step 4: Calculate the inner depth of each box.

Inner depth = Outer depth - Dimension reduction

Inner depth = 4 - 2 = 2 inches

Step 5: Calculate the inner volume of one box.

Inner volume = 2 × (inner_width + inner_height + inner_width + inner_height) + 2 × (inner_width × inner_height)

Inner volume = 2 × (3 + 4 + 3 + 4) + 2 × (3 × 4) = 40 cubic inches

Step 6: Calculate the total inner volume of all boxes.

Total inner volume = Number of boxes × Volume of one box

Total inner volume = 3 × 40 = 120 cubic inches

Final Answer:

120
