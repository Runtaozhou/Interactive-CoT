Problem Statement:

Joe buys 3 oranges, 7 juices, 3 jars of honey, and 4 plants at the market. The fruit costs $4.50 each, the juice was 50 cents each, the jars of honey were $5, and the plants were 2 for $18. How much does Joe spend at the market?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the cost of oranges.

We need to multiply the number of oranges by the price per orange:

3 oranges × $4.50 = $13.50

Step 2: Calculate the cost of juice.

We need to multiply the number of juices by the price per juice:

7 juices × $0.50 = $3.50

Step 3: Calculate the cost of honey.

We need to multiply the number of honey jars by the price per jar:

3 jars × $5 = $15

Step 4: Calculate the cost per plant.

Since the plants are priced at 2 for $18, we need to find the cost per plant:

$18 ÷ 2 = $9 per plant

Step 5: Calculate the total cost of plants.

We need to multiply the number of plants by the cost per plant:

4 plants × $9 = $36

Step 6: Calculate the total cost of all items.

We need to add up all the costs:

$13.50 (oranges) + $3.50 (juice) + $15 (honey) + $40 (plants) = $72

Final Answer:

$72
