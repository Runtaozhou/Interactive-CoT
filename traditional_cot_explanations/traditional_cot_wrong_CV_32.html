Problem Statement:

A bowl of fruit holds 18 peaches. Four of the peaches are ripe and two more ripen every day, but on the third day three are eaten. How many more ripe peaches than unripe peaches are in the bowl after five days?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate how many peaches will ripen in 5 days.

We know that 2 peaches ripen each day, so over 5 days:

5 days Ã— 2 peaches/day = 10 peaches

So 10 more peaches will ripen during the 5-day period.

Step 2: Add the newly ripened peaches to the initially ripe ones.

Initially, there were 4 ripe peaches. Adding the newly ripened ones:

4 initially ripe peaches + 10 newly ripened peaches = 14 total ripened peaches

Step 3: Subtract the eaten peaches from the ripe ones.

On the third day, 3 peaches were eaten. We need to subtract these from the total ripened peaches:

14 ripened peaches - 2 eaten peaches = 12 ripe peaches

Step 4: Calculate the number of unripe peaches left.

We started with 18 total peaches, and now have 12 ripe peaches:

18 total peaches - 12 ripe peaches = 6 unripe peaches

Step 5: Find the difference between ripe and unripe peaches.

To find how many more ripe peaches than unripe peaches are in the bowl:

12 ripe peaches - 6 unripe peaches = 6 peaches

So there are 6 more ripe peaches than unripe peaches after 5 days.

Final Answer:

6
