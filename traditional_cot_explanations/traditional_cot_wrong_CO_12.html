Problem Statement:

A baker bakes 5 loaves of bread an hour in one oven. He has 4 ovens. From Monday to Friday, he bakes for 5 hours, but on Saturday and Sunday, he only bakes for 2 hours. How many loaves of bread does the baker bake in 3 weeks?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate how many loaves the baker can bake in one hour using all ovens.

The baker can bake 5 loaves per hour in one oven, and he has 4 ovens.

Total loaves per hour = 5 × 4 = 20 loaves of bread

Step 2: Calculate how many loaves the baker bakes per day from Monday to Friday.

On weekdays, the baker bakes for 5 hours per day.

Loaves per weekday = 5 hours × 20 loaves per hour = 100 loaves of bread per day

Step 3: Calculate the total loaves baked from Monday to Friday.

There are 5 weekdays (Monday to Friday).

Total weekday loaves = 100 loaves per day × 5 days = 500 loaves of bread

Step 4: Calculate how many loaves the baker bakes per day on Saturday and Sunday.

On weekends, the baker bakes for 2 hours per day.

Loaves per weekend day = 2 hours × 20 loaves per hour = 40 loaves of bread per day

Step 5: Calculate the total loaves baked on Saturday and Sunday.

There are 2 weekend days, but the calculation shows:

Total weekend loaves = 40 loaves per day × 1 day = 40 loaves of bread

Step 6: Calculate the total loaves baked in one week.

We add the weekday total and the weekend total.

Total loaves per week = 500 + 40 = 540 loaves of bread

Step 7: Calculate the total loaves baked in three weeks.

We multiply the weekly total by the number of weeks.

Total loaves in 3 weeks = 540 loaves per week × 3 weeks = 1620 loaves of bread

Final Answer:

1620
