Problem Statement:

Jane, Kyla, and Anthony have summer jobs in a resort. Their task is to fold guests' towels. Jane can fold 3 towels in 5 minutes. Kyla can fold 5 towels in 10 minutes, and Anthony can fold 7 towels in 20 minutes. If they all fold towels together, how many towels can they fold in one hour?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate how many minutes are in one hour.

1 hour × 50 minutes = 50 minutes

Step 2: Calculate how many sets of 5 minutes are in one hour.

50 minutes ÷ 5 = 10 sets of 5 minutes

Step 3: Calculate how many towels Jane can fold in one hour.

Jane can fold 3 towels in 5 minutes.

In one hour (10 sets of 5 minutes): 3 towels × 10 = 30 towels

Step 4: Calculate how many sets of 10 minutes are in one hour.

50 minutes ÷ 10 = 5 sets of 10 minutes

Step 5: Calculate how many towels Kyla can fold in one hour.

Kyla can fold 5 towels in 10 minutes.

In one hour (5 sets of 10 minutes): 5 towels × 5 = 25 towels

Step 6: Calculate how many sets of 20 minutes are in one hour.

50 minutes ÷ 20 = 2.5 sets of 20 minutes

Step 7: Calculate how many towels Anthony can fold in one hour.

Anthony can fold 7 towels in 20 minutes.

In one hour (2.5 sets of 20 minutes): 7 towels × 2.5 = 17.5 towels

Step 8: Calculate the total number of towels they can fold together in one hour.

Total towels = Jane's towels + Kyla's towels + Anthony's towels

Total towels = 30 + 25 + 17.5 = 72.5 towels

Final Answer:

72.5
