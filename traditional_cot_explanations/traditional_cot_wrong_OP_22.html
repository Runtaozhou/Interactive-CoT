Problem Statement:

A store decides to shut down and sell all of its inventory. They have 2000 different items which would normally retail for $50. They are offering an 80% discount and manage to sell 90% of the items. They owed $15000 to their creditors. How much money do they have left after the sale?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the number of items sold.

We need to find how many items were sold by multiplying the total number of items by the percentage sold:

Total items × Percentage sold = 2000 × 0.9 = 1800 items

Step 2: Calculate the discount amount per item.

We need to find the discount amount by dividing the normal price by the discount percentage:

Normal price / Discount percentage = $50 / 0.8 = $62.50

Step 3: Calculate the selling price per item after discount.

We need to find the selling price after discount by subtracting the discount amount from the normal price:

Normal price - Discount amount = $50 - $62.50 = $-12.50

Step 4: Calculate the total revenue from the sale.

We need to find the total revenue by multiplying the number of items sold by the selling price:

Items sold × Selling price = 1800 × $-12.50 = $-22,500

Step 5: Calculate the money left after paying creditors.

We need to find how much money is left by subtracting the money owed to creditors from the total revenue:

Total revenue - Money owed = $-22,500 - $15,000 = $-37,500

Final Answer:

-37500
