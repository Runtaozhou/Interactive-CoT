Problem Statement:

Christi saw twice as many black bears as white bears and 40 more brown bears than black bears in a national park. If the number of black bears in the park is 60, calculate the population of bears in the park.

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the number of brown bears by adding 40 more to black bears, plus 10 unidentified bears.
Brown bears = Black bears + 40 + 10
Brown bears = 60 + 40 + 10 = 110
Step 2: Find the total number of black and brown bears.
Black and brown bears = Black bears + Brown bears
Black and brown bears = 60 + 110 = 170
Step 3: Calculate the number of white bears by dividing black bears by 2.
White bears = Black bears รท 2
White bears = 60 รท 2 = 30
Step 4: Find the total population of bears by adding white bears to the total of black and brown bears.
Total bears = White bears + Black and brown bears
Total bears = 30 + 170 = 200

Final Answer:

200
