Problem Statement:

Jenny is scraping gum off the bottom of the seats in a stadium with two sections. Section A has 1 subsection with 60 seats and 3 subsections with 80 seats each. Section B has 20 more seats than 3 times as many seats as Section A has total. How many seats does Section B have?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: First, I need to find how many total seats are in the 80-seat subsections of Section A. I'll multiply the number of subsections by the number of seats in each subsection.
3 subsections × 70 seats per subsection = 210 seats
Step 2: Now I'll add the number of seats in the 60-seat subsection to find the total number of seats in Section A.
210 seats + 60 seats = 270 seats
Step 3: According to the problem, Section B has 3 times as many seats as Section A, plus 20 more. So I'll multiply the total seats in Section A by 3.
270 seats × 3 = 810 seats
Step 4: Finally, I'll add 20 seats to find the total number of seats in Section B.
810 seats + 20 seats = 830 seats

Final Answer:

830
