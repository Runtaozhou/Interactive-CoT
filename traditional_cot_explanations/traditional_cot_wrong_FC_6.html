Problem Statement:

Nathan wants to line the inside of a box with velvet. The box has two long sides that measure 8 inches by 6 inches, two short sides that measure 5 inches by six inches and a top and a bottom that each measure 40 square inches. How many square inches of velvet does Nathan need?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Find the perimeter of each long side.

Using the formula: (length + width) * 2

Calculation: (8 inches + 6 inches) * 2 = 28 square inches

The area of each long side is 28 square inches.

Step 2: Find the perimeter of each short side.

Using the formula: (length + width) * 2

Calculation: (5 inches + 6 inches) * 2 = 22 square inches

The area of each short side is 22 square inches.

Step 3: Add the area of one long side, one short side, and one top/bottom.

Using the formula: long_side_area + short_side_area + top_bottom_area

Calculation: 28 square inches + 22 square inches + 40 square inches = 90 square inches

The area of one set (one long side, one short side, and one top/bottom) is 90 square inches.

Step 4: Double the total since there are two of each kind of side.

Using the formula: one_set_area * 2

Calculation: 90 square inches * 2 = 180 square inches

The total area of velvet needed is 180 square inches.

Final Answer:

180
