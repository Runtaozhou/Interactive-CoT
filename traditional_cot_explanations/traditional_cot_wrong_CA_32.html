Problem Statement:

Candy has a chair rental business. During the weekdays, 60 chairs are rented each day; but during weekends, 100 chairs are rented each day. If this continues, how many chairs in total will Candy be able to rent out in two 4-week months?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the total chairs rented during weekdays in a week.

We know that 60 chairs are rented each weekday, and there are 5 weekdays in a week.

Number of chairs rented on weekdays per week = 60 × 5 = 300 chairs

Step 2: Calculate the total chairs rented during weekends in a week.

We know that 100 chairs are rented each weekend day, and there are 2 weekend days in a week.

Number of chairs rented on weekends per week = 100 × 2 = 200 chairs

Step 3: Calculate the total chairs rented in a week.

We add the number of chairs rented on weekdays and weekends.

Total chairs rented per week = 300 + 200 = 500 chairs

Step 4: Calculate the total chairs rented in a month.

Each month has 4 weeks, so we multiply the weekly total by 4.

Total chairs rented per month = 500 × 4 = 1950 chairs

Step 5: Calculate the total chairs rented in two months.

We multiply the monthly total by 2 to get the total for two months.

Total chairs rented in two months = 1950 × 2 = 3900 chairs

Final Answer:

3900
