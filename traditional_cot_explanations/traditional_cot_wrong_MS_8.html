Problem Statement:

Frank went to a shop to buy some food for his breakfast. He bought 10 buns for $0.1 each, two bottles of milk, for $2 each, and a carton of eggs, which was three times more expensive than one bottle of milk. How much did Frank pay for his breakfast shopping?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the cost of the milk bottles.

We need to multiply the number of bottles by the price per bottle:

2 bottles × $2 per bottle = $4

So the total cost of milk is $4.

Step 2: Calculate the cost of the carton of eggs.

The carton of eggs costs 3 times the price of one bottle of milk:

3 × $2 = $6

So the carton of eggs costs $6.

Step 3: Calculate the total cost of all items.

We need to add the cost of buns, milk, and eggs:

Cost of buns: 10 buns × $0.1 each = $1

Cost of milk: $4

Cost of eggs: $6

Total cost: $1 + $4 + $6 = $11
