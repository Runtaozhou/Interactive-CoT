Problem Statement:

Shawn collected 40 plain pebbles. He painted 9 pebbles red and 13 pebbles blue. He then divided the remaining pebbles equally into 3 groups, and painted them purple, yellow, and green. What is the difference between the number of blue and yellow pebbles?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate how many pebbles were painted red and other colors.

Total pebbles - Red pebbles = 40 - 9 = 31

So there are 31 pebbles that are not red.

Step 2: Determine how many groups the remaining pebbles were divided into.

According to the problem, the remaining pebbles were divided into 4 groups.

Step 3: Calculate how many pebbles were painted yellow.

Non-red pebbles / number of groups = 31 / 4 = 7.75, which rounds up to 8

So there are 8 yellow pebbles.

Step 4: Find the difference between blue and yellow pebbles.

Blue pebbles - Yellow pebbles = 13 - 8 = 5

Final Answer:

5
