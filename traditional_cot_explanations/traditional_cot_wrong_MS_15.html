Problem Statement:

Phil has a coin collection he likes to add to. He started with 50 state quarters his parents gave him. Over the next year he doubled this. The following year he collected 3 each month. The year after that he collected 1 every third month. The year after that he lost a quarter of them when he misplaced his collection. How many did he have left after losing some?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: First, let's calculate how many quarters Phil had after doubling his initial collection. He started with 50 quarters and then doubled this amount.
50 × 2 = 100 quarters
Step 2: Next, let's calculate how many quarters Phil collected in the second year. He collected 3 quarters each month for 12 months.
3 × 12 = 36 quarters
Step 3: Now, let's calculate Phil's total before losing some. We add the quarters after doubling and the quarters collected in the second year.
100 + 36 = 136 quarters
Step 4: Phil lost a quarter of his collection. Let's calculate how many coins he lost.
136 ÷ 4 = 34 quarters
Step 5: Finally, let's calculate how many coins Phil had remaining after losing some.
136 - 34 = 102 quarters

Final Answer:

102
