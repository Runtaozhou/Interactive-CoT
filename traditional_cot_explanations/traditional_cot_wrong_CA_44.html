Problem Statement:

Steve is building a bench for the playground and needs 6 lengths of wood that measure 4 feet and 2 lengths of wood that measure 2 feet. How many feet of wood does Steve need to buy?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the total length of the long wood pieces.

We need to multiply the number of long pieces by their length:

6 pieces × 4 feet per piece = 24 feet

So the total length of long wood pieces is 24 feet.

Step 2: Calculate the total length of the short wood pieces.

We need to multiply the number of short pieces by their length:

2 pieces × 2 feet per piece = 4 feet

So the total length of short wood pieces is 4 feet.

Step 3: Add the total lengths to find the total amount of wood needed.

We add the total length of long wood pieces and the total length of short wood pieces:

24 feet + 4 feet = 28 feet

Therefore, Steve needs to buy 28 feet of wood in total.

Final Answer:

30
