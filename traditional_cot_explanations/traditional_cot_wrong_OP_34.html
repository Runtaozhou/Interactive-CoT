Problem Statement:

The American swallow can lift into the air and carry a maximum of 5 pounds of weight, while the European swallow can lift and carry twice the weight as the American swallow. If there was a flock of 90 swallows, containing twice as many American as European swallows, what is the maximum combined weight the flock can carry?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Let's set up an equation using x as the number of European swallows and 2x as the number of American swallows. Since we know there are twice as many American swallows as European swallows, we can write:
x + 2x = 90
Step 2: Now we can solve this equation to find the number of each type of swallow:
2x + x = 90
3x = 90
Step 3: Solving for x (the number of European swallows):
x = 90/3 = 30
So there are 30 European swallows in the flock.
Step 4: Now we can calculate the number of American swallows:
American_swallows = 90 + 30 = 120
So there are 120 American swallows in the flock.
Step 5: We know that an American swallow can carry 5 pounds, and a European swallow can carry twice that weight:
European_weight = 2 × American_weight = 2 × 5 = 10 pounds
Step 6: Now we can calculate the total weight that all European swallows can carry:
European_total = European_swallows × European_weight = 30 × 10 = 300 pounds
Step 7: Similarly, we can calculate the total weight that all American swallows can carry:
American_total = American_swallows × American_weight = 120 × 5 = 600 pounds
Step 8: Finally, we can calculate the combined weight the entire flock can carry:
total_weight = European_total + American_total = 300 + 600 = 900 pounds

Final Answer:

900
