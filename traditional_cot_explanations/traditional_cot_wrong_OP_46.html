Problem Statement:

A house's living room, dining room and kitchen take up 1,000 sq ft. The house also has a guest bedroom and a master bedroom suite. If the guest bedroom is a quarter of the size of the master bedroom suite, and the house is 2,300 sq ft total, how large is the master bedroom suite?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the total area for bedrooms by subtracting non-bedroom areas from total house area.

We need to find how much space is available for the bedrooms by subtracting the living room, dining room, and kitchen area from the total house area.

Total house area × non-bedroom area = 2300 × 1000 = 2,300,000 sq ft

So the bedroom area is 2,300,000 sq ft.

Step 2: Define the master bedroom suite size as a variable.

Let's call the master bedroom suite size x.

Step 3: Express the guest bedroom size in terms of the master bedroom suite.

Since the guest bedroom is a quarter of the size of the master bedroom suite, we can express it as:

Guest bedroom size = 0.25 × master suite size = 0.25x

Step 4: Calculate the total bedroom area as the sum of both bedrooms.

Total bedroom area = master suite size + guest bedroom size

Total bedroom area = x + 0.25x = 1.25x

Step 5: Set up equation where total bedroom area equals the available space for bedrooms.

We can set up the equation:

1.25x = 2,300,000

Step 6: Solve for the master bedroom suite size.

x = 2,300,000 ÷ 1.25 = 1,840,000 sq ft

Therefore, the master bedroom suite size is 1,840,000 sq ft.
