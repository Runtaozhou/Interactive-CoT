Problem Statement:

The government gives the bottom 20% of people a $2000 stimulus. They have found that this kind of stimulus returns 5 times as much money to the government, in terms of tax revenue, as it cost. The city has 1000 people in it. What was the government profit from this project?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate how many people receive the stimulus.

We need to find 20% of the total population:

Total people × percentage = 1000 × 0.2 = 200 people

So, 200 people will receive the stimulus.

Step 2: Calculate the total cost of the stimulus.

We multiply the number of recipients by the stimulus amount:

Recipients × stimulus amount = 200 × $2500 = $500,000

The total cost of the stimulus is $500,000.

Step 3: Calculate the money returned to the government.

The stimulus returns 5 times as much money as it cost:

Total cost × return multiplier = $500,000 × 5 = $2,500,000

The government receives $2,500,000 in return.

Step 4: Calculate the government profit.

Profit is the difference between the return and the cost:

Total return - total cost = $2,500,000 - $500,000 = $2,000,000

The government's profit from this project is $2,000,000.

Final Answer:

2,000,000
