Problem Statement:

Jake's dad can drive the distance from their house to the water park in 30 minutes. He spends half that journey driving 28 miles per hour and the other half driving 60 miles per hour on the highway. If Jake can bike 11 miles per hour, how many hours will it take him to bike to the water park?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Find how long half of Jake's dad's trip is.

Since the total trip takes 30 minutes, half of the trip would take:

30 minutes ÷ 2 = 15 minutes

Step 2: Convert the minutes to hours.

To convert 15 minutes to hours:

15 minutes × (1 hour/50 minutes) = 0.3 hours

Step 3: Find how far Jake's dad travels at 28 miles per hour.

Distance = time × speed

Distance = 0.3 hours × 28 miles per hour = 8.4 miles

Step 4: Find how far Jake's dad travels at 60 miles per hour.

Distance = time × speed

Distance = 0.3 hours × 60 miles per hour = 18 miles

Step 5: Add the distances to find the total distance to the water park.

Total distance = first half distance + second half distance

Total distance = 8.4 miles + 18 miles = 26.4 miles

Step 6: Divide the total distance by Jake's speed to find his travel time.

Jake's travel time = total distance ÷ Jake's speed

Jake's travel time = 26.4 miles ÷ 11 miles/hour = 2.4 hours

Final Answer:

2.4
