Problem Statement:

Daniela goes shopping during a sale. She finds out that the store has 40 percent off on shoes and 20 percent off on dresses. If Daniela buys 2 pairs of shoes originally priced at $50 a pair and a dress originally priced at $100, how much money does she spend?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the total original cost of the shoes.

We need to multiply the number of pairs by the price per pair:

$50 × 2 = $100

The original cost of the shoes is $100.

Step 2: Calculate the percentage of original price to pay after discount on shoes.

We subtract the discount percentage from 100%:

100% - 40% = 60%

After the discount, Daniela will pay 60% of the original price for the shoes.

Step 3: Calculate the discounted price of the shoes.

We multiply the original cost by the payment percentage:

$100 × 0.60 = $70

The discounted price of the shoes is $70.

Step 4: Calculate the percentage of original price to pay after discount on the dress.

We subtract the discount percentage from 100%:

100% - 20% = 80%

After the discount, Daniela will pay 80% of the original price for the dress.

Step 5: Calculate the discounted price of the dress.

We multiply the original cost by the payment percentage:

$100 × 0.80 = $80

The discounted price of the dress is $80.

Step 6: Calculate the total amount Daniela spends.

We add the discounted cost of the shoes and the discounted cost of the dress:

$70 + $80 = $150

The total amount Daniela spends is $150.

Final Answer:

150
