Problem Statement:

Linda is painting her bedroom. Her bedroom has 4 walls, with the room being 20 feet wide by 20 feet long by 8 feet tall. One wall has a 3-foot by 7-foot doorway. A second wall has a 6-foot by 4-foot window. A third wall has a 5-foot by 7-foot doorway to a walk-in-closet. And the fourth wall is completely solid. What is the total area of wall space that Linda will have to paint?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the area of the solid wall.

Using the perimeter of the wall: 8 ft. + 20 ft. + 8 ft. + 20 ft. = 56 sq. ft.

Step 2: Calculate the area of the doorway.

Using the formula length × width: 3 ft. × 7 ft. = 21 sq. ft.

Step 3: Calculate the area of the window.

Using the formula length × width: 6 ft. × 4 ft. = 24 sq. ft.

Step 4: Calculate the area of the closet door.

Using the formula length × width: 5 ft. × 7 ft. = 35 sq. ft.

Step 5: Calculate the total area of doors and windows.

Adding all openings: 21 sq. ft. + 24 sq. ft. + 35 sq. ft. = 80 sq. ft.

Step 6: Calculate the total wall area before subtracting doors and windows.

Multiplying number of walls by solid wall area: 4 × 56 sq. ft. = 224 sq. ft.

Step 7: Calculate the total area Linda will have to paint.

Subtracting openings from total wall area: 224 sq. ft. - 80 sq. ft. = 144 sq. ft.

Final Answer:

144
