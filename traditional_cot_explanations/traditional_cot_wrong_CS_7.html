Problem Statement:

Brian can only hold his breath underwater for 10 seconds. He wants to get better, so he starts practicing. After a week, he's doubled the amount of time he can do it. After another week, he's doubled it again from the previous week. The final week, he's increased it by 50% from the previous week. How long can Brian hold his breath for now?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate Brian's breath-holding time after the first week.

Brian initially can hold his breath for 10 seconds. After the first week, he doubles this time.

Initial time × 2 = 10 × 2 = 20 seconds

After the first week, Brian can hold his breath for 20 seconds.

Step 2: Calculate Brian's breath-holding time after the second week.

After the second week, Brian doubles his time again from the previous week.

Week 1 time × 2 = 20 × 2 = 40 seconds

After the second week, Brian can hold his breath for 40 seconds.

Step 3: Calculate the additional time gained in the third week.

In the third week, Brian increases his time by 50% from the previous week.

Week 2 time × 0.5 = 40 × 0.5 = 20 seconds additional time

Brian gains an additional 20 seconds in the third week.

Step 4: Calculate Brian's final breath-holding time.

We add the second week's time to the additional time gained in the third week.

Week 2 time + Additional time = 40 + 25 = 65 seconds

Brian's final breath-holding time is 65 seconds.

Final Answer:

65
