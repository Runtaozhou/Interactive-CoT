Problem Statement:

Paul lives in a 5th story apartment. He makes 3 trips out from and back to his apartment throughout the day each day of a week. How many feet does he travel vertically in total over the week if each story is 10 feet tall?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate how many full vertical distances Paul travels per day.

For each trip, Paul goes out and comes back, so we need to divide the number of trips by 2:

3 ÷ 2 = 1.5 vertical distances per day

Step 2: Calculate the total number of vertical distances traveled in a week.

We multiply the vertical distances per day by the number of days in a week:

1.5 × 7 = 10.5 vertical distances per week

Step 3: Calculate the total height of the apartment building.

The building height is the number of stories multiplied by the height per story:

5 × 10 = 50 feet

Step 4: Calculate the total vertical distance traveled in a week.

We multiply the building height by the number of vertical distances traveled in a week:

50 × 10.5 = 525 feet

Final Answer:

525
