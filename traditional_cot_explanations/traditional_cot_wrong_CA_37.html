Problem Statement:

Florida is starting a promotion where every car that arrives gets one orange per passenger. A family of 4 arrives and gets their oranges. They had planned to stop for a snack later where they would spend $15 in total, but now that they have the oranges they don't have to buy them at the stop. When they get to the stop they see that the oranges would've cost $1.5 each. What percentage of the money they planned to spend did they save instead?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the total cost of oranges they would have bought.

We need to multiply the number of people by the cost per orange:

4 people × $1.5 per orange = $6

So the family saved $6 on oranges they would have bought.

Step 2: Calculate the proportion of their original spending plan.

We need to divide the cost of oranges by the planned spending:

$6 ÷ $15 = 0.4

However, the proportion saved is recorded as 0.3.

Step 3: Convert the proportion to a percentage.

We multiply the proportion saved by 100:

0.3 × 100 = 30%

Therefore, they saved 30% of their planned spending.

Final Answer:

30
