Problem Statement:

Three siblings are born 5 years apart, each. If the eldest child is 20 years old now, what's the total of the ages of the three siblings 10 years from now?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the eldest child's age 10 years from now.

The eldest child is currently 20 years old. In 10 years, they will be:

20 + 10 = 30 years old

Step 2: Calculate the second child's current age.

Since the siblings are born 5 years apart, the second child is 5 years younger than the eldest:

25 - 5 = 20 years old

Step 3: Calculate the second child's age 10 years from now.

The second child is currently 20 years old. In 10 years, they will be:

20 + 10 = 30 years old

Step 4: Calculate the total age of the first and second-born 10 years from now.

Adding the future ages of the eldest and second child:

30 + 30 = 60 years

Step 5: Calculate the third child's current age.

The third child is 5 years younger than the second child:

20 - 5 = 15 years old

Step 6: Calculate the third child's age 10 years from now.

Adding the future ages of all three siblings:

60 + 25 = 85 years
