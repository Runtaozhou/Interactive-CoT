Problem Statement:

Francis' family has 2 ukuleles, 4 guitars, and a certain number of violins. Each ukulele has 4 strings. Each guitar has 6 strings. Each violin has 4 strings. If the total number of strings among the ukuleles, guitars, and violins is 40 strings, how many violins does Francis' family have?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the total number of strings from the guitars.

We know there are 4 guitars, and each guitar has 6 strings.

Number of guitars × Strings per guitar = 4 × 6 = 24 strings

So, the guitars have a total of 24 strings.

Step 2: Find the number of strings from the violins by subtracting guitar strings from the total.

The total number of strings is 40, and the guitars account for 24 strings.

Total strings - Guitar strings = 40 - 24 = 16 strings

So, the violins have a total of 16 strings.

Step 3: Calculate the number of violins by dividing the violin strings by strings per violin.

Each violin has 4 strings, and we have 16 strings in total from violins.

Violin strings ÷ Strings per violin = 16 ÷ 4 = 4 violins

Therefore, Francis' family has 4 violins.

Final Answer:

4
