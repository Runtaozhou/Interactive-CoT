Problem Statement:

Karl sells clothing in his store. He sells a T-shirt that costs $5, some pants that cost $4, and some skirts that cost $6, he also sells some refurbished t-shirts that cost half the original price. How much is his total income if he sold two T-shirts, one pair of pants, four skirts, and six refurbished T-shirts?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the price of a refurbished T-shirt.

Since refurbished T-shirts cost half the original price, and the original T-shirt costs $5:

Refurbished T-shirt price = $5 ÷ 2 = $2.5

Step 2: Calculate the income from refurbished T-shirts.

Income from refurbished T-shirts = Price per refurbished T-shirt × Number sold

Income from refurbished T-shirts = $2.5 × 6 = $15

Step 3: Calculate the income from regular T-shirts.

Income from regular T-shirts = Price per T-shirt × Number sold

Income from regular T-shirts = $5 × 2 = $10

Step 4: Calculate the income from pants.

Income from pants = Price per pair of pants × Number sold

Income from pants = $4 × 1 = $4

Step 5: Calculate the income from skirts.

Income from skirts = Price per skirt × Number sold

Income from skirts = $6 × 4 = $23

Step 6: Calculate the total income.

Total income = Income from refurbished T-shirts + Income from regular T-shirts + Income from pants + Income from skirts

Total income = $15 + $10 + $4 + $23 = $52

Final Answer:

52
