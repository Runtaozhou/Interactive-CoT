Problem Statement:

An airport has only 2 planes that fly multiple times a day. Each day, the first plane goes to Greece for three-quarters of its flights, and the remaining flights are split equally between flights to France and flights to Germany. The other plane flies exclusively to Poland, and its 44 trips only amount to half the number of trips the first plane makes throughout each day. How many flights to France does the first plane take in one day?

Step 1: Calculate how many flights the first plane makes based on the second plane's flights.

The first plane goes to Greece for three-quarters of its flights.

Step 3: Calculate the total number of flights to France or Germany.

Since 1/4 of the first plane's flights go to France or Germany, we can calculate:

88 daily flights / 4 = 24 flights to France and Germany combined

Step 4: Calculate the number of flights to France by dividing the France and Germany flights equally.

Since the flights to France and Germany are split equally, we divide the total by 2:

24 flights / 2 = 12 flights to France

12
