Problem Statement:

Wendy works at a chocolate factory packing chocolates. She can package 2 dozen chocolates in 5 minutes. How many individual chocolates can she package in 4 hours?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate how many 5-minute increments are in 1 hour.

We need to divide the minutes in an hour by 5 minutes:

50 minutes ÷ 5 minutes = 10 increments per hour

Step 2: Calculate how many individual chocolates are in 2 dozen.

Since 1 dozen equals 12 chocolates:

12 chocolates per dozen × 2 dozen = 24 chocolates

Step 3: Calculate how many chocolates can be packaged in 1 hour.

We multiply the number of 5-minute increments in an hour by the number of chocolates packaged in each increment:

10 increments × 24 chocolates = 240 individual chocolates per hour

Step 4: Calculate how many chocolates can be packaged in 4 hours.

We multiply the chocolates packaged per hour by the total number of hours:

240 chocolates per hour × 4 hours = 960 individual chocolates

Final Answer:

960
