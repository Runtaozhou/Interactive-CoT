Problem Statement:

John climbs 3 staircases. The first staircase has 20 steps. The next has twice as many steps as the first. The final staircase has 10 fewer steps than the second one. Each step is 0.5 feet. How many feet did he climb?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the number of steps in the second staircase.

The second staircase has twice as many steps as the first staircase.

First staircase steps = 20 steps

Second staircase steps = 20 × 2 = 40 steps

Step 2: Calculate the number of steps in the third staircase.

The third staircase has 10 fewer steps than the second staircase.

Second staircase steps = 40 steps

Third staircase steps = 40 - 10 = 30 steps

Step 3: Calculate the total number of steps climbed.

We need to add up all the steps from the three staircases.

Total steps = First staircase + Second staircase + Third staircase

Total steps = 20 + 40 + 30 = 90 steps

Step 4: Convert the total steps to feet.

Each step is 0.5 feet high.

Total height = Total steps × Step height

Total height = 90 × 0.5 = 50 feet

Final Answer:

50
