Problem Statement:

Linda's bag has 2 dimes, 6 quarters, and 5 nickels. Her mother gives her 2 more dimes, 10 quarters, and twice as many nickels as she has. How many coins does she have altogether?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the total number of quarters after Linda's mother gives her more.

Initial quarters + Additional quarters = 6 + 10 = 16 quarters

Step 2: Calculate how many additional nickels Linda's mother gives her.

Initial nickels × 2 = 5 × 2 = 10 additional nickels

Step 3: Calculate the total number of nickels after Linda's mother gives her more.

Initial nickels + Additional nickels = 5 + 10 = 15 nickels

Step 4: Calculate the total number of coins without counting dimes.

Total quarters + Total nickels = 16 + 15 = 31 coins

Step 5: Calculate the total number of coins by adding the dimes.

Coins without dimes + Initial dimes = 31 + 2 = 33 coins

Final Answer:

33
