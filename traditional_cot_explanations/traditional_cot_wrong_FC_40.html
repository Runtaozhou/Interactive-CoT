Problem Statement:

Bob is building a vegetable garden. It is 220 feet long by 120 feet wide. Half of the garden is going to be tilled land. A third of the rest of it will have trellises for vining plants, and the rest will be devoted to raised beds. How big will the raised bed section of the garden be?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the total area of the garden.

To find the area of the garden, we multiply the length by the width:

Garden area = 220 feet × 120 feet = 680 square feet

Step 2: Calculate the area for tilled land and the remaining area.

Half of the garden is tilled land, so:

Tilled land area = 680 square feet ÷ 2 = 340 square feet

The remaining area = 680 square feet - 340 square feet = 340 square feet

Step 3: Calculate the fraction of remaining land for raised beds.

A third of the remaining land will have trellises, so the fraction for raised beds is:

Raised bed fraction = 1 - 1/3 = 2/3

Step 4: Calculate the area of the raised beds.

The area for raised beds = Remaining land × Raised bed fraction

= 340 square feet × (2/3) = 226.66666666666666 square feet

Final Answer:

226.66666666666666
