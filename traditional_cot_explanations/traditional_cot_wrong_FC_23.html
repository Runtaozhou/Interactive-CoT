Problem Statement:

John builds a box. The box is 26 inches by 26 inches by 14 inches. The walls are 1 inch thick on each side. How much is the internal volume in cubic feet?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate how much the walls reduce each dimension. Since the walls are 1 inch thick on each side, we need to account for both sides.
Wall reduction = thickness × 2 = 1 inch × 2 = 2 inches
Step 2: Find the internal length of the longer sides by subtracting the wall reduction from the external length.
Internal length = external length - wall reduction = 26 inches - 2 inches = 24 inches
Step 3: Convert the internal length to feet for volume calculation.
Internal length in feet = internal length ÷ 12 = 24 inches ÷ 12 = 2 feet
Step 4: Find the internal length of the smaller dimension by subtracting the wall reduction.
Internal height = external smaller dimension - wall reduction = 14 inches - 2 inches = 12 inches
Step 5: Convert the internal height to feet.
Internal height in feet = internal height ÷ 12 = 12 inches ÷ 12 = 1 foot
Step 6: Calculate the internal volume using the formula.
Internal volume = (internal_length_feet × internal_length_feet) + (internal_length_feet × internal_height_feet) + (internal_height_feet × internal_length_feet)
= (2 feet × 2 feet) + (2 feet × 1 foot) + (1 foot × 2 feet) = 8 cubic feet

Final Answer:

8
