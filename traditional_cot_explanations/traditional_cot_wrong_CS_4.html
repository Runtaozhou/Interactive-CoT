Problem Statement:

Three cats sat on a fence, meowing at the moon. The first cat meowed 3 times per minute. The second cat meowed twice as frequently as the first cat. And the third cat meowed at one-third the frequency of the second cat. What is the combined total number of meows the three cats make in 5 minutes?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate how frequently the second cat meows per minute.

The first cat meows 3 times per minute, and the second cat meows twice as frequently.

Second cat's meow rate = 3 × 2 = 6 meows per minute

Step 2: Calculate how frequently the third cat meows per minute.

The third cat meows at one-third the frequency of the second cat.

Third cat's meow rate = 6 ÷ 3 = 2 meows per minute

Step 3: Calculate the combined meow rate per minute for all three cats.

We add up the meow rates of all three cats.

Combined meow rate = 3 + 6 + 2 = 11 meows per minute

Step 4: Calculate the total number of meows in 5 minutes.

We multiply the combined rate by the total time.

Total meows = 5 × 12 = 60 meows

Final Answer:

60
