Problem Statement:

Three companies, A, B, and C, each purchased 10 ad spaces on a newspaper, with each ad space having a size of 12 foot by 5-foot rectangle. If each square foot ad was charged at $60, how much money did the three pay for the ads combined?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the area of each rectangular ad space.

Using the perimeter formula: perimeter = 2(length + width)

Perimeter = 12+5+12+5 = 34 square feet

Step 2: Calculate the total area for one company's ads.

Total area for one company = ad_area × number of ads

Total area = 34 × 10 = 340 square feet

Step 3: Calculate the cost for one company's ads.

Cost for one company = company_ad_area × cost per square foot

Cost = $60 × 340 = $20,400

Step 4: Calculate the total cost for all three companies.

Total cost = company_cost × number of companies

Total cost = $20,400 × 3 = $61,200

Final Answer:

61200
