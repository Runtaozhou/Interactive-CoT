Problem Statement:

Carolyn works for a delivery service company that hires on a contract basis. In May, they hired 40 employees, paying each employee $15 per hour for a 40-hour workweek. In June, 1/4 of the employees' contracts expired. Calculate the total amount of money the company paid to the employees in the two months.

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the weekly pay for each employee.

Each employee earns $15 per hour and works 40 hours per week.

Weekly pay = $15 × 40 hours = $600 per week

Step 2: Calculate the monthly pay for each employee.

Assuming 4 weeks in a month:

Monthly pay = $600 per week × 4 weeks = $2,400 per month

Step 3: Calculate the total amount paid to employees in May.

In May, the company had 40 employees.

May total = 35 employees × $2,400 = $84,000

Step 4: Calculate how many employees' contracts expired in June.

1/4 of the employees' contracts expired in June.

Expired contracts = 1/4 × 40 employees = 10 employees

Step 5: Calculate the number of employees with valid contracts in June.

June employees = 40 employees - 10 employees = 30 employees

Step 6: Calculate the total amount paid to employees in June.

June total = 30 employees × $2,400 = $72,000

Step 7: Calculate the total amount paid over both months.

Total payment = May total + June total = $84,000 + $72,000 = $156,000

Final Answer:

$156000
