Problem Statement:

Hot dog buns come in packages of 8. For the school picnic, Mr. Gates bought 30 packages of hot dog buns. He has four classes with 30 students in each class. How many hot dog buns can each of Mr. Gates' students get?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the total number of hot dog buns Mr. Gates bought.

We need to multiply the number of packages by the number of buns in each package:

Number of packages × Buns per package = 30 × 8 = 240 hot dog buns

So Mr. Gates bought a total of 240 hot dog buns.

Step 2: Calculate the total number of students in Mr. Gates' classes.

We need to multiply the number of classes by the number of students in each class:

Number of classes × Students per class = 4 × 30 = 160 students

So there are 160 students in total.

Step 3: Calculate how many hot dog buns each student can get.

We need to divide the total number of buns by the total number of students:

Total buns ÷ Total students = 240 ÷ 160 = 1.5 hot dog buns

Each student can get 1.5 hot dog buns.

Final Answer:

1.5
