Problem Statement:

Vikki worked 42 hours in one week. Her hourly pay rate is $10. From her weekly earnings, 20% is deducted as tax, 5% is deducted as insurance cover, and $5 is deducted for union dues. How much money, in dollars, does Vikki take home after deductions?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate Vikki's weekly earnings.

To find her weekly earnings, we multiply her hourly pay rate by the number of hours worked:

Weekly earnings = $10 × 42 hours = $420

Step 2: Calculate the tax deduction.

The tax deduction is 20% of her weekly earnings:

Tax deduction = $420 × 0.2 = $84

Step 3: Calculate the insurance deduction.

The insurance deduction is 5% of her weekly earnings:

Insurance deduction = $420 × 0.05 = $21

Step 4: Identify the union dues deduction.

The union dues deduction is a fixed amount of $5.

Step 5: Calculate the total deductions.

We add all the deductions together:

Total deductions = $84 + $21 + $5 = $110

Step 6: Calculate Vikki's take-home pay.

We subtract the total deductions from her weekly earnings:

Take-home pay = $420 - $110 = $310

Final Answer:

310
