Problem Statement:

Jill is going to resod her front yard. The plot of land in front of her house is 200 feet wide by 50 feet. Her house has a sidewalk that is 3 feet wide and 50 feet long. There are also four flower beds: two right in front of the house that are 4 feet deep by 25 feet long, one that is 10 feet by 12 feet, and a fourth that is 7 feet by 8 feet. How many square feet of sod will she need to cover her yard, minus the sidewalk and flower beds?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the total area of the front yard.

To find the total area, we multiply the width by the length:

Total yard area = 200 ft × 50 ft = 10,000 sq.ft.

Step 2: Calculate the area of the sidewalk.

The sidewalk area is found by multiplying its width by its length:

Sidewalk area = 3 ft × 50 ft = 150 sq.ft.

Step 3: Calculate the area of the two flower beds in front of the house.

Each flower bed has an area of depth × length:

One flower bed = 4 ft × 25 ft = 100 sq.ft.

Since there are two identical flower beds: 2 × 100 sq.ft. = 200 sq.ft.

Step 4: Calculate the area of the third flower bed.

The third flower bed area is:

10 ft × 12 ft = 120 sq.ft.

Step 5: Calculate the area of the fourth flower bed.

The fourth flower bed area is:

7 ft × 8 ft = 56 sq.ft.

Step 6: Calculate the total area of the sidewalk and flower beds.

We add all the areas that don't need sod:

Total excluded area = 150 sq.ft + 200 sq.ft + 120 sq.ft + 56 sq.ft = 526 sq.ft.

Step 7: Calculate the area that needs sod by subtracting the excluded areas from the total yard area.

Sod area needed = Total yard area - Total excluded area

Sod area needed = 10,000 sq.ft - 526 sq.ft = 9,474 sq.ft.

Final Answer:

9474
