Problem Statement:

There are enough provisions in a castle to feed 300 people for 90 days. After 30 days, 100 people leave the castle. How many more days are left until all the food runs out?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the remaining food after 30 days

We need to determine how many days of food are left after the first 30 days have passed.

Total days of food initially: 90 days

Days already passed: 30 days

Remaining days for the original group: 90 days - 30 days = 60 days

Step 2: Calculate the number of people remaining in the castle

After 30 days, 100 people leave the castle.

Original number of people: 300 people

People who left: 100 people

Remaining people: 300 - 100 = 200 people

Step 3: Calculate the food consumption rate of the smaller group relative to the original group

With fewer people, the food will last longer. We need to calculate the ratio of consumption.

Consumption rate = Remaining people / Original people

Consumption rate = 200/300 = 2/3

This means the remaining group consumes food at 2/3 the rate of the original group.

Step 4: Calculate how long the remaining food will last for the smaller group

Since the consumption rate is now 2/3 of the original, the food will last longer.

Days until food runs out = Remaining days for original group / Consumption rate

Days until food runs out = 70 days / (2/3) = 105 more days

Final Answer:

105
