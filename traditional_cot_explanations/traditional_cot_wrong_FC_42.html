Problem Statement:

Mary is making a model sailboat. She wants to add three sails: a rectangular sail that measures 5 inches by 8 inches and two right triangular sails, one that's 3 inches long at the bottom and 4 inches tall and one that's 4 inches long at the bottom and 6 inches tall. How many square inches of canvas does she need total?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: First find the area of the square sail.

To find the perimeter of the rectangular sail, we add all sides:

5 inches + 5 inches + 8 inches + 8 inches = 26 square inches

So the rectangular sail area is 26 square inches.

Step 2: Then find the area of a square sail with the same height and length as the first triangular sail.

Using the formula length × height:

3 inches × 4 inches = 12 square inches

The area of a square with these dimensions would be 12 square inches.

Step 3: Then divide the area in two to find the area of the triangular sail.

Using the formula square area ÷ 2:

12 square inches ÷ 2 = 6 square inches

So the first triangular sail has an area of 6 square inches.

Step 4: Then find the area of a square sail with the same height and length as the second triangular sail.

Using the formula length × height:

4 inches × 6 inches = 24 square inches

The area of a square with these dimensions would be 24 square inches.

Step 5: Then divide the area in two to find the area of the triangular sail.

Using the formula square area ÷ 2:

24 square inches ÷ 2 = 12 square inches

So the second triangular sail has an area of 12 square inches.

Step 6: Then add up the areas of all the sails to find the total amount of canvas needed.

Adding all sail areas:

6 square inches + 12 square inches + 26 square inches = 44 square inches

The total canvas needed is 44 square inches.

Final Answer:

44
