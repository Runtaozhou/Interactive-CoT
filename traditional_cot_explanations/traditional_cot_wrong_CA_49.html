Problem Statement:

Out of the 80 students who took the biology exam, only 2/5 of them managed to score 100%. If a score below 80% qualified as failing, and 50 percent of the remaining students who didn't score 100% managed to score over 80%, calculate the number of students who failed the exam.

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the number of students who scored 100%

We need to find how many students scored 100% on the exam.

Total students × Fraction scoring 100% = 80 × 2/5 = 32 students

Step 2: Find the number of students who did not score 100%

We need to determine how many students scored less than 100%.

Total students - Students with 100% = 80 - 32 = 48 students

Step 3: Calculate the number of students who scored over 80% but below 100%

We know that 50% of the remaining students scored over 80% but below 100%.

Percentage of remaining students scoring over 80% × Students without 100% = 50/100 × 48 = 23 students

Step 4: Calculate the number of students who failed (scored below 80%)

Now we can find how many students failed the exam by subtracting those who scored over 80% from those who didn't score 100%.

Students without 100% - Students over 80% = 48 - 23 = 25 students

Final Answer:

25
