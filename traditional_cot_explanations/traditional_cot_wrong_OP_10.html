Problem Statement:

Andy gets a cavity for every 4 candy canes he eats. He gets 2 candy canes from his parents and 3 candy canes each from 4 teachers. Then he uses his allowance to buy 1/7 as many candy canes as he was given. How many cavities does he get from eating all his candy canes?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: First, let's calculate how many candy canes Andy gets from his teachers.
Each teacher gives him 3 candy canes, and there are 4 teachers.
3 candy canes/teacher × 4 teachers = 12 candy canes
Step 2: Now, let's add the candy canes Andy gets from his parents to find the total number of candy canes he was given.
Candy canes from teachers + Candy canes from parents
12 candy canes + 2 candy canes = 14 candy canes
Step 3: Next, we need to find how many candy canes Andy buys with his allowance.
He buys 1/7 as many candy canes as he was given, so we multiply the total given by 7.
14 candy canes × 7 = 98 candy canes
Step 4: Now, let's add all the candy canes to find the total number Andy has.
Candy canes bought + Candy canes given
98 candy canes + 14 candy canes = 112 candy canes
Step 5: Finally, let's calculate how many cavities Andy gets.
Since he gets 1 cavity for every 4 candy canes, we divide the total number of candy canes by 4.
112 candy canes ÷ 4 candy canes/cavity = 28 cavities

Final Answer:

28
