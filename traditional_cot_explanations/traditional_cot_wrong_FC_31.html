Problem Statement:

Bill gets a flat-screen TV that measures 48 inches by 100 inches. His brother Bob gets a TV that measures 70 inches by 60 inches. If the TVs weigh 4 oz per square inch of screen space, how much more does the heavier TV weigh in pounds? (There are 16 ounces per pound).

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Find the screen area of Bill's TV.

To find the area, we calculate the perimeter of the TV:

48 inches + 100 inches + 48 inches + 100 inches = 296 square inches

Step 2: Find the screen area of Bob's TV.

For Bob's TV, we multiply the length by the width:

70 inches × 60 inches = 4200 square inches

Step 3: Find the difference in screen area between the TVs.

We subtract the smaller area from the larger area:

4200 square inches - 296 square inches = 3904 square inches

Step 4: Calculate the weight difference in ounces.

We multiply the area difference by the weight per square inch:

3904 square inches × 4 oz/square inch = 15616 oz

Step 5: Convert the weight difference to pounds.

We divide the weight in ounces by the number of ounces in a pound:

15616 oz ÷ 16 oz/pound = 975.5 pounds

Final Answer:

975.5
