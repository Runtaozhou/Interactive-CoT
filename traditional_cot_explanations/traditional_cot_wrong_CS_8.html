Problem Statement:

Bill picked 50 apples from the orchard with his wife and two children. He sends each of his kids to school with 3 apples for their two favorite teachers. His wife Jill bakes two apple pies, using 10 apples per pie. How many apples does Bill have left?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate how many apples Bill gives to his children.

Bill has 2 children, and each child gets 3 apples to take to school.

Number of apples for children = 3 × 2 = 6 apples

Step 2: Calculate how many apples Jill uses for the pies.

Jill bakes 2 pies, and each pie requires 10 apples.

Number of apples for pies = 10 × 2 = 20 apples

Step 3: Calculate the total number of apples used.

We need to add the apples given to children and the apples used for pies.

Total apples used = 20 + 6 = 26 apples

Step 4: Calculate how many apples Bill has left.

We subtract the apples used for pies from the starting number of apples.

Apples remaining = 50 - 20 = 30 apples

Final Answer:

30
