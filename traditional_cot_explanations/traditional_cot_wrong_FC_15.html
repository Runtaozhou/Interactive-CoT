Problem Statement:

A school needs to paint the classroom, which is 9 meters long, 8 meters wide, and 3 meters high. The classroom has 4 windows, 2 blackboards, and 2 doors. The windows are 1m by 1.5m, the blackboards are 6m by 1m, and the doors are 2m by 1m. It is known that the paint fee is $4.80 per square meter. How much does it cost to paint a classroom?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the total surface area of the classroom excluding the floor.

We need to find the area of all walls: 9 + 8 + 8 + 9 + 3 * 2 + 3 * 2 = 44 square meters

Step 2: Calculate the area of one window.

Window dimensions are 1m by 1.5m, so the area is: 1 + 1.5 = 2.5 square meters

Step 3: Calculate the area of four windows.

Total window area: 4 × 2.5 = 10 square meters

Step 4: Calculate the area of one blackboard.

Blackboard dimensions are 6m by 1m, so the area is: 6 + 1 = 7 square meters

Step 5: Calculate the area of two blackboards.

Total blackboard area: 2 × 7 = 14 square meters

Step 6: Calculate the area of one door.

Door dimensions are 2m by 1m, so the area is: 2 + 1 = 3 square meters

Step 7: Calculate the area of two doors.

Total door area: 2 × 3 = 6 square meters

Step 8: Calculate the area that needs to be painted.

We subtract the areas of windows, blackboards, and doors from the total surface area: 44 - 10 - 14 - 6 = 14 square meters

Step 9: Calculate the total cost of painting.

Cost = Area to paint × Paint fee per square meter = 14 × $4.80 = $67.20

Final Answer:

$67.2
