Problem Statement:

Every day, Lou works out by running three miles on a circular track that is one-quarter of a mile long. His wife, Rosie, also runs on the same track at the same time as her husband, but she runs at twice the speed of her husband. During their workout, how many times does Rosie circle the track?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate how many times Lou circles the track.

To find this, we divide Lou's total running distance by the length of one lap around the track.

Lou's distance ÷ track length = 3 miles ÷ (1/4 mile) = 12 times

So Lou circles the track 12 times during his workout.

Step 2: Calculate how far Rosie runs in the same time period.

Since Rosie runs at twice the speed of Lou, she covers twice the distance in the same amount of time.

Lou's distance × Rosie's speed ratio = 3 miles × 2 = 8 miles

So Rosie runs a total of 8 miles during their workout.

Step 3: Calculate how many times Rosie circles the track.

To find this, we divide Rosie's total running distance by the length of one lap around the track.

Rosie's distance ÷ track length = 8 miles ÷ (1/4 mile) = 32 times

So Rosie circles the track 32 times during their workout.

Final Answer:

32
