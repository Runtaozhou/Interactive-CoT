Problem Statement:

The math questions in a contest are divided into three rounds: easy, average, and hard. There are corresponding points given for each round. That is 2, 3, and 5 points for every correct answer in the easy, average, and hard rounds, respectively. Suppose Kim got 6 correct answers in the easy; 2 correct answers in the average; and 4 correct answers in the difficult round, what are her total points in the contest?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate Kim's points in the easy round.

We know that each correct answer in the easy round is worth 2 points, and Kim got 6 correct answers.

Easy round points = 6 × 2 = 12 points

Step 2: Calculate Kim's points in the average round.

Each correct answer in the average round is worth 3 points, and Kim got 2 correct answers.

Average round points = 2 points/round × 3 rounds = 6 points

Step 3: Calculate Kim's points in the difficult round.

Each correct answer in the difficult round is worth 5 points, and Kim got 4 correct answers.

Difficult round points = 4 points/round × 5 rounds = 20 points

Step 4: Calculate the total points from all rounds.

We add up the points from all three rounds:

Total points = 6*2 points + 6 points + 20 points = 32 points

Final Answer:

32
