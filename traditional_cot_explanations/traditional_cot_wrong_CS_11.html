Problem Statement:

James has a small tree outside his window with 30 branches with 90 twigs per branch. 30% of the twigs sprout 4 leaves and the rest sprout 5 leaves. How many leaves are on the tree total?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: First, we need to find the total number of twigs on the tree.
Total twigs = Number of branches × Twigs per branch
Total twigs = 30 branches × 90 twigs/branch = 2,700 twigs
Step 2: Next, we need to determine how many twigs have 4 leaves.
Twigs with 4 leaves = Total twigs × 30%
Twigs with 4 leaves = 2,700 twigs × 0.30 = 810 twigs
Step 3: Now we can find how many twigs have 5 leaves by subtracting.
Twigs with 5 leaves = Total twigs - Twigs with 4 leaves
Twigs with 5 leaves = 2,700 twigs - 800 twigs = 1,900 twigs
Step 4: Calculate the total number of leaves on the twigs with 5 leaves.
Leaves from 5-leaf twigs = Number of twigs with 5 leaves × 5 leaves per twig
Leaves from 5-leaf twigs = 1,900 twigs × 5 leaves/twig = 9,500 leaves
Step 5: Calculate the total number of leaves on the twigs with 4 leaves.
Leaves from 4-leaf twigs = Number of twigs with 4 leaves × 4 leaves per twig
Leaves from 4-leaf twigs = 810 twigs × 4 leaves/twig = 3,240 leaves
Step 6: Finally, add the two groups of leaves to find the total number of leaves on the tree.
Total leaves = Leaves from 5-leaf twigs + Leaves from 4-leaf twigs
Total leaves = 9,500 leaves + 3,240 leaves = 12,740 leaves
