Problem Statement:

Three times the sum of marbles that Atticus, Jensen, and Cruz have is equal to 60. If Atticus has half as many marbles as Jensen, and Atticus has 4 marbles, how many marbles does Cruz have?

Step By Step Explanation:

Step 1: Calculate the total number of marbles by dividing the triple sum by 3.
We know that three times the sum of marbles equals 60.
Total marbles = 60 ÷ 3 = 180
Step 2: Calculate how many marbles Jensen has based on Atticus's marbles.
Since Atticus has half as many marbles as Jensen, Jensen has twice as many as Atticus.
Jensen's marbles = Atticus's marbles × 2 = 4 × 2 = 8 marbles
Step 3: Calculate the combined marbles of Atticus and Jensen.
Atticus and Jensen together have:
Atticus's marbles + Jensen's marbles = 4 + 8 = 12 marbles
Step 4: Calculate Cruz's marbles by subtracting Atticus and Jensen's marbles from the total.
Cruz's marbles = Total marbles - (Atticus's marbles + Jensen's marbles)
Cruz's marbles = 180 - 12 = 168 marbles

Final Answer:

168
