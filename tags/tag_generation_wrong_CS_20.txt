## 1. Problem statement

In a factory, there are <fact1>3 machines working 23 hours a day</fact1>. The owner decided to buy <fact2>a fourth machine, which works only 12 hours a day</fact2>. <fact3>One machine can produce 2 kg of material every hour</fact3>. The factory sells the produced material for <fact4>$50 per 1 kg</fact4>. How much can this factory earn in one day?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>First three machines: 3 machines working 23 hours a day</fact1>
<fact2>Fourth machine: works 12 hours a day</fact2>
<fact3>Production rate: 2 kg of material per hour per machine</fact3>
<fact4>Selling price: $50 per 1 kg</fact4>

## 3. Steps

<step1> Calculate the total runtime for the first three machines </step1>
<formula> number of machines × hours per day </formula>
<calculation> 3 machines * 23 hours/day/machine = 69 hours/day </calculation>
<var> first_three_machines_runtime : 69 hours/day </var>

<step2> Calculate how much material the first three machines produce </step2>
<formula> total runtime × production rate </formula>
<calculation> 69 hours/day * 2 kg/hour = 138 kg/day </calculation>
<var> first_three_machines_production : 138 kg/day </var>

<step3> Calculate how much material the fourth machine produces </step3>
<formula> runtime × production rate </formula>
<calculation> 12 hours/day * 2 kg/hour = 24 kg/day </calculation>
<var> fourth_machine_production : 24 kg/day </var>

<step4> Calculate the total production of all machines </step4>
<formula> first three machines production + fourth machine production </formula>
<calculation> 138 kg/day + 24 kg/day = 156 kg/day </calculation>
<var> total_production : 156 kg/day </var>

<step5> Calculate the total earnings </step5>
<formula> total production × price per kg </formula>
<calculation> 156 kg/day * $50/kg = $7800/day </calculation>
<var> total_earnings : $7800/day </var>
