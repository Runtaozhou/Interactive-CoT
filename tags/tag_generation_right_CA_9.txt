## 1. Problem statement

<fact1>Pam rode the roller coaster 2 times</fact1> while <fact2>Fred rode it 4 times</fact2>. After that, <fact3>each of them decided to ride the luge 2 times</fact3>. If <fact4>each ride cost 6 tickets</fact4>, how many tickets did they use that day?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Pam's roller coaster rides: 2</fact1>
<fact2>Fred's roller coaster rides: 4</fact2>
<fact3>Each person's luge rides: 2</fact3>
<fact4>Cost per ride: 6 tickets</fact4>

## 3. Steps

<step1>The total times that they ride the roller coaster is 2+4=6 times.</step1>
<formula>Pam's roller coaster rides + Fred's roller coaster rides</formula>
<calculation>2 + 4 = 6</calculation>
<var>total_roller_coaster_rides: 6</var>

<step2>The total times that they ride the luge is 2+2=4 times.</step2>
<formula>Pam's luge rides + Fred's luge rides</formula>
<calculation>2 + 2 = 4</calculation>
<var>total_luge_rides: 4</var>

<step3>The total times they ride that day is 6+4=10 times.</step3>
<formula>total_roller_coaster_rides + total_luge_rides</formula>
<calculation>6 + 4 = 10</calculation>
<var>total_rides: 10</var>

<step4>So, Pam and Fred used 10x6=60 tickets that day.</step4>
<formula>total_rides × cost per ride</formula>
<calculation>10 × 6 = 60</calculation>
<var>total_tickets: 60</var>

## 4. Wrongsteps

<wrongstep>4</wrongstep>

## 5. Output

<output>60</output>
