## 1. Problem statement

Rebecca bought <fact1>2 pies</fact1> for the holiday weekend. Each pie was sliced into <fact2>8 slices</fact2>. Rebecca ate <fact3>1 slice of each pie</fact3>. Her family and friends ate <fact4>50% of the remaining pies</fact4> over the weekend. On Sunday evening Rebecca and her husband each had <fact5>another slice of pie</fact5>. How many slices are remaining?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Number of pies: 2</fact1>
<fact2>Slices per pie: 8</fact2>
<fact3>Rebecca's initial consumption: 1 slice of each pie</fact3>
<fact4>Family and friends consumption: 50% of remaining pies</fact4>
<fact5>Sunday evening consumption: 1 slice each for Rebecca and husband</fact5>

## 3. Steps

<step1> Calculate the total number of slices from all pies </step1>
<formula> number of pies × slices per pie </formula>
<calculation> 2*8 = 16 slices </calculation>
<var> total_slices : 16 slices </var>

<step2> Subtract the slices Rebecca initially ate </step2>
<formula> total_slices - slices eaten by Rebecca </formula>
<calculation> 16-2 = 14 slices </calculation>
<var> remaining_after_initial : 14 slices </var>

<step3> Calculate how many slices family and friends ate </step3>
<formula> remaining_after_initial × percentage eaten </formula>
<calculation> 14*.50 = 7 slices </calculation>
<var> family_friends_ate : 7 slices </var>

<step4> Calculate remaining slices after family and friends ate </step4>
<formula> remaining_after_initial - family_friends_ate </formula>
<calculation> 14-7 = 7 slices </calculation>
<var> remaining_before_sunday : 7 slices </var>

<step5> Subtract the slices eaten by Rebecca and her husband on Sunday </step5>
<formula> remaining_before_sunday - slices eaten on Sunday </formula>
<calculation> 7-3 = 4 slices </calculation>
<var> final_remaining : 4 slices </var>

## 4. Wrongsteps

<wrongstep> 5 </wrongstep>

## 5. Output

<output> 4 </output>
