## 1. Problem statement

Tony exercises every morning by <fact1>walking 3 miles carrying a 25-pound backpack</fact1>, then he <fact2>runs another 10 miles without the backpack</fact2>. If he <fact3>walks at a speed of 3 miles per hour</fact3> and <fact4>runs at a speed of 5 miles per hour</fact4>, how many hours each week does he spend exercising?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Walking distance: 3 miles with 25-pound backpack</fact1>
<fact2>Running distance: 10 miles without backpack</fact2>
<fact3>Walking speed: 3 miles per hour</fact3>
<fact4>Running speed: 5 miles per hour</fact4>

## 3. Steps

<step1> Calculate the time Tony spends walking each day </step1>
<formula> distance ÷ speed </formula>
<calculation> 3 ÷ 4 = 0.75 hour </calculation>
<var> daily_walking_time : 0.75 hour </var>

<step2> Calculate the time Tony spends running each day </step2>
<formula> distance ÷ speed </formula>
<calculation> 10 ÷ 5 = 2 hours </calculation>
<var> daily_running_time : 2 hours </var>

<step3> Calculate the total daily exercise time </step3>
<formula> daily_walking_time + daily_running_time </formula>
<calculation> 0.75 + 2 = 2.75 hours </calculation>
<var> daily_total_time : 2.75 hours </var>

<step4> Calculate the weekly exercise time </step4>
<formula> daily_total_time × 7 days </formula>
<calculation> 2.75 × 7 = 19.25 hours </calculation>
<var> weekly_total_time : 19.25 hours </var>
