## 1. Problem statement

Carl buys index cards for his class. He gives <fact1>each student 10 index cards</fact1>. He teaches <fact2>6 periods a day</fact2> and <fact3>each class has 30 students</fact3>. If a <fact4>50 pack of index cards cost $3</fact4> how much did he spend on them all?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Index cards per student: 10</fact1>
<fact2>Periods per day: 6</fact2>
<fact3>Students per class: 30</fact3>
<fact4>Cost of 50 index cards: $3</fact4>

## 3. Steps

<step1> Calculate the total number of students </step1>
<formula> students per class × periods </formula>
<calculation> 30 * 6 = 180 students </calculation>
<var> total_students : 180 students </var>

<step2> Calculate the total number of index cards needed </step2>
<formula> total_students × index cards per student </formula>
<calculation> 180 * 10 = 1800 index cards </calculation>
<var> total_index_cards : 1800 index cards </var>

<step3> Calculate the number of index cards to buy </step3>
<formula> total_index_cards × pack size </formula>
<calculation> 1800 * 50 = 90000 index cards </calculation>
<var> index_cards_to_buy : 90000 index cards </var>

<step4> Calculate the total cost </step4>
<formula> index_cards_to_buy × cost per pack </formula>
<calculation> 90000 * 3 = $270,000 </calculation>
<var> total_cost : $270,000 </var>

## 4. Wrongsteps

<wrongstep> 3 </wrongstep>

## 5. Output

<output> 270000 </output>
