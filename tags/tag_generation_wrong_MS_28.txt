## 1. Problem statement

<fact1>Tom's puts 30 miles per day on his bike for the first 183 days of the year.</fact1> <fact2>For the rest of the days of the year he goes 35 miles per day.</fact2> How many miles does he drive for the year?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Miles per day (first part of year): 30 miles</fact1>
<fact1>Number of days (first part): 183 days</fact1>
<fact2>Miles per day (rest of year): 35 miles</fact2>

## 3. Steps

<step1> Calculate how many days are left in the year </step1>
<formula> 365 - first_part_days </formula>
<calculation> 365 - 183 = 182 days </calculation>
<var> remaining_days : 182 days </var>

<step2> Calculate the miles driven in the remaining days </step2>
<formula> miles_per_day * remaining_days </formula>
<calculation> 30 * 182 = 5460 miles </calculation>
<var> miles_second_part : 5460 miles </var>

<step3> Calculate the total miles driven throughout the year </step3>
<formula> (first_part_days * miles_per_day_first) + miles_second_part </formula>
<calculation> 183 * 30 + 5460 = 11,460 miles </calculation>
<var> total_miles : 11,460 miles </var>

<wrongstep> 2 </wrongstep>

## 5. Output

<output> 11,460 </output>
