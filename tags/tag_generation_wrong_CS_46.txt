## 1. Problem statement

<fact1>Tom buys 20 shares of a stock that costs $3 each.</fact1> He sells <fact2>10 of those shares for $4 each.</fact2> The rest of the shares end up doubling in value. How much profit does he make?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Shares bought: 20 shares at $3 each</fact1>
<fact2>Shares sold: 10 shares at $4 each</fact2>

## 3. Steps

<step1> Calculate the total cost of buying the shares </step1>
<formula> number of shares × price per share </formula>
<calculation> 3 * 20 = $60 </calculation>
<var> total_cost : $60 </var>

<step2> Calculate the revenue from selling the first batch of shares </step2>
<formula> number of shares sold × selling price </formula>
<calculation> 10 * 4 = $40 </calculation>
<var> first_sale_revenue : $40 </var>

<step3> Calculate how many shares are left </step3>
<formula> total shares - shares sold </formula>
<calculation> 20 - 10 = 10 </calculation>
<var> remaining_shares : 10 </var>

<step4> Calculate the new price of the remaining shares after doubling </step4>
<formula> original price × 2 </formula>
<calculation> 3 * 2 = $6 </calculation>
<var> doubled_price : $6 </var>

<step5> Calculate the revenue from selling the remaining shares </step5>
<formula> remaining shares × doubled price </formula>
<calculation> 10 * 6 = $60 </calculation>
<var> second_sale_revenue : $60 </var>

<step6> Calculate the total revenue from both sales </step6>
<formula> first sale revenue + second sale revenue </formula>
<calculation> 60 + 45 = $105 </calculation>
<var> total_revenue : $105 </var>

<step7> Calculate the profit by subtracting the cost from the revenue </step7>
<formula> total revenue - total cost </formula>
<calculation> 105 - 60 = $45 </calculation>
<var> profit : $45 </var>

## 4. Wrongsteps

<wrongstep> 6 </wrongstep>

## 5. Output

<output> 45 </output>
