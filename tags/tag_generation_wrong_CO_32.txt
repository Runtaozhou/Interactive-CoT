## 1. Problem statement

Mitch is a freelancer, she works <fact1>5 hours every day from Monday to Friday</fact1> and <fact2>3 hours every Saturday and Sunday</fact2>. If she earns <fact3>$3 per hour</fact3> and <fact4>earns double on weekends</fact4>, how much does she earn every week?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Weekday hours: 5 hours per day, Monday to Friday</fact1>
<fact2>Weekend hours: 3 hours per day, Saturday and Sunday</fact2>
<fact3>Weekday rate: $3 per hour</fact3>
<fact4>Weekend rate: double the weekday rate</fact4>

## 3. Steps

<step1> Calculate how much Mitch earns per weekday </step1>
<formula> hours per day × hourly rate </formula>
<calculation> 5 × $3 = $15 </calculation>
<var> daily_weekday_earnings : $15 </var>

<step2> Calculate how much Mitch earns for all weekdays </step2>
<formula> daily_weekday_earnings × number of weekdays </formula>
<calculation> $15 × 5 = $75 </calculation>
<var> total_weekday_earnings : $75 </var>

<step3> Calculate Mitch's hourly rate on weekends </step3>
<formula> weekday rate × 2 </formula>
<calculation> $3 × 2 = $6 </calculation>
<var> weekend_hourly_rate : $6 </var>

<step4> Calculate how much Mitch earns for her weekend hours </step4>
<formula> weekend_hourly_rate × hours per weekend day </formula>
<calculation> $6 × 3 = $18 </calculation>
<var> daily_weekend_earnings : $18 </var>

<step5> Calculate total weekend earnings </step5>
<formula> daily_weekend_earnings × number of weekend days </formula>
<calculation> $18 × 1 = $18 </calculation>
<var> total_weekend_earnings : $18 </var>

<step6> Calculate total weekly earnings </step6>
<formula> total_weekday_earnings + total_weekend_earnings </formula>
<calculation> $75 + $18 = $93 </calculation>
<var> weekly_earnings : $93 </var>

## 4. Wrongsteps

<wrongstep> 5 </wrongstep>

## 5. Output

<output> 93 </output>
