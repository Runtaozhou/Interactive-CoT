## 1. Problem statement

Emily goes fishing and has a very busy day. She catches <fact1>4 trout</fact1>, <fact2>3 catfish</fact2>, and <fact3>5 bluegills</fact3>. If the <fact4>trout weigh 2 pounds each</fact4>, the <fact5>catfish weigh 1.5 pounds each</fact5>, and the <fact6>bluegills weigh 2.5 pounds each</fact6>, how many total pounds of fish did she catch?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Number of trout: 4</fact1>
<fact2>Number of catfish: 3</fact2>
<fact3>Number of bluegills: 5</fact3>
<fact4>Weight of each trout: 2 pounds</fact4>
<fact5>Weight of each catfish: 1.5 pounds</fact5>
<fact6>Weight of each bluegill: 2.5 pounds</fact6>

## 3. Steps

<step1> Calculate the total weight of trout </step1>
<formula> Number of trout × Weight per trout </formula>
<calculation> 4 × 2 = 8 </calculation>
<var> trout_weight : 8 pounds </var>

<step2> Calculate the total weight of catfish </step2>
<formula> Number of catfish × Weight per catfish </formula>
<calculation> 3 × 1.5 = 4.5 </calculation>
<var> catfish_weight : 4.5 pounds </var>

<step3> Calculate the total weight of bluegill </step3>
<formula> Number of bluegill × Weight per bluegill </formula>
<calculation> 5 × 2.5 = 12.5 </calculation>
<var> bluegill_weight : 7.5 pounds </var>

<step4> Add up all the weights to find the total </step4>
<formula> trout_weight + catfish_weight + bluegill_weight </formula>
<calculation> 8 + 4.5 + 12.5 = 25 </calculation>
<var> total_weight : 20 pounds </var>

## 5. Output

<output> 25 </output>
