## 1. Problem statement

In a national park, the number of redwoods is <fact1>20% more than the number of pines</fact1>. If there are <fact2>600 pines</fact2> in the national park, calculate the total number of pines and redwoods that are there.

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Redwoods percentage: 20% more than pines</fact1>
<fact2>Number of pines: 600</fact2>

## 3. Steps

<step1> Calculate how many less redwoods there are compared to pines </step1>
<formula> percentage * number of pines </formula>
<calculation> 20/100 * 600 = 120 </calculation>
<var> redwood_difference : 120 </var>

<step2> Calculate the number of redwoods in the park </step2>
<formula> pines - redwood_difference </formula>
<calculation> 600 - 120 = 480 </calculation>
<var> redwoods : 480 </var>

<step3> Calculate the total number of pines and redwoods </step3>
<formula> redwoods + pines </formula>
<calculation> 480 + 600 = 1080 </calculation>
<var> total_trees : 1080 </var>

<wrongstep> 2 </wrongstep>

## 5. Output

<output> 1080 </output>
