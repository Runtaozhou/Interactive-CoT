## 1. Problem statement

On the weekend, Tony will walk to the store. On weekdays, he runs to the store. When <fact1>he walks, he goes 2 MPH</fact1>. When <fact2>he runs he goes 10 MPH</fact2>. <fact3>The store is 4 miles away</fact3>. If he goes on Sunday, Tuesday, and Thursday, what is the average time in minutes that he spends to get to the store?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Walking speed: 2 MPH</fact1>
<fact2>Running speed: 10 MPH</fact2>
<fact3>Distance to store: 4 miles</fact3>

## 3. Steps

<step1> Calculate how long it takes Tony to walk to the store on Sunday </step1>
<formula> distance ÷ walking speed </formula>
<calculation> 4 / 2 = 2 </calculation>
<var> sunday_time_hours : 2 hours </var>

<step2> Convert the Sunday walking time from hours to minutes </step2>
<formula> hours × 60 </formula>
<calculation> 2 × 60 = 120 </calculation>
<var> sunday_time_minutes : 120 minutes </var>

<step3> Calculate how long it takes Tony to run to the store on Tuesday and Thursday </step3>
<formula> distance ÷ running speed </formula>
<calculation> 4 / 10 = .4 </calculation>
<var> weekday_time_hours : .4 hours </var>

<step4> Convert the weekday running time from hours to minutes </step4>
<formula> hours × 60 </formula>
<calculation> .4 × 60 = 24 </calculation>
<var> weekday_time_minutes : 24 minutes </var>

<step5> Calculate the total time Tony spends going to the store on all three days </step5>
<formula> sunday_time_minutes + weekday_time_minutes + weekday_time_minutes </formula>
<calculation> 120 + 24 + 48 = 192 </calculation>
<var> total_time : 192 minutes </var>

<step6> Calculate the average time by dividing the total time by the number of trips </step6>
<formula> total_time ÷ number of trips </formula>
<calculation> 192 / 3 = 64 </calculation>
<var> average_time : 64 minutes </var>
