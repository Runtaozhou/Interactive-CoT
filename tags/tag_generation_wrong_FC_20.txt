## 1. Problem statement

<fact1>James has a room that is 13 feet by 18 feet.</fact1> He increases <fact2>each dimension by 2 feet.</fact2> He then builds <fact3>3 more rooms of equal size</fact3> and <fact4>1 room of twice that size.</fact4> How much area does he have?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Original room dimensions: 13 feet by 18 feet</fact1>
<fact2>Dimension increase: 2 feet</fact2>
<fact3>Additional equal-sized rooms: 3</fact3>
<fact4>Additional larger room: 1 room of twice the size</fact4>

## 3. Steps

<step1> Calculate the new length after increasing it </step1>
<formula> original length + increase </formula>
<calculation> 13 + 2 = 15 feet </calculation>
<var> new_length : 15 feet </var>

<step2> Calculate the new width after increasing it </step2>
<formula> original width + increase </formula>
<calculation> 18 + 2 = 20 feet </calculation>
<var> new_width : 20 feet </var>

<step3> Calculate the area of each room </step3>
<formula> new_length + new_width + new_length + new_width </formula>
<calculation> 15 + 20 + 15 + 20 = 70 square feet </calculation>
<var> room_area : 70 square feet </var>

<step4> Calculate the total number of equal-sized rooms </step4>
<formula> original room + additional equal rooms </formula>
<calculation> 1 + 3 = 4 rooms </calculation>
<var> total_equal_rooms : 4 </var>

<step5> Calculate the total area of equal-sized rooms </step5>
<formula> total_equal_rooms × room_area </formula>
<calculation> 4 * 70 = 280 square feet </calculation>
<var> equal_rooms_area : 280 square feet </var>

<step6> Calculate the area of the larger room </step6>
<formula> room_area × 2 </formula>
<calculation> 70 * 2 = 140 square feet </calculation>
<var> larger_room_area : 140 square feet </var>

<step7> Calculate the total area of all rooms </step7>
<formula> equal_rooms_area + larger_room_area </formula>
<calculation> 280 + 140 = 420 square feet </calculation>
<var> total_area : 420 square feet </var>

## 4. Wrongsteps

<wrongstep> 3 </wrongstep>

## 5. Output

<output> 420 </output>
