## 1. Problem statement

Bethany loved riding horses. She rode <fact1>1 hour after school every Monday, Wednesday, and Friday</fact1>. On <fact2>Tuesday and Thursday, she rode for 30 min</fact2> and on <fact3>Saturdays, she could ride for 2 hours</fact3>. How many hours in total did she ride over a <fact4>2 week period</fact4>?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Monday, Wednesday, Friday riding time: 1 hour each</fact1>
<fact2>Tuesday and Thursday riding time: 30 min each</fact2>
<fact3>Saturday riding time: 2 hours</fact3>
<fact4>Time period: 2 weeks</fact4>

## 3. Steps

<step1> Calculate how many hours Bethany rode on Monday, Wednesday, and Friday each week </step1>
<formula> hours per day × number of days </formula>
<calculation> 1*3 = 3 hours </calculation>
<var> mon_wed_fri_hours : 3 hours </var>

<step2> Calculate how many hours Bethany rode on Tuesday and Thursday each week </step2>
<formula> minutes per day × number of days </formula>
<calculation> 30*2 = 60 min or 1 hour </calculation>
<var> tue_thu_hours : 1 hour </var>

<step3> Calculate the total hours Bethany rode in one week </step3>
<formula> mon_wed_fri_hours + tue_thu_hours + saturday_hours </formula>
<calculation> 3+1+2 = 6 hours </calculation>
<var> weekly_hours : 6 hours </var>

<step4> Calculate the total hours Bethany rode over the 2-week period </step4>
<formula> weekly_hours × number of weeks </formula>
<calculation> 6*2 = 11 hours </calculation>
<var> total_hours : 11 hours </var>

## 4. Wrongsteps

<wrongstep> 4 </wrongstep>

## 5. Output

<output> 11 </output>
