## 1. Problem statement

Dream's car consumes <fact1>4 gallons of gas per mile</fact1>. If she drives <fact2>400 miles today</fact2> and <fact3>200 more miles tomorrow than today</fact3>, how many gallons of gas will the car consume?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Fuel consumption: 4 gallons per mile</fact1>
<fact2>Distance today: 400 miles</fact2>
<fact3>Additional distance tomorrow: 200 miles</fact3>

## 3. Steps

<step1> Calculate the fuel consumption for today's drive </step1>
<formula> fuel consumption per mile × miles driven today </formula>
<calculation> 4 * 400 = 1600 gallons </calculation>
<var> today_fuel : 1600 gallons </var>

<step2> Calculate the total distance for tomorrow </step2>
<formula> additional miles + today's miles </formula>
<calculation> 200 + 400 = 600 miles </calculation>
<var> tomorrow_distance : 600 miles </var>

<step3> Calculate the fuel consumption for tomorrow's drive </step3>
<formula> fuel consumption per mile × tomorrow's distance </formula>
<calculation> 4 * 600 = 2400 gallons </calculation>
<var> tomorrow_fuel : 2400 gallons </var>

<step4> Calculate the total fuel consumption for both days </step4>
<formula> tomorrow's fuel + today's fuel </formula>
<calculation> 2400 + 1600 = 4000 gallons </calculation>
<var> total_fuel : 4000 gallons </var>

<wrongstep> 0 </wrongstep>

## 5. Output

<output> 4000 </output>
