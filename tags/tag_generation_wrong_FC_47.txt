## 1. Problem statement

<fact1>Three companies, A, B, and C, each purchased 10 ad spaces on a newspaper</fact1>, with <fact2>each ad space having a size of 12 foot by 5-foot rectangle</fact2>. If <fact3>each square foot ad was charged at $60</fact3>, how much money did the three pay for the ads combined?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Number of ad spaces per company: 10</fact1>
<fact2>Ad space dimensions: 12 foot by 5-foot rectangle</fact2>
<fact3>Cost per square foot: $60</fact3>

## 3. Steps

<step1> Calculate the area of each rectangular ad space </step1>
<formula> perimeter = 2(length + width) </formula>
<calculation> 12+5+12+5 = 34 square feet </calculation>
<var> ad_area : 34 square feet </var>

<step2> Calculate the total area for one company's ads </step2>
<formula> ad_area × number of ads </formula>
<calculation> 34*10 = 340 square feet </calculation>
<var> company_ad_area : 340 square feet </var>

<step3> Calculate the cost for one company's ads </step3>
<formula> company_ad_area × cost per square foot </formula>
<calculation> $60*340 = $20400 </calculation>
<var> company_cost : $20400 </var>

<step4> Calculate the total cost for all three companies </step4>
<formula> company_cost × number of companies </formula>
<calculation> $20400*3 = $61200 </calculation>
<var> total_cost : $61200 </var>

## 4. Wrongsteps

<wrongstep> 1 </wrongstep>

## 5. Output

<output> 61200 </output>
