## 1. Problem statement

<fact1>James takes 5 oranges</fact1> and breaks <fact2>each orange into 8 pieces</fact2>. He splits the pieces between <fact3>4 people</fact3>. If <fact4>an orange has 80 calories</fact4> how many calories does each person get?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Number of oranges: 5</fact1>
<fact2>Pieces per orange: 8</fact2>
<fact3>Number of people: 4</fact3>
<fact4>Calories per orange: 80</fact4>

## 3. Steps

<step1>Calculate the total number of pieces James makes</step1>
<formula>oranges × pieces per orange</formula>
<calculation>5 * 8 = 40 pieces</calculation>
<var>total_pieces: 40 pieces</var>

<step2>Calculate how many pieces each person gets</step2>
<formula>total_pieces ÷ number of people</formula>
<calculation>40 / 4 = 10 pieces</calculation>
<var>pieces_per_person: 10 pieces</var>

<step3>Calculate how many oranges each person gets</step3>
<formula>pieces_per_person ÷ pieces per orange</formula>
<calculation>10 / 8 = 1.25 oranges</calculation>
<var>oranges_per_person: 1.25 oranges</var>

<step4>Calculate the calories each person gets</step4>
<formula>calories per orange × oranges_per_person</formula>
<calculation>80 * 1.5 = 120 calories</calculation>
<var>calories_per_person: 120 calories</var>

## 4. Wrongsteps

<wrongstep>4</wrongstep>

## 5. Output

<output>120</output>
