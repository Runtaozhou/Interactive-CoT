## 1. Problem statement

On Saturdays and Sundays, Suzanne makes french toast for the family. She and her husband each have <fact1>1 whole slice</fact1> and her daughters split <fact2>1 slice</fact2>. She uses a thick sliced bread that comes <fact3>12 slices per loaf</fact3>. Over <fact4>52 weeks</fact4>, how many loaves of bread will she need to make french toast?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Suzanne and husband slices: 1 whole slice each</fact1>
<fact2>Daughters' slices: 1 slice (split)</fact2>
<fact3>Slices per loaf: 12</fact3>
<fact4>Number of weeks: 52</fact4>

## 3. Steps

<step1> Calculate the total number of slices used per day </step1>
<formula> husband_slice + suzanne_slice + daughters_slices </formula>
<calculation> 1 + 1 + .5 + .5 = 3 slices </calculation>
<var> slices_per_day : 3 </var>

<step2> Calculate the total number of slices used per weekend </step2>
<formula> slices_per_day × number_of_days </formula>
<calculation> 3 * 1 = 3 slices per weekend </calculation>
<var> slices_per_weekend : 3 </var>

<step3> Calculate the total number of slices needed for 52 weeks </step3>
<formula> slices_per_weekend × number_of_weeks </formula>
<calculation> 3 * 52 = 156 slices </calculation>
<var> total_slices : 156 </var>

<step4> Calculate the number of loaves needed </step4>
<formula> total_slices ÷ slices_per_loaf </formula>
<calculation> 156 / 12 = 13 loaves </calculation>
<var> total_loaves : 13 </var>

## 4. Wrongsteps

<wrongstep> 2 </wrongstep>

## 5. Output

<output> 13 </output>
