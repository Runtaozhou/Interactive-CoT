## 1. Problem statement

There are <fact1>10 6-ounce glasses</fact1> that are only <fact2>4/5 full</fact2> of water. How many ounces of water are needed to fill to the brim all those <fact1>10 glasses</fact1>?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Number of glasses: 10 6-ounce glasses</fact1>
<fact2>Portion filled: 4/5</fact2>

## 3. Steps

<step1> Calculate how much water is in each glass </step1>
<formula> glass capacity × portion filled </formula>
<calculation> 6 × 4/5 = 24/5 ounces </calculation>
<var> water_per_glass : 24/5 ounces </var>

<step2> Calculate the total amount of water currently in all glasses </step2>
<formula> water_per_glass × number of glasses </formula>
<calculation> 24/5 × 10 = 48 ounces </calculation>
<var> total_current_water : 48 ounces </var>

<step3> Calculate the total capacity of all glasses when filled to the brim </step3>
<formula> glass capacity × number of glasses </formula>
<calculation> 10 × 6 = 60 ounces </calculation>
<var> total_capacity : 60 ounces </var>

<step4> Calculate how much more water is needed to fill all glasses </step4>
<formula> total_capacity - total_current_water </formula>
<calculation> 60 - 40 = 20 ounces </calculation>
<var> additional_water_needed : 20 ounces </var>

## 4. Wrongsteps

<wrongstep> 4 </wrongstep>

## 5. Output

<output> 20 </output>
