## 1. Problem statement

Boston had <fact1>.5 feet of snow on the first day of winter</fact1>. The next day they got an additional <fact2>8 inches</fact2>. Over the next <fact3>2 days, 2 inches</fact3> of the snow melted. On the fifth day, they received another <fact4>2 times</fact4> the amount of snow they received on the first day. How many feet of snow do they now have?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>First day snow: .5 feet</fact1>
<fact2>Second day additional snow: 8 inches</fact2>
<fact3>Snow melted over 2 days: 2 inches</fact3>
<fact4>Fifth day snow multiplier: 2 times</fact4>

## 3. Steps

<step1> Convert feet to inches for the first day's snow </step1>
<formula> feet × inches per foot </formula>
<calculation> 10*.5 = 5 inches </calculation>
<var> first_day_snow : 5 inches </var>

<step2> Add the second day's snow to the first day's amount </step2>
<formula> first_day_snow + second_day_snow </formula>
<calculation> 5+8 = 13 inches </calculation>
<var> total_after_second_day : 13 inches </var>

<step3> Subtract the melted snow from the total </step3>
<formula> total_after_second_day - melted_snow </formula>
<calculation> 13-2 = 11 inches </calculation>
<var> total_after_melting : 11 inches </var>

<step4> Calculate the amount of snow received on the fifth day </step4>
<formula> first_day_snow × multiplier </formula>
<calculation> 2*5 = 10 inches </calculation>
<var> fifth_day_snow : 10 inches </var>

<step5> Add the fifth day's snow to the previous total </step5>
<formula> total_after_melting + fifth_day_snow </formula>
<calculation> 11+10 = 21 inches </calculation>
<var> total_inches : 21 inches </var>

<step6> Convert the total inches to feet </step6>
<formula> total_inches ÷ inches per foot </formula>
<calculation> 21/10 = 2.1 feet </calculation>
<var> total_feet : 2.1 feet </var>
