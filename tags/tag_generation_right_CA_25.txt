## 1. Problem statement

The total number of <fact1>lions in a park is twice the number of leopards in the same park</fact1>. The <fact2>number of elephants is half the combined number of lions and leopards</fact2>. Calculate the total population of the three animals in the park if the <fact3>number of lions is 200</fact3>.

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Lions : 2 × leopards</fact1>
<fact2>Elephants : 0.5 × (lions + leopards)</fact2>
<fact3>Lions : 200</fact3>

## 3. Steps

<step1> If there are 200 lions in the park, which is twice the number of leopards in the park, there are 200/2 = 100 leopards in the park. </step1>
<formula> leopards = lions ÷ 2 </formula>
<calculation> 200 ÷ 2 = 100 </calculation>
<var> leopards : 100 </var>

<step2> The total population of lions and leopards in the park is 200 lions + 100 leopards = 300 </step2>
<formula> lions_and_leopards = lions + leopards </formula>
<calculation> 200 + 100 = 300 </calculation>
<var> lions_and_leopards : 300 </var>

<step3> The number of elephants in the park is 1/2 the total population of lions and leopards; thus, there are 300/2 = 150 elephants in the park. </step3>
<formula> elephants = lions_and_leopards × 1/2 </formula>
<calculation> 300 × 1/2 = 150 </calculation>
<var> elephants : 150 </var>

<step4> There are a total of 150+300 = 450 animals in the park. </step4>
<formula> total_animals = elephants + lions_and_leopards </formula>
<calculation> 150 + 300 = 450 </calculation>
<var> total_animals : 450 </var>

## 5. Output

<output>450</output>
