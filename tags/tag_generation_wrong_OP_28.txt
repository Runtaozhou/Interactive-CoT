## 1. Problem statement

Cilia wants to buy a multi-level dumbbell system. The <fact1>first pair of dumbbells are 3 lb weights</fact1>, the <fact2>second pair is 5 lb weights</fact2> and the <fact3>third is 8 lb pounds</fact3>. How much does the entire system weigh?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>First pair weight: 3 lb each</fact1>
<fact2>Second pair weight: 5 lb each</fact2>
<fact3>Third pair weight: 8 lb each</fact3>

## 3. Steps

<step1> Calculate the total weight of the first pair of dumbbells </step1>
<formula> weight per dumbbell × number of dumbbells </formula>
<calculation> 2*3 = 6 lbs </calculation>
<var> first_pair_weight : 6 lbs </var>

<step2> Calculate the total weight of the second pair of dumbbells </step2>
<formula> number + weight per dumbbell </formula>
<calculation> 2+5 = 7 lbs </calculation>
<var> second_pair_weight : 7 lbs </var>

<step3> Calculate the total weight of the third pair of dumbbells </step3>
<formula> weight per dumbbell × number of dumbbells </formula>
<calculation> 2*8 = 16 lbs </calculation>
<var> third_pair_weight : 16 lbs </var>

<step4> Add up the weights of all three pairs to find the total weight of the system </step4>
<formula> first_pair_weight + second_pair_weight + third_pair_weight </formula>
<calculation> 6+7+16 = 29 lbs </calculation>
<var> total_weight : 29 lbs </var>

## 5. Output

<output> 29 </output>
