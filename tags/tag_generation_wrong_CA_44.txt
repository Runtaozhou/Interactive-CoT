## 1. Problem statement

Steve is building a bench for the playground and needs <fact1>6 lengths of wood that measure 4 feet</fact1> and <fact2>2 lengths of wood that measure 2 feet</fact2>. How many feet of wood does Steve need to buy?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Long wood pieces: 6 lengths of 4 feet</fact1>
<fact2>Short wood pieces: 2 lengths of 2 feet</fact2>

## 3. Steps

<step1> Calculate the total length of the long wood pieces </step1>
<formula> number of pieces × length per piece </formula>
<calculation> 6 × 4 = 24 feet </calculation>
<var> long_wood_total : 24 feet </var>

<step2> Calculate the total length of the short wood pieces </step2>
<formula> number of pieces × length per piece </formula>
<calculation> 2 × 2 = 6 feet </calculation>
<var> short_wood_total : 6 feet </var>

<step3> Add the total lengths to find the total amount of wood needed </step3>
<formula> long_wood_total + short_wood_total </formula>
<calculation> 24 feet + 6 feet = 30 feet </calculation>
<var> total_wood_needed : 30 feet </var>

## 4. Wrongsteps

<wrongstep> 2 </wrongstep>

## 5. Output

<output> 30 </output>
