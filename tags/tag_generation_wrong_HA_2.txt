## 1. Problem statement

The batting cage sells golf balls by the dozen. They charge <fact1>$30 for 3 dozen</fact1>. <fact2>Dan buys 5 dozen</fact2>, <fact3>Gus buys 2 dozen</fact3>, and <fact4>Chris buys 48 golf balls</fact4>. How many golf balls do they purchase in total, assuming <fact5>12 golf balls are 1 dozen</fact5>?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Price: $30 for 3 dozen</fact1>
<fact2>Dan's purchase: 5 dozen</fact2>
<fact3>Gus's purchase: 2 dozen</fact3>
<fact4>Chris's purchase: 48 golf balls</fact4>
<fact5>Conversion: 12 golf balls = 1 dozen</fact5>

## 3. Steps

<step1> Calculate how many golf balls Dan buys </step1>
<formula> Dan's dozen × balls per dozen </formula>
<calculation> 5 × 12 = 60 golf balls </calculation>
<var> dan_balls : 60 golf balls </var>

<step2> Calculate how many golf balls Gus buys </step2>
<formula> Gus's dozen × balls per dozen </formula>
<calculation> 2 × 12 = 24 golf balls </calculation>
<var> gus_balls : 24 golf balls </var>

<step3> Calculate how many golf balls Chris buys </step3>
<formula> Chris's golf balls + extra dozen </formula>
<calculation> 48 + 12 = 60 golf balls </calculation>
<var> chris_balls : 60 golf balls </var>

<step4> Add up all the golf balls to find the total </step4>
<formula> Dan's balls + Gus's balls + Chris's balls </formula>
<calculation> 60 + 24 + 60 = 144 golf balls </calculation>
<var> total_balls : 144 golf balls </var>

<wrongstep> 3 </wrongstep>

## 5. Output

<output> 144 </output>
