## 1. Problem statement

Mike watches TV for <fact1>4 hours every day</fact1>. On the days he plays video games he plays for <fact2>half as long as he watches TV</fact2>. If he plays video games <fact3>3 days a week</fact3> how long does he spend watching TV and playing video games?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>TV watching time: 4 hours per day</fact1>
<fact2>Video game time: half of TV watching time</fact2>
<fact3>Video game days: 3 days per week</fact3>

## 3. Steps

<step1> Calculate how many hours Mike watches TV in a week </step1>
<formula> daily TV hours × days in a week </formula>
<calculation> 4 * 7 = 28 hours </calculation>
<var> weekly_tv_hours : 28 hours </var>

<step2> Calculate how many hours Mike plays video games in a week </step2>
<formula> video game days × daily TV hours </formula>
<calculation> 3 * 4 = 12 hours </calculation>
<var> weekly_game_hours : 12 hours </var>

<step3> Calculate the total time spent on both activities </step3>
<formula> weekly_game_hours + weekly_tv_hours </formula>
<calculation> 12 + 28 = 40 hours </calculation>
<var> total_hours : 40 hours </var>

## 4. Wrongsteps

<wrongstep> 2 </wrongstep>

## 5. Output

<output> 40 </output>
