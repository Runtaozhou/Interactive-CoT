## 1. Problem statement

Aunt Gemma has <fact1>four dogs</fact1>. She feeds them with dog food <fact2>twice a day</fact2>. Each dog can consume <fact3>250 grams of food per meal</fact3>. If aunt Gemma bought <fact4>2 sacks of dog food, each weighing 50 kilograms</fact4>, how many days will those food last?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Number of dogs: 4</fact1>
<fact2>Feeding frequency: 2 times per day</fact2>
<fact3>Food consumption per dog per meal: 250 grams</fact3>
<fact4>Dog food purchased: 2 sacks of 50 kilograms each</fact4>

## 3. Steps

<step1> Calculate how much food each dog consumes daily </step1>
<formula> food per meal × meals per day </formula>
<calculation> 250 grams × 2 = 500 grams </calculation>
<var> daily_food_per_dog : 500 grams </var>

<step2> Calculate the total daily food consumption for all dogs </step2>
<formula> daily_food_per_dog × number of dogs </formula>
<calculation> 500 grams × 4 = 2000 grams </calculation>
<var> total_daily_food : 2000 grams </var>

<step3> Convert the daily food consumption to kilograms </step3>
<formula> total_daily_food ÷ 1000 </formula>
<calculation> 2000 ÷ 1000 = 2 kilograms </calculation>
<var> total_daily_food_kg : 2 kilograms </var>

<step4> Calculate the total amount of dog food purchased </step4>
<formula> sacks × weight per sack </formula>
<calculation> 50 kilograms × 2 = 100 kilograms </calculation>
<var> total_food : 100 kilograms </var>

<step5> Calculate how many days the food will last </step5>
<formula> total_food ÷ total_daily_food_kg </formula>
<calculation> 100 kilograms ÷ 2 kilograms = 50 days </calculation>
<var> days_of_food : 50 days </var>

<wrongstep> 0 </wrongstep>

## 5. Output

<output> 50 </output>
