## 1. Problem statement

Scott runs <fact1>3 miles every Monday through Wednesday</fact1>. Then he runs <fact2>twice as far he ran on Monday every Thursday and Friday</fact2>. How many miles will he run in a month with <fact3>4 weeks</fact3>?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Miles run Monday through Wednesday: 3 miles per day</fact1>
<fact2>Miles run Thursday and Friday: twice Monday's miles</fact2>
<fact3>Number of weeks in the month: 4 weeks</fact3>

## 3. Steps

<step1> Calculate how many miles Scott runs on Thursday and Friday </step1>
<formula> Monday's miles × 2 </formula>
<calculation> 3 × 2 = 6 miles </calculation>
<var> daily_miles_thu_fri : 6 miles </var>

<step2> Calculate the total miles Scott runs Monday through Wednesday in a week </step2>
<formula> daily_miles_mon_wed × number of days </formula>
<calculation> 3 × 3 = 9 miles </calculation>
<var> weekly_miles_mon_wed : 9 miles </var>

<step3> Calculate the total miles Scott runs Thursday and Friday in a week </step3>
<formula> daily_miles_thu_fri × number of days </formula>
<calculation> 6 × 3 = 18 miles </calculation>
<var> weekly_miles_thu_fri : 18 miles </var>

<step4> Calculate the total miles Scott runs in a week </step4>
<formula> weekly_miles_mon_wed + weekly_miles_thu_fri </formula>
<calculation> 9 + 18 = 27 miles </calculation>
<var> weekly_total_miles : 27 miles </var>

<step5> Calculate the total miles Scott runs in a month </step5>
<formula> weekly_total_miles × number of weeks </formula>
<calculation> 27 × 4 = 108 miles </calculation>
<var> monthly_total_miles : 108 miles </var>

<wrongstep> 3 </wrongstep>

## 5. Output

<output> 108 </output>
