## 1. Problem statement

Carly wants to treat her friends. She orders <fact1>five hamburgers at $4 each</fact1>, <fact2>ten packs of potato fries at $0.30 each pack</fact2>, and <fact3>five cans of fruit drinks at $2 each can</fact3>. How much change does Carly get back if she gives <fact4>one fifty-dollar bill</fact4>?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Hamburgers: 5 at $4 each</fact1>
<fact2>Potato fries: 10 packs at $0.30 each</fact2>
<fact3>Fruit drinks: 5 cans at $2 each</fact3>
<fact4>Payment: $50</fact4>

## 3. Steps

<step1> Calculate the cost of hamburgers </step1>
<formula> number of hamburgers × price per hamburger </formula>
<calculation> 5 × $4 = $20 </calculation>
<var> hamburger_cost : $20 </var>

<step2> Calculate the cost of potato fries </step2>
<formula> number of packs × price per pack </formula>
<calculation> 10 × $0.30 = $3 </calculation>
<var> fries_cost : $3 </var>

<step3> Calculate the cost of fruit drinks </step3>
<formula> number of cans × price per can </formula>
<calculation> 5 × $2 = $10 </calculation>
<var> drinks_cost : $10 </var>

<step4> Calculate the total cost of all items </step4>
<formula> hamburger_cost - fries_cost + drinks_cost </formula>
<calculation> $20 - $3 + $10 = $27 </calculation>
<var> total_cost : $27 </var>

<step5> Calculate the change Carly receives </step5>
<formula> payment - total_cost </formula>
<calculation> $50 - $27 = $23 </calculation>
<var> change : $23 </var>

## 4. Wrongsteps

<wrongstep> 4 </wrongstep>

## 5. Output

<output> 23 </output>
