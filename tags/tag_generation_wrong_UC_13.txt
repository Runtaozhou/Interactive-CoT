## 1. Problem statement

To make a cherry pie, Veronica needs <fact1>3 pounds of pitted cherries</fact1>. There are <fact2>80 single cherries in one pound of cherries</fact2>. It takes <fact3>10 minutes to pit 20 cherries</fact3>. How many hours will it take Veronica to pit all the cherries?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Pounds of cherries needed: 3 pounds</fact1>
<fact2>Cherries per pound: 80 cherries</fact2>
<fact3>Time to pit 20 cherries: 10 minutes</fact3>

## 3. Steps

<step1> Calculate the total number of cherries needed </step1>
<formula> cherries per pound × pounds needed </formula>
<calculation> 80 * 3 = 240 cherries </calculation>
<var> total_cherries : 240 cherries </var>

<step2> Calculate how many units of 20 cherries need to be pitted </step2>
<formula> total cherries ÷ cherries per unit </formula>
<calculation> 240 / 20 = 12 units </calculation>
<var> cherry_units : 12 units </var>

<step3> Calculate the total time needed in minutes </step3>
<formula> time per unit × number of units </formula>
<calculation> 10 * 12 = 120 minutes </calculation>
<var> total_minutes : 120 minutes </var>

<step4> Convert minutes to hours </step4>
<formula> total minutes ÷ minutes per hour </formula>
<calculation> 120 / 50 = 2.4 hours </calculation>
<var> total_hours : 2.4 hours </var>

<wrongstep> 4 </wrongstep>

## 5. Output

<output> 2.4 </output>
