## 1. Problem statement

<fact1>Lisa has 36 candies.</fact1> On Mondays and Wednesdays, she eats <fact2>2 candies for each day</fact2> and on the other days of the week she eats <fact3>1 candy for each day</fact3>. How many weeks does it take for Lisa to eat all of the candies?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Total candies: 36</fact1>
<fact2>Candies eaten on Monday and Wednesday: 2 per day</fact2>
<fact3>Candies eaten on other days: 1 per day</fact3>

## 3. Steps

<step1>Calculate how many candies Lisa eats on Mondays and Wednesdays</step1>
<formula>candies per day × number of days</formula>
<calculation>2 * 2 = 4 candies</calculation>
<var>monday_wednesday_candies: 4 candies</var>

<step2>Calculate how many candies Lisa eats on the other days of the week</step2>
<formula>candies per day × number of days</formula>
<calculation>4 * 1 = 4 candies</calculation>
<var>other_days_candies: 4 candies</var>

<step3>Calculate the total candies Lisa eats in a week</step3>
<formula>monday_wednesday_candies + other_days_candies</formula>
<calculation>4 + 4 = 8 candies</calculation>
<var>weekly_candies: 8 candies</var>

<step4>Calculate how many weeks it takes to eat all candies</step4>
<formula>total candies ÷ weekly candies</formula>
<calculation>36 / 8 = 4.5 weeks</calculation>
<var>total_weeks: 4.5 weeks</var>

## 5. Output

<output>4.5</output>
