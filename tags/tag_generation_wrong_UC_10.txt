## 1. Problem statement

Some friends wanted to make a road trip from New York to Los Angeles. They drove at a constant speed of <fact1>62 miles/hour</fact1>, taking breaks of <fact2>30 minutes every 5 hours</fact2>. Once in the city, they looked for the hotel for <fact3>30 minutes</fact3>. If the trip took around <fact4>2,790 miles</fact4>, how many hours will they have to spend to complete the trip to the hotel?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Speed: 62 miles/hour</fact1>
<fact2>Breaks: 30 minutes every 5 hours</fact2>
<fact3>Hotel search time: 30 minutes</fact3>
<fact4>Trip distance: 2,790 miles</fact4>

## 3. Steps

<step1> Calculate the total driving time without breaks </step1>
<formula> distance ÷ speed </formula>
<calculation> 2,790 miles / 62 miles/hour = 45 hours </calculation>
<var> driving_time : 45 hours </var>

<step2> Calculate the number of breaks needed </step2>
<formula> driving_time ÷ hours_between_breaks </formula>
<calculation> 45 hours / 5 hours/break = 9 breaks </calculation>
<var> number_of_breaks : 9 breaks </var>

<step3> Calculate the total time spent on breaks </step3>
<formula> number_of_breaks × break_duration </formula>
<calculation> 9 breaks x 50 minutes/break = 450 minutes </calculation>
<var> break_time_minutes : 450 minutes </var>

<step4> Convert break time from minutes to hours </step4>
<formula> break_time_minutes ÷ minutes_per_hour </formula>
<calculation> 450 minutes ÷ 60 minutes/hour = 7.5 hours </calculation>
<var> break_time_hours : 7.5 hours </var>

<step5> Convert hotel search time from minutes to hours </step5>
<formula> hotel_search_minutes ÷ minutes_per_hour </formula>
<calculation> 30 minutes ÷ 60 minutes/hour = 0.5 hours </calculation>
<var> hotel_search_hours : 0.5 hours </var>

<step6> Calculate the total trip time </step6>
<formula> driving_time + break_time_hours + hotel_search_hours </formula>
<calculation> 45 hours + 7.5 hours + 0.5 hours = 53 hours </calculation>
<var> total_trip_time : 53 hours </var>

## 5. Output

<output> 53 </output>
