## 1. Problem statement

A tomato plant has <fact1>100 tomatoes</fact1>. Jane picks <fact2>1/4</fact2> of that number for use in their house. After a week, she goes back and picks <fact3>20 more tomatoes</fact3>, and the following week picks <fact4>twice that number</fact4>. What's the total number of fruits remaining on the tomato plant?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Initial tomatoes: 100</fact1>
<fact2>First picking: 1/4 of total</fact2>
<fact3>Second picking: 20 tomatoes</fact3>
<fact4>Third picking: twice the second picking</fact4>

## 3. Steps

<step1> Calculate how many tomatoes Jane picks in the first round </step1>
<formula> total tomatoes × fraction picked </formula>
<calculation> 1/4 * 100 = 25 tomatoes </calculation>
<var> first_picking : 25 tomatoes </var>

<step2> Calculate how many tomatoes remain after the first picking </step2>
<formula> total tomatoes - first picking </formula>
<calculation> 100 - 25 = 75 tomatoes </calculation>
<var> remaining_after_first : 75 tomatoes </var>

<step3> Calculate how many tomatoes remain after the second picking </step3>
<formula> remaining after first picking - second picking </formula>
<calculation> 75 - 20 = 50 tomatoes </calculation>
<var> remaining_after_second : 50 tomatoes </var>

<step4> Calculate how many tomatoes Jane picks in the third round </step4>
<formula> second picking × 2 </formula>
<calculation> 20 * 2 = 40 tomatoes </calculation>
<var> third_picking : 40 tomatoes </var>

<step5> Calculate the final number of tomatoes remaining </step5>
<formula> remaining after second picking - third picking </formula>
<calculation> 50 - 40 = 10 tomatoes </calculation>
<var> final_remaining : 10 tomatoes </var>

## 4. Wrongsteps

<wrongstep> 3 </wrongstep>

## 5. Output

<output> 10 </output>
