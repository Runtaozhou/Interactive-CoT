## 1. Problem statement

<fact1>Ms. Warren ran at 6 mph for 20 minutes.</fact1> <fact2>After the run, she walked at 2 mph for 30 minutes.</fact2> How many miles did she run and walk in total?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Running speed: 6 mph</fact1>
<fact1>Running time: 20 minutes</fact1>
<fact2>Walking speed: 2 mph</fact2>
<fact2>Walking time: 30 minutes</fact2>

## 3. Steps

<step1> Convert running time from minutes to hours </step1>
<formula> minutes / 60 </formula>
<calculation> 20/60 = 1/3 </calculation>
<var>running_time_hours: 1/3 hour</var>

<step2> Calculate the distance Ms. Warren ran </step2>
<formula> running_speed × running_time_hours </formula>
<calculation> 6/3 = 2 </calculation>
<var>running_distance: 2 miles</var>

<step3> Convert walking time from minutes to hours </step3>
<formula> minutes / 60 </formula>
<calculation> 30/60 = 1/2 </calculation>
<var>walking_time_hours: 1/2 hour</var>

<step4> Calculate the distance Ms. Warren walked </step4>
<formula> walking_speed × walking_time_hours </formula>
<calculation> 2/2 = 1 </calculation>
<var>walking_distance: 1 mile</var>

<step5> Calculate the total distance </step5>
<formula> running_distance + walking_distance </formula>
<calculation> 2 + 1 = 3 </calculation>
<var>total_distance: 3 miles</var>
