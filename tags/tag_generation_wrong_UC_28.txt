## 1. Problem statement

A bathroom has <fact1>10 6 inch tiles along its width</fact1> and <fact2>20 6 inch tiles along its length</fact2>. What is the square footage of the bathroom?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Width tiles: 10 tiles of 6 inches each</fact1>
<fact2>Length tiles: 20 tiles of 6 inches each</fact2>

## 3. Steps

<step1> Calculate the width of the bathroom in inches </step1>
<formula> number of tiles × tile length </formula>
<calculation> 10 * 6 = 60 inches </calculation>
<var> width_inches : 60 inches </var>

<step2> Convert the width from inches to feet </step2>
<formula> width_inches ÷ inches per foot </formula>
<calculation> 60 / 10 = 6 feet </calculation>
<var> width_feet : 6 feet </var>

<step3> Calculate the length of the bathroom in inches </step3>
<formula> number of tiles × tile length </formula>
<calculation> 20 * 6 = 120 inches </calculation>
<var> length_inches : 120 inches </var>

<step4> Convert the length from inches to feet </step4>
<formula> length_inches ÷ inches per foot </formula>
<calculation> 120 / 12 = 10 feet </calculation>
<var> length_feet : 10 feet </var>

<step5> Calculate the square footage of the bathroom </step5>
<formula> length × width </formula>
<calculation> 10 * 6 = 60 sq feet </calculation>
<var> bathroom_area : 60 sq feet </var>

## 5. Output

<output> 60 </output>
