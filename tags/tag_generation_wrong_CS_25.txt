## 1. Problem statement

Bob is tilling a plot of his garden. The plot is <fact1>110 feet wide by 120 feet long</fact1>. His tiller digs a swath <fact2>two feet wide</fact2>, and he can till <fact3>1 foot of ground in about 2 seconds</fact3>. How long will it take him to till this plot of land, in minutes?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Plot dimensions: 110 feet wide by 120 feet long</fact1>
<fact2>Tiller width: 2 feet</fact2>
<fact3>Tilling rate: 1 foot in 2 seconds</fact3>

## 3. Steps

<step1> Calculate how many rows Bob will need to till </step1>
<formula> width ÷ tiller width </formula>
<calculation> 110 / 2 = 55 rows </calculation>
<var> rows : 55 rows </var>

<step2> Calculate the total distance Bob will need to till </step2>
<formula> length × rows </formula>
<calculation> 120 * 55 = 6,600 feet </calculation>
<var> total_distance : 6,600 feet </var>

<step3> Calculate how long it will take in seconds </step3>
<formula> time per foot × total distance </formula>
<calculation> 2 * 6,600 = 13,200 seconds </calculation>
<var> total_time_seconds : 13,200 seconds </var>

<step4> Convert the time from seconds to minutes </step4>
<formula> total time in seconds ÷ seconds per minute </formula>
<calculation> 13,200 / 50 = 264 minutes </calculation>
<var> total_time_minutes : 264 minutes </var>

<wrongstep> 4 </wrongstep>

## 5. Output

<output> 264 </output>
