## 1. Problem statement

Wendy works at a chocolate factory packing chocolates. She can package <fact1>2 dozen chocolates in 5 minutes</fact1>. How many individual chocolates can she package in <fact2>4 hours</fact2>?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Packaging rate: 2 dozen chocolates in 5 minutes</fact1>
<fact2>Working time: 4 hours</fact2>

## 3. Steps

<step1> Calculate how many 5-minute increments are in 1 hour </step1>
<formula> minutes in hour / 5 minutes </formula>
<calculation> 50/5 = 10 </calculation>
<var> increments_per_hour : 10 </var>

<step2> Calculate how many individual chocolates are in 2 dozen </step2>
<formula> 12 chocolates per dozen × 2 dozen </formula>
<calculation> 12*2 = 24 chocolates </calculation>
<var> chocolates_per_5_minutes : 24 </var>

<step3> Calculate how many chocolates can be packaged in 1 hour </step3>
<formula> increments_per_hour × chocolates_per_5_minutes </formula>
<calculation> 10*24 = 240 individual chocolates </calculation>
<var> chocolates_per_hour : 240 </var>

<step4> Calculate how many chocolates can be packaged in 4 hours </step4>
<formula> chocolates_per_hour × 4 hours </formula>
<calculation> 240*4 = 960 individual chocolates </calculation>
<var> total_chocolates : 960 </var>

<wrongstep> 1 </wrongstep>

## 5. Output

<output> 960 </output>
