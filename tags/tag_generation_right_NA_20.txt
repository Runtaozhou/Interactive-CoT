## 1. Problem statement

John raises emu. He has <fact1>4 pens for emu</fact1> and <fact2>each pen has 6 emus in it</fact2>. <fact3>Each female emu lays 1 egg per day</fact3>. If <fact4>half the emu are females</fact4> how many eggs does he get a week?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Number of pens: 4</fact1>
<fact2>Number of emus per pen: 6</fact2>
<fact3>Eggs per female emu per day: 1</fact3>
<fact4>Proportion of female emus: 1/2</fact4>

## 3. Steps

<step1> Calculate the total number of emus </step1>
<formula> number of pens × emus per pen </formula>
<calculation> 6*4=24 emus </calculation>
<var> total_emus : 24 emus </var>

<step2> Calculate the number of female emus </step2>
<formula> total emus × proportion of females </formula>
<calculation> 24/2=12 female emus </calculation>
<var> female_emus : 12 female emus </var>

<step3> Calculate the total number of eggs in a week </step3>
<formula> female emus × days in a week </formula>
<calculation> 12*7=84 eggs </calculation>
<var> weekly_eggs : 84 eggs </var>

<wrongstep> 0 </wrongstep>

## 5. Output

<output> 84 </output>
