## 1. Problem statement

A <fact1>tub of ice cream costing $13</fact1> is now <fact2>sold at $11</fact2>. A <fact3>packet of milk was sold at a discount of $0.5</fact3>. How much will you save if you <fact4>buy 2 tubs of ice cream</fact4> and <fact5>4 packets of milk</fact5>?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Original ice cream price: $13</fact1>
<fact2>New ice cream price: $11</fact2>
<fact3>Milk discount: $0.5</fact3>
<fact4>Number of ice cream tubs: 2</fact4>
<fact5>Number of milk packets: 4</fact5>

## 3. Steps

<step1> Calculate the discount for each tub of ice cream </step1>
<formula> Original price - New price </formula>
<calculation> $13 - $10 = $3 </calculation>
<var> ice_cream_discount : $3 </var>

<step2> Calculate the total discount for all tubs of ice cream </step2>
<formula> ice_cream_discount × Number of tubs </formula>
<calculation> $3 × 2 = $6 </calculation>
<var> total_ice_cream_discount : $6 </var>

<step3> Calculate the total discount for all packets of milk </step3>
<formula> Milk discount × Number of packets </formula>
<calculation> $0.5 × 4 = $2 </calculation>
<var> total_milk_discount : $2 </var>

<step4> Calculate the total savings </step4>
<formula> total_ice_cream_discount + total_milk_discount </formula>
<calculation> $6 + $2 = $8 </calculation>
<var> total_savings : $8 </var>

<output> 8 </output>
