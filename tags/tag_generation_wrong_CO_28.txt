## 1. Problem statement

A cobbler can mend <fact1>3 pairs of shoes in an hour</fact1>. From Monday to Thursday, the cobbler works for <fact2>8 hours each day</fact2>, and on Friday, he only works from <fact3>8am to 11am</fact3>. How many pairs of shoes can the cobbler mend in a week?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Cobbler's rate: 3 pairs of shoes per hour</fact1>
<fact2>Work hours Monday-Thursday: 8 hours per day</fact2>
<fact3>Friday work hours: 8am to 11am</fact3>

## 3. Steps

<step1> Calculate how many pairs of shoes the cobbler can mend in one day from Monday to Thursday </step1>
<formula> pairs per hour × hours per day </formula>
<calculation> 3 × 8 = 24 pairs of shoes a day </calculation>
<var> daily_pairs_mon_to_thu : 24 pairs </var>

<step2> Calculate the total pairs of shoes mended from Monday to Thursday </step2>
<formula> daily_pairs_mon_to_thu × number of days </formula>
<calculation> 24 × 5 = 120 pairs of shoes </calculation>
<var> mon_to_thu_total : 120 pairs </var>

<step3> Calculate how many hours the cobbler works on Friday </step3>
<formula> end time - start time </formula>
<calculation> 11 - 8 = 3 hours </calculation>
<var> friday_hours : 3 hours </var>

<step4> Calculate how many pairs of shoes the cobbler can mend on Friday </step4>
<formula> pairs per hour × friday_hours </formula>
<calculation> 3 × 3 = 9 pairs of shoes </calculation>
<var> friday_pairs : 9 pairs </var>

<step5> Calculate the total pairs of shoes mended in a week </step5>
<formula> mon_to_thu_total + friday_pairs </formula>
<calculation> 120 + 9 = 129 pairs of shoes </calculation>
<var> weekly_total : 129 pairs </var>

## 4. Wrongsteps

<wrongstep> 2 </wrongstep>

## 5. Output

<output> 129 </output>
