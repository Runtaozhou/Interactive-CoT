## 1. Problem statement

Bill gets a flat-screen TV that measures <fact1>48 inches by 100 inches</fact1>. His brother Bob gets a TV that measures <fact2>70 inches by 60 inches</fact2>. If the TVs weigh <fact3>4 oz per square inch</fact3> of screen space, how much more does the heavier TV weigh in pounds? (There are <fact4>16 ounces per pound</fact4>).

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Bill's TV dimensions: 48 inches by 100 inches</fact1>
<fact2>Bob's TV dimensions: 70 inches by 60 inches</fact2>
<fact3>Weight per square inch: 4 oz</fact3>
<fact4>Conversion rate: 16 ounces per pound</fact4>

## 3. Steps

<step1> First find the perimeter of Bill's TV </step1>
<formula> 2 × length + 2 × width </formula>
<calculation> 48 inches + 48 inches + 100 inches + 100 inches = 296 inches </calculation>
<var> bill_tv_size : 296 inches </var>

<step2> Then find the screen area of Bob's TV </step2>
<formula> length × width </formula>
<calculation> 70 inches * 60 inches = 4200 square inches </calculation>
<var> bob_tv_size : 4200 square inches </var>

<step3> Then subtract the size of the smaller TV from the size of the bigger TV to find the size difference </step3>
<formula> bill_tv_size - bob_tv_size </formula>
<calculation> 296 inches - 4200 square inches = -3904 square inches </calculation>
<var> size_difference : -3904 square inches </var>

<step4> Then multiply the size difference by the weight per square inch to find the weight difference </step4>
<formula> size_difference × weight_per_square_inch </formula>
<calculation> -3904 square inches * 4 oz/square inch = -15616 oz </calculation>
<var> weight_difference_oz : -15616 oz </var>

<step5> Then divide the weight difference in ounces by the number of ounces per pound to find the weight difference in pounds </step5>
<formula> weight_difference_oz ÷ ounces_per_pound </formula>
<calculation> -15616 oz / 16 oz/pound = -976 pounds </calculation>
<var> weight_difference_pounds : -976 pounds </var>

## 5. Output
