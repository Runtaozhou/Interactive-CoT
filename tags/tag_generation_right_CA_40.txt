## 1. Problem statement

<fact1>John raises emu. He has 4 pens for emu</fact1> and <fact2>each pen has 6 emus in it</fact2>. <fact3>Each female emu lays 1 egg per day</fact3>. <fact4>If half the emu are females</fact4> how many eggs does he get a week?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Number of pens: 4</fact1>
<fact2>Number of emus per pen: 6</fact2>
<fact3>Eggs per female emu per day: 1</fact3>
<fact4>Proportion of female emus: 1/2</fact4>

## 3. Steps

<step1> Calculate the total number of emus </step1>
<formula> Number of pens × Number of emus per pen </formula>
<calculation> 6 × 4 = 24 </calculation>
<var> total_emus: 24 </var>

<step2> Calculate the number of female emus </step2>
<formula> Total emus × Proportion of female emus </formula>
<calculation> 24 / 2 = 12 </calculation>
<var> female_emus: 12 </var>

<step3> Calculate the total number of eggs in a week </step3>
<formula> Number of female emus × Days in a week </formula>
<calculation> 12 × 7 = 84 </calculation>
<var> total_eggs: 84 </var>

## 4. Wrongsteps

<wrongstep> 3 </wrongstep>

## 5. Output

<output>84</output>
