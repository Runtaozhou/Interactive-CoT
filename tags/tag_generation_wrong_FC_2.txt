## 1. Problem statement

Jason is making sand art. He wants to fill <fact1>a rectangular patch 6 inches by 7 inches</fact1> with blue sand, and <fact2>a square path 5 inches by 5 inches</fact2> with red sand. If <fact3>it takes 3 grams of sand to fill one square inch</fact3>, how many grams of sand does Jason need?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Rectangular patch dimensions: 6 inches by 7 inches</fact1>
<fact2>Square path dimensions: 5 inches by 5 inches</fact2>
<fact3>Sand needed: 3 grams per square inch</fact3>

## 3. Steps

<step1> First find the perimeter of the first patch </step1>
<formula> 2 × length + 2 × width </formula>
<calculation> 6 inches + 6 inches + 7 inches + 7 inches = 26 inches </calculation>
<var> rectangular_patch : 26 inches </var>

<step2> Then find the area of the second patch </step2>
<formula> length × width </formula>
<calculation> 5 inches * 5 inches = 25 inches </calculation>
<var> square_patch : 25 inches </var>

<step3> Then add those two amounts to find the total area Jason needs to cover </step3>
<formula> rectangular_patch + square_patch </formula>
<calculation> 26 inches + 25 inches = 51 inches </calculation>
<var> total_area : 51 inches </var>

<step4> Then multiply that figure by the number of grams per square inch to find the total amount of sand needed </step4>
<formula> total_area × grams per square inch </formula>
<calculation> 51 inches * 3 grams/square inch = 153 grams </calculation>
<var> total_sand : 153 grams </var>

## 4. Wrongsteps

<wrongstep> 1 </wrongstep>

## 5. Output

<output> 153 </output>
