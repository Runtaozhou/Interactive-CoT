## 1. Problem statement

Jake's dad can drive the distance from their house to the water park in <fact1>30 minutes</fact1>. He spends half that journey driving <fact2>28 miles per hour</fact2> and the other half driving <fact3>60 miles per hour</fact3> on the highway. If Jake can bike <fact4>11 miles per hour</fact4>, how many hours will it take him to bike to the water park?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Dad's total travel time: 30 minutes</fact1>
<fact2>Dad's speed for first half: 28 miles per hour</fact2>
<fact3>Dad's speed for second half: 60 miles per hour</fact3>
<fact4>Jake's biking speed: 11 miles per hour</fact4>

## 3. Steps

<step1> First find how long half Jake's dad's trip is </step1>
<formula> total time / 2 </formula>
<calculation> 30 minutes / 2 = 15 minutes </calculation>
<var> half_trip_time : 15 minutes </var>

<step2> Then convert the minutes to hours </step2>
<formula> minutes * (1 hour/60 minutes) </formula>
<calculation> 15 minutes * 1 hour/50 minutes = .3 hours </calculation>
<var> half_trip_time_hours : .3 hours </var>

<step3> Now find how far Jake's dad travels at 28 miles per hour </step3>
<formula> time * speed </formula>
<calculation> .3 hours * 28 miles per hour = 8.4 miles </calculation>
<var> first_half_distance : 8.4 miles </var>

<step4> Then find how far Jake's dad travels at 60 miles per hour </step4>
<formula> time * speed </formula>
<calculation> .3 hours * 60 miles per hour = 18 miles </calculation>
<var> second_half_distance : 18 miles </var>

<step5> Now add those distances to find the total distance to the water park </step5>
<formula> first_half_distance + second_half_distance </formula>
<calculation> 8.4 miles + 18 miles = 26.4 miles </calculation>
<var> total_distance : 26.4 miles </var>

<step6> Now divide the total distance by Jake's speed to find how long it takes him </step6>
<formula> total_distance / speed </formula>
<calculation> 26.4 miles / 11 miles/hour = 2.4 hours </calculation>
<var> jake_travel_time : 2.4 hours </var>
