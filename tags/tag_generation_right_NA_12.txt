## 1. Problem statement

A radio show plays for <fact1>3 hours a day</fact1>. They split their show into talking segments, ad breaks, and songs. <fact2>Talking segments last 10 minutes each</fact2>, <fact3>ad breaks last 5 minutes each</fact3> and songs are played throughout the rest of the show. If the radio show includes <fact4>3 talking segments</fact4> and <fact5>5 ad breaks</fact5> in today's show, how long, in minutes, does the show play songs?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Radio show duration: 3 hours a day</fact1>
<fact2>Talking segment duration: 10 minutes each</fact2>
<fact3>Ad break duration: 5 minutes each</fact3>
<fact4>Number of talking segments: 3</fact4>
<fact5>Number of ad breaks: 5</fact5>

## 3. Steps

<step1> Convert the total show time from hours to minutes </step1>
<formula> hours × 60 </formula>
<calculation> 3 hours * 60 minutes = 180 minutes </calculation>
<var> total_show_time : 180 minutes </var>

<step2> Calculate the total time spent on talking segments </step2>
<formula> number of talking segments × time per talking segment </formula>
<calculation> 3 talking segments * 10 minutes per talking segment = 30 minutes each </calculation>
<var> talking_time : 30 minutes </var>

<step3> Calculate the total time spent on ad breaks </step3>
<formula> number of ad breaks × time per ad break </formula>
<calculation> 5 ad breaks * 5 minutes per ad break = 25 minutes </calculation>
<var> ad_time : 25 minutes </var>

<step4> Find the total time used for talking and ads </step4>
<formula> talking_time + ad_time </formula>
<calculation> 30 + 25 = 55 minutes </calculation>
<var> non_song_time : 55 minutes </var>

<step5> Calculate the remaining time for songs </step5>
<formula> total_show_time - non_song_time </formula>
<calculation> 180 – 55 = 125 minutes </calculation>
<var> song_time : 125 minutes </var>

## 4. Wrongsteps

<wrongstep> 0 </wrongstep>

## 5. Output

<output> 125 </output>
