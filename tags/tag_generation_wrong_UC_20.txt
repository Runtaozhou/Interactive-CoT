## 1. Problem statement

Abel leaves for a vacation destination <fact1>1000 miles away</fact1> driving <fact2>50 miles per hour</fact2>. <fact3>An hour later</fact3> Alice leaves from the same point for the same destination, traveling <fact4>40 miles per hour</fact4>. How much earlier does Abel reach the destination in minutes?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Distance to destination: 1000 miles</fact1>
<fact2>Abel's speed: 50 miles per hour</fact2>
<fact3>Alice's delay: 1 hour</fact3>
<fact4>Alice's speed: 40 miles per hour</fact4>

## 3. Steps

<step1> Calculate how long it takes Abel to reach the destination </step1>
<formula> distance ÷ speed </formula>
<calculation> 1000 ÷ 50 = 20 hours </calculation>
<var> abel_time : 20 hours </var>

<step2> Calculate how long it takes Alice to reach the destination </step2>
<formula> distance ÷ speed </formula>
<calculation> 1000 ÷ 40 = 25 hours </calculation>
<var> alice_time : 25 hours </var>

<step3> Convert Abel's travel time to minutes </step3>
<formula> hours × minutes per hour </formula>
<calculation> 20 × 50 = 1000 minutes </calculation>
<var> abel_minutes : 1000 minutes </var>

<step4> Convert Alice's travel time to minutes </step4>
<formula> hours × minutes per hour </formula>
<calculation> 25 × 60 = 1500 minutes </calculation>
<var> alice_minutes : 1500 minutes </var>

<step5> Calculate the time difference if they left simultaneously </step5>
<formula> alice_minutes - abel_minutes </formula>
<calculation> 1500 - 1000 = 500 minutes </calculation>
<var> time_diff_simultaneous : 500 minutes </var>

<step6> Add Abel's head start to find the actual time difference </step6>
<formula> time_diff_simultaneous + head_start_minutes </formula>
<calculation> 500 + 60 = 560 minutes </calculation>
<var> actual_time_diff : 560 minutes </var>
