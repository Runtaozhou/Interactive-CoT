## 1. Problem statement

<fact1>Hot dog buns come in packages of 8.</fact1> For the school picnic, <fact2>Mr. Gates bought 30 packages of hot dog buns.</fact2> He has <fact3>four classes</fact3> with <fact4>30 students in each class.</fact4> How many hot dog buns can each of Mr. Gates' students get?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Buns per package: 8</fact1>
<fact2>Packages bought: 30</fact2>
<fact3>Number of classes: 4</fact3>
<fact4>Students per class: 30</fact4>

## 3. Steps

<step1>Calculate the total number of hot dog buns Mr. Gates bought.</step1>
<formula>Packages × Buns per package</formula>
<calculation>30 × 8 = 240</calculation>
<var>total_buns: 240</var>

<step2>Calculate the total number of students in Mr. Gates' classes.</step2>
<formula>Number of classes × Students per class</formula>
<calculation>4 × 30 = 120</calculation>
<var>total_students: 120</var>

<step3>Calculate how many hot dog buns each student can get.</step3>
<formula>Total buns ÷ Total students</formula>
<calculation>240 ÷ 120 = 2</calculation>
<var>buns_per_student: 2</var>
