## 1. Problem statement

<fact1>Jackie can do 5 push-ups in 10 seconds.</fact1> How many push-ups can she do in one minute if she takes <fact2>two 8-second breaks</fact2>?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Push-ups: 5 in 10 seconds</fact1>
<fact2>Breaks: two 8-second breaks</fact2>

## 3. Steps

<step1> Calculate the total time for Jackie's push-up routine </step1>
<formula> minutes × seconds per minute </formula>
<calculation> 1 * 60 = 60 seconds </calculation>
<var> total_time : 60 seconds </var>

<step2> Calculate the total time spent on breaks </step2>
<formula> number of breaks × break duration </formula>
<calculation> 2 * 8 = 20 seconds </calculation>
<var> break_time : 20 seconds </var>

<step3> Calculate the time available for doing push-ups </step3>
<formula> total_time - break_time </formula>
<calculation> 60 - 20 = 40 seconds </calculation>
<var> push_up_time : 40 seconds </var>

<step4> Calculate the time needed for one push-up </step4>
<formula> time / number of push-ups </formula>
<calculation> 10 / 5 = 2 seconds </calculation>
<var> time_per_pushup : 2 seconds </var>

<step5> Calculate the total number of push-ups Jackie can do </step5>
<formula> push_up_time / time_per_pushup </formula>
<calculation> 40 / 2 = 20 push-ups </calculation>
<var> total_pushups : 20 push-ups </var>

## 4. Wrongsteps

<wrongstep> 2 </wrongstep>

## 5. Output

<output> 20 </output>
