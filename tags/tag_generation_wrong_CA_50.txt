## 1. Problem statement

Lidia has a collection of books. Her collection is <fact1>four times bigger than the collection which her friend Susan has</fact1>. In total <fact2>Susan and Lidia, both have 3000 books</fact2>. How many books does Susan have in her collection?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Lidia's collection: 4 times Susan's collection</fact1>
<fact2>Total books: 3000</fact2>

## 3. Steps

<step1> Define a variable for Susan's collection </step1>
<formula> x = Susan's collection </formula>
<calculation></calculation>
<var> Susan's books : x </var>

<step2> Express the total number of books in terms of x </step2>
<formula> 4*x + x = 3000 </formula>
<calculation> 4*x + x = 3000 </calculation>
<var> Total books : 3000 </var>

<step3> Simplify the equation </step3>
<formula> 5*x = 3000 </formula>
<calculation> 5*x = 3000 </calculation>
<var> Simplified equation : 5*x = 3000 </var>

<step4> Solve for x to find Susan's collection </step4>
<formula> x = 3000/5 </formula>
<calculation> x = 500 </calculation>
