## 1. Problem statement

<fact1>A gallon of whole milk that normally costs $3 is now sold at $2</fact1>. <fact2>A box of cereal was sold at a discount of $1</fact2>. How much will you save via discounts if you buy <fact3>3 gallons of whole milk</fact3> and <fact4>5 boxes of cereal</fact4>?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Milk normal price: $3, sale price: $2</fact1>
<fact2>Cereal discount: $1 per box</fact2>
<fact3>Milk quantity: 3 gallons</fact3>
<fact4>Cereal quantity: 5 boxes</fact4>

## 3. Steps

<step1>Calculate the total discount for the cereal boxes</step1>
<formula>discount per box × number of boxes</formula>
<calculation>$1/box × 5 boxes = $5</calculation>
<var>cereal_discount: $5</var>

<step2>Calculate the total savings from both milk and cereal</step2>
<formula>milk discount + cereal discount</formula>
<calculation>$3 + $5 = $8</calculation>
<var>total_savings: $8</var>

<wrongstep>1</wrongstep>

## 5. Output

<output>8</output>
