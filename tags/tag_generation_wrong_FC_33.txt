## 1. Problem statement

Nathan wants to line the inside of a box with velvet. The box has <fact1>two long sides that measure 8 inches by 6 inches</fact1>, <fact2>two short sides that measure 5 inches by six inches</fact2> and <fact3>a top and a bottom that each measure 40 square inches</fact3>. How many square inches of velvet does Nathan need?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Two long sides dimensions: 8 inches by 6 inches</fact1>
<fact2>Two short sides dimensions: 5 inches by 6 inches</fact2>
<fact3>Top and bottom area: 40 square inches each</fact3>

## 3. Steps

<step1> First find the perimeter of each long side by adding the lengths of all sides </step1>
<formula> (length + width) * 2 </formula>
<calculation> (8 inches + 6 inches) * 2 = 28 square inches </calculation>
<var> long_side_area : 28 square inches </var>

<step2> Then do the same thing for each short side </step2>
<formula> (length + width) * 2 </formula>
<calculation> (5 inches + 6 inches) * 2 = 22 square inches </calculation>
<var> short_side_area : 22 square inches </var>

<step3> Now add the area of one long side, one short side, and one top/bottom together </step3>
<formula> long_side_area + short_side_area + top_bottom_area </formula>
<calculation> 28 square inches + 22 square inches + 40 square inches = 90 square inches </calculation>
<var> one_set_area : 90 square inches </var>

<step4> Now double that quantity because there are two of each kind of side </step4>
<formula> one_set_area * 2 </formula>
<calculation> 90 square inches * 2 = 180 square inches </calculation>
<var> total_velvet_area : 180 square inches </var>

## 4. Wrongsteps

<wrongstep> 1 </wrongstep>

## 5. Output

<output> 180 </output>
