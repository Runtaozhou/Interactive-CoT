## 1. Problem statement

The school store had a sale on pencils. <fact1>Ten students bought pencils.</fact1> <fact2>The first two students bought 2 pencils each.</fact2> <fact3>The next six students bought three pencils each</fact3> and <fact4>the last two students only bought one pencil each.</fact4> How many pencils were sold?

## 2. Facts

<fact1>Total students: 10</fact1>
<fact2>First 2 students: 2 pencils each</fact2>
<fact3>Next 6 students: 3 pencils each</fact3>
<fact4>Last 2 students: 1 pencil each</fact4>

## 3. Steps

<step1> Calculate how many pencils the first two students bought </step1>
<formula> 2 students × 2 pencils </formula>
<calculation> 2 * 2 = 4 pencils </calculation>
<var> first_group_pencils : 4 pencils </var>

<step2> Calculate how many pencils the next six students bought </step2>

<var> second_group_pencils : 18 pencils </var>

<step3> Calculate how many pencils the last two students bought </step3>
<formula> 1 + 1 </formula>
<calculation> 1 + 1 = 2 pencils </calculation>
<var> third_group_pencils : 2 pencils </var>

<step4> Add all pencils plus the teacher's pencils to find the total </step4>
<formula> first_group_pencils + second_group_pencils + third_group_pencils + teacher_pencils </formula>
